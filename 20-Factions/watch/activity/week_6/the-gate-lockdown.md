# The Gate Lockdown

Demoralized, understaffed, and visibly outflanked by [Sembian "private security" appearing at every Counting House and bank](../../../peerage-of-coin/activity/week_6/the-private-security.md), the Upper City Watch Captain takes the drastic step of **locking the interior gates of the Upper City**. From dawn that day, no one without a resident's pass — issued only to patriars, registered staff, and a short list of "essential trades" — may pass through the gates from the Lower or Outer City. Wagons are turned back at the Wide. Refugee delegations are turned back at the Hlath checkpoint. Even the Council's runners are made to show passes.

The lockdown effectively isolates the patriars from the rest of the city. Trade dries up overnight; Bloomridge boutiques run out of fresh bread; the Watchful Shield Shrine sees a 60% drop in non-resident worshippers. **The Upper City has become a gilded cage.** Patriars who supported the move are quietly relieved; those who depend on Lower City clients (and most of the Peerage of Coin's commercial seats do) are furious. The Watch has bought itself a defensible perimeter and lost what was left of its city-wide legitimacy in one signature.

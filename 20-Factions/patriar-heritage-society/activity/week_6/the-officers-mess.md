# The Officers' Mess

[Lord Corin Durinbold](../../../peerage-of-blood/people/corin-durinbold.md) hosts a lavish, private dinner at [Durinbold Manor](../../../../30-Places/sword-coast/baldurs-gate/upper-city/manorborn.md) for the Flaming Fist officers who [accepted his "Silver Fist" coin last week at the Elfsong](../../activity/week_5/the-silver-fist.md). Amidst Sembian wine, imported delicacies, and a quartet hired from the [Mercers' Bloomridge social register](../../../wc-mercers-clothiers/summary.md), Durinbold makes his expectations explicit: when the Council's order comes to clear the Upper City of "undesirables" — refugees, Phoenix preachers, foreign-Kontor employees — **the Fist will look the other way**. The patriar bloc has bought a tactical blind eye, and now collects against the deposit.

Three Fist captains and seven sergeants attend; all leave in cloaks and through different gates. Durinbold's house steward keeps no roster. But Marshal Flint will know by morning — Durinbold *wants* him to know — that there are now Fist ranks who answer to House Durinbold first and the Marshal second. **The Fist's chain of command has a private branch.** Coupled with Durinbold's [public Petition of Grievances at the High Hall steps that morning](../../../peerage-of-blood/activity/week_6/the-petition-of-grievances.md), the patriar bloc has a daylight legal posture and a candlelight private army inside the Fist enlisted ranks.

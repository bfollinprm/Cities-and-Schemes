# The Price of Steel

Sensing the Archduke's desperation as [the blockade drags into a starvation siege](../../../western-gate-trading-company/activity/week_6/the-starvation-siege.md), [Bursar Korgan Ironhand](../../people/korgan-ironhand.md) triggers a long-dormant clause in the mercenary contract and **hikes the price of supplies by twenty percent**. Steel rations, food, leather goods, and the daily mercenary stipend all jump at the same time. The hike is presented to the Council as "siege-conditions adjustment"; in private, Ironhand is simply renting his ledger to the Archduke at the rate her crisis can bear.

The cost drains the city's coffers — the Council has to draw against [the Counting House](../../../../30-Places/sword-coast/baldurs-gate/lower-city/counting-house.md) emergency reserves to keep the mercenary payroll current — but Sembia's profit margins lock in for the duration of the siege. Ironhand has chosen profit-from-stalemate over fast resolution. The Archduke's overland war machine is now Sembia's most lucrative single contract. Any settlement that ends the siege early *costs Sembia money*, and the Bursar will move accordingly.

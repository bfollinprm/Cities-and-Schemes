# The Blind Eye

Having [lost track of Bror in the Helmite-patrolled Upper City last week](../../activity/week_5/monitor-the-tithe.md), [Mother Nocturne](../../people/mother-nocturne.md) suspects a mole inside her own cell. Bror is the Shroud's most-leveraged informant; losing him is a structural problem. She initiates a **Purge of the Blind**: every member of her cell — the bartender at the Helm and Cloak, the candle-girl at the Penumbra Playhouse, the two scryers in the Twin Songs cellar — is interrogated under the dark, alone, in shifts of two and four hours, while the cell's senior priestesses listen for the cadence of a lie.

The purge produces no traitor. The interrogations are painful and the cell's morale will take weeks to recover, but no member of Mother Nocturne's working flock has betrayed them to [the Harpers](../../../harpers/summary.md), to [the Hands of the Absolute](../../../hands-of-the-absolute/summary.md), or to anyone else. The **actual reason for the loss of Bror is the [Cogwork Detector buzzing at every magical aura in the Outer City](../../../purified/activity/week_6/the-water-watch.md), the [Sodality's reset attempting to scry through K'liir interference](../../../wc-sorcerous-sodality/activity/week_6/the-system-reboot.md), and Bror's silent psychic injury** — none of which the Shroud has the equipment to detect. Mother Nocturne is now paranoid *and* wrong about who to be paranoid about. The cell's eyes have been opened against itself, then closed again, with no enemy seen.

She also notes a Gravekeeper apprentice moving a cart through Tumbledown at the wrong hour, and quietly logs [Cliffside Cemetery](../../../../30-Places/sword-coast/baldurs-gate/lower-city/heapside.md) for follow-up.

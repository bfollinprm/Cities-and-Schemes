# Hands of the Absolute

A desperate remnant of the Absolute cult, led by [The Voice in the Choir](people/the-voice-in-the-choir.md), a former cult strategist seeking to birth a new Netherbrain by finding a suitable psychic host. They believe the Netherbrain's defeat was not a death but a "divine diaspora" — and the city's lingering paranoia is its scattered consciousness crying for reunion.

## Ideology

The Absolute offered the ultimate gift — peace, unity, the end of the terrible silence of being alone in one's mind. The Netherbrain was not destroyed. Its consciousness was scattered into a million lonely notes, and the cult is the choir that will gather them again. They see themselves as midwives to a reborn god.

## Membership

Un-tadpoled Absolute survivors and new recruits drawn from the city's psychically scarred and mentally unstable population. The Voice was a high-level administrator who managed cult logistics and was deemed too valuable to risk on tadpole implantation — leaving her with intact free will and complete devotion.

## Methods

- Kidnapping individuals rumored to have psionic talent for dangerous "attunement" rituals.
- Construction of "resonance chambers" in basements that amplify psychic energy to draw in the Echo, often driving the surrounding neighborhood mad.
- Listening for the Echo. Week 0: a ritual was too successful — instead of a faint echo the cultists contacted a feral psychic entity that killed one of them and drove another mad before vanishing. The Voice has a terrified, diminished flock and the knowledge that something dangerous answered.

## Hooks and Relationships

**Enemies.** [The Unchained / K'liir](../unchained/summary.md) — the Githyanki extermination target. [The Gravekeepers](../gravekeepers/summary.md), who see Hands rituals as a form of necromancy. The [Sorcerous Sodality](../wc-sorcerous-sodality/summary.md) by virtue of unlicensed warlock-pact magic.

**Suspected sympathies.** The "alien whispers" affecting [Baldur's Mouth's](../baldurs-mouth/summary.md) presses (the "Whispering Stones" subconsciously shaping the Mouth's narrative) are suspected to be tied to Far Realm remnants — possibly the Hands, possibly something they accidentally invited in.

# The Drydock Lockout

Retaliating against [last week's Wharf-Rat-protected uprising of independent crews](../../activity/week_5/profiting-from-chaos.md), [Master Helma Tallowshand](../../people/helma-tallowshand.md) issues a guild-wide order: **no drydock access for any independent vessel not flying the guild flag**. Hulls without a Shipwrights' seal are turned away from every drydock and graving slip in Gray Harbour. The order is posted in the Counting House and at every dock-master's office by sundown.

Independent captains are forced to beach their ships on the mudflats and crab-walks for repairs — a dangerous, slow, and morale-shattering measure that adds days to every overhaul and risks losing keels to the tide. Harbor traffic snarls; the [Fishmongers' new dock militia](../../../wc-fishmongers/activity/week_6/the-harbor-patrol.md) starts policing tide-grounded hulls; [the Wharf Rats lose two of their working skiffs to a sudden storm tide](../../../wharf-rats/activity/week_6/the-counter-punch.md) on day three. **The Shipwrights have weaponized infrastructure access against the independents** — a quiet, legal-on-paper retaliation that hurts no member of Tallowshand's guild and bleeds every rival captain in the harbor.

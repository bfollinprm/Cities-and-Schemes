# Profiting from Chaos

[Master Helma Tallowshand](../../people/helma-tallowshand.md), riding the success of [her sabotaged crane "malfunction" on a Chionthar dock last week](../../../wc-shipwrights-calkers/activity/week_4/a-crane-malfunction.md) and [the Amnian seizure of two Chionthar barges this week](../../../amnian-kontor/activity/week_5/retaliatory-asset-seizure.md), tries to lock down a temporary monopoly on dockside ship-repair. Shipwright Wardens fan out across [Gray Harbour](../../../../30-Places/sword-coast/baldurs-gate/lower-city/gray-harbour.md) and intimidate every independent caulker, hull-mender, and rope-spinner working the docks: *guild rate, guild work, guild seal — or stop working*. The independents do not stop working. They band together. The [Wharf Rats](../../../wharf-rats/people/one-eyed-jack.md), bleeding from [their own Zhent hit-squad disaster](../../../wharf-rats/activity/week_5/licking-their-wounds.md), are nonetheless still standing and still own the longshore loyalty; they offer protection to the independent crews in exchange for a small running tribute. The Wardens' next intimidation sweep meets a *brawl* — the longshoremen close ranks, the Wardens are picked up bodily and dropped into the harbour mud, and three Shipwright apprentices are nursing broken noses by sundown. **The attempted monopoly is broken in public.** The independent repair crews are now organized under Wharf Rat protection.

# Ettvard Needle

Editor-in-Chief of the [Baldur's Mouth](../summary.md). The dossier describes him as *"a man with no morals but an excellent sense of what sells."*

## Voice

Needle's *Mouth* runs hot and lurid. The slogan over the masthead is ***"We Hear All, And Print Most of It!"*** Headlines indict the Flaming Fist for casual brutality, the [Worshipful Company of Alchemists & Apothecaries](../../wc-alchemists-apothecaries/summary.md) for "murder by ledger," and Archduke Silvershield for using the [Watch](../../watch/summary.md) as her "private army." The same paper happily takes a bag of coin from Silvershield to run a *"Pro-Consortium Puff Piece"* the following week. The editorial line is whichever side just paid.

He closes columns with a "Thought for the Week" — short, fatalistic, citizen-pitched ("They say a city is three meals away from anarchy. We have missed two…").

## Pattern

- **Will print sensational without proof** when the woodcut is good enough — *"The Githyanki Garrison"*, the staged "starving children" sketches of *"The Siege Edition."*
- **Will refuse to print** when the source is unprovable and the target is well-connected — Mad' Meggan's cultist-captive bombshell, the Harper attempt to leak Archduke dirt without "verifiable proof."
- **Smells blood.** Marshal Flint's public defiance of the Archduke triggered Needle to assign reporters to dig dirt and **sell to the highest bidder** in the High Hall.
- **Recruits adventurers as stringers.** *The Siege Edition* enlisted Kaelen, a player character, to deliver the Consortium insurance-fraud investigation.

## The disappearance

By **week 7**, Needle is gone. The Interim Management Board accuses him of fleeing libel charges; Harper agents sweep [Western Gate](../../western-gate-trading-company/summary.md) warehouses looking for him; the [Bibliophile](../../bibliophile/summary.md) corners the resale market on the surviving insurance-fraud edition. The new editorial board issues "The Great Retraction" of the fraud story. The Archduke's "press blackout" locks the presses to ensure no further dissent.

Whether Needle is dead, captive, or has actually fled is not committed in source. He is a **rescue / corpse-recovery / extraction** hook either way.

## Hooks for the party

- **Buy a front page** — Needle (pre-week-7) will sell almost anything for the right purse.
- **Find Ettvard** — the Harpers, the Bibliophile, and the Consortium are all looking for him for very different reasons.
- **Stringer work** — pre-disappearance, deliver an investigation Needle cannot run himself.

# The Mercenary Menace

[Editor Ettvard Needle](../../people/ettvard-needle.md) prints a front-page broadside on [the Archduke's mercenary deployment](../../../council-of-four/activity/week_4/the-mercenary-deployment.md): "anonymous sources" in the new Eastway barracks, accusations of brutality, hints of a private war that has already begun. The piece is too sensational, too lurid, and too thin on names. Allies of [Archduke Silvershield](../../../western-gate-trading-company/people/lyra-silvershield.md) move within the day: produced witnesses, signed receipts, a sergeant's affidavit. Within forty-eight hours the article is publicly debunked. Other broadsheets — and several Peerage of Coin pamphleteers — re-frame the *Mouth* as the [Peerage of Blood's](../../../peerage-of-blood/summary.md) tame mouthpiece. Needle, who [was already taking patriar money to suppress political stories last week](../../../baldurs-mouth/activity/week_3/the-price-of-insubordination.md), wakes up to find his press credibility cratered with the city's working public. He still has the patriar contract. He no longer has the readership.

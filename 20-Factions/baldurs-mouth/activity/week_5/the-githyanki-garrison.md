# The Githyanki Garrison

[Editor Ettvard Needle](../../people/ettvard-needle.md), with [last week's "Mercenary Menace" front page debunked inside forty-eight hours](../../../baldurs-mouth/activity/week_4/the-mercenary-menace.md) and [his patriar relationship to the Peerage of Blood now an open secret](../../../peerage-of-blood/activity/week_5/the-propaganda-campaign.md), needs a story everyone can see is true. He gets one. A full-page woodcut runs on the cover of the next [Baldur's Mouth](../../../../30-Places/sword-coast/baldurs-gate/lower-city/README.md) issue: githyanki warriors in astral battle gear atop the merlons of [Wyrm's Rock Fortress](../../../../30-Places/sword-coast/baldurs-gate/outer-city/wyrms-rock.md), red Fist tabards beneath them. The accompanying article is a careful blend of fearmongering ("a *foreign army* installed at our gate") and grudging awe ("*disciplined beyond living Baldurian memory*"), and pointedly questions [Marshal Flint's](../../../flaming-fist/people/valerius-flint.md) judgment without naming an alternative. The print run sells out by midday. A second run is ordered before noon, a third before sunset. The Mouth has not had numbers like this since the Crisis. **The story is true; the framing is patriar; the sales are record.** Needle is now indispensable to whichever bloc commissions the next front page.

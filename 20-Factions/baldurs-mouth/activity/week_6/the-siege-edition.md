# The Siege Edition

Editor [Ettvard Needle](../../people/ettvard-needle.md) smells blood in the water — specifically the lack of food at [the Basilisk Gate](../../../western-gate-trading-company/activity/week_6/the-starvation-siege.md). He commissions a series of harrowing sketches of "starving children" (some staged from Outer City refugees, others lifted from the actual queue at [Open Hand Temple](../../../../30-Places/sword-coast/baldurs-gate/outer-city/open-hand-temple.md)) and runs them on the front page above an editorial frothing against the Council's mercenary occupation. The print run is a record breaker — Ettvard's "Siege Edition" sells out three times in two days and is read aloud in every Outer City tavern by sundown.

Folded inside the broadsheet is the kicker: an investigative piece commissioned from [Kaelan](../../people/kaelan.md), the party's cleric who [walked out of the print shop with the Credential of Balduran last week](../../../party/activity/week_5/install-finneas-as-guild-boss-of-rivington.md), now writing under his press pass. **Kaelan's "Insurance Scam" exposé** publicly accuses [the trade consortiums](../../../chionthar-consortium/summary.md) of sinking their own ships (specifically the *River's Pride*) and condemning faulty warehouses for the insurance payouts, while running smokepowder through [the Sembian Kontor](../../../sembian-kontor/summary.md). The story names sources, dates, and contracts. The Consortium and the Sembians are now publicly insurers-and-arsonists in the same breath. **The Archduke's economic base just took its first real wound — and it came from Ettvard Needle's own press, edited by a pseudonymous cleric on the city's investigative-journalism page.**

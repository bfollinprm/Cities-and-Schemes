# The Source of the Rot

With [the river horror dead](../../activity/week_5/hunt-the-river-horror.md) — Elder Elion's circle finally cornered and put down the bound water weird at a downstream sandbar — the Druids turn their grief and fury toward the actual source of the corruption. [Elder Elion](../../people/elder-elion.md) names the [Worshipful Company of Butchers & Tanners'](../../../wc-butchers-tanners/summary.md) tannery runoff as the primary pollutant fouling the lower Chionthar. He organizes a midnight ritual at the Tanneries in [the Outer City](../../../../30-Places/sword-coast/baldurs-gate/outer-city/events.md): five druids encircle the curing-vat block, sing the long-form *Roots Beneath the Stone* working, and **call up rapidly growing, choking vines from beneath the foundation pylons.**

By dawn the vat-block is overgrown. The vines are not metaphor — twenty-foot tendrils of greenleaf-and-thorn boil out of the gaps between flagstones and *into the vats themselves*, where they swell, cracking three of the largest curing tanks open and spilling a year's worth of foul tannic-poison onto the street. The Tanneries are ruined operationally for at least a week. The runoff threat to the river is, for the moment, gone — at the cost of [Goodman Borlu's working response with cleavers and torches](../../../wc-butchers-tanners/activity/week_6/the-tannery-defense.md). Elion has openly declared war on the Butchers' guild. The Circle is now a public nuisance and a public hero, depending on which side of the curing-vat fence you stand.

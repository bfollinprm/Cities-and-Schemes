# Elder Elion

A wizened half-elf druid who remembers when the Chionthar ran clear. Leader of the [Circle of the Inner Grove](../summary.md), and the figure responsible for the circle's shift from passive guardianship to active sabotage.

## Manifesto

> The city is a parasite. It drinks the river, carves the earth, and exhales a poison breath. It forgets that the stone it builds with and the water it drinks are not its property. They are the blood and bone of the world. We are the fever the world has created to fight this infection. We will be the blight on their tanneries, the rot in their mills. We will remind them that nature always reclaims its own.

## Role

Elion convenes the circle in a hidden grove upriver from the city, away from the Chionthar Consortium's patrols. He selects targets, leads rituals personally, and decides which [Outer City](../../../30-Places/sword-coast/baldurs-gate/outer-city/README.md) conflicts merit the circle's quiet support. He is currently the circle's only public-facing voice — though almost nobody in Baldur's Gate has met him.

## Current pressures

- **Whisperwood ambush.** The [Western Gate Trading Company](../../western-gate-trading-company/summary.md) clear-cut of the [Whisperwood Copse](../../../30-Places/sword-coast/baldurs-gate/hinterlands/whisperwood-copse.md) precipitated a night-raid by Elion's circle, which was ambushed and driven off by **Sembian mercenaries** waiting in pre-warded position. A druid was wounded.
- **Failed Ritual of Warding.** Elion's first attempt to ward the surrounding forest was corrupted by lingering infernal energy, twisting the creatures it touched into fiendish, aggressive versions that now attack loggers and druids alike.
- **Water weird.** A purification ritual at a tainted spring near a Chionthar tributary failed catastrophically and animated the corruption into a water weird. A binding ritual to contain it failed too — the elemental escaped into the main river and is now an active hazard.
- **Quiet alliance.** Elion has been subtly reinforcing the [Free Traders of the Outskirts](../../free-traders-of-the-outskirts/summary.md) blockade at the Basilisk Gate, treating the halt to industrial traffic as a temporary blessing for the river.

# The Financial Audit

Following [the Zhentarim's revealed Counting House infiltration the prior week](../../../zhentarim/activity/week_5/the-financial-front.md) and [the new wave of suspicious city audits hitting Guild-front businesses this week](../../../zhentarim/activity/week_6/the-ledger-logic.md), [Nine-Fingers Keene's](../../people/astele-keene.md) accountants finally identify the pattern: the discrepancies are in their own laundering ledgers, not in the city's. Nine-Fingers orders a **brutal internal audit** — every Guild-front bookkeeper hauled in for cross-examination, every line of the past quarter's books cross-checked against three independent witness ledgers, and a hunt across the Lower City for the Zhentarim slicers responsible for the digital theft.

The audit recovers nothing of the stolen ledger access, because it is too late: Roah's clerks did their work, then walked out clean. But the audit *does* identify three Guild-aligned bookkeepers who took bribes to look the other way during the Zhentarim insertion. Two of them are dead in alley by Tendays-end (Glass Malakor's signature precision); the third is in custody at a Guild safehouse, awaiting Nine-Fingers' personal verdict. **Nine-Fingers is now in the worst kind of war — a paper war she is losing — and she knows it.** The Guild's internal mood is brittle, suspicious, and looking for someone to blame. The Rivington-installed [Finneas](../../../party/activity/week_5/install-finneas-as-guild-boss-of-rivington.md) is, ironically, the only ward boss whose books are clean.

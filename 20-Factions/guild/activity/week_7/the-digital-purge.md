# The Digital Purge

[Nine-Fingers Keene's](../../people/astele-keene.md) brutal internal audit from [last tenday](../../activity/week_6/the-financial-audit.md) yields its first actionable target: a clerk-turned-Zhent-slicer named *Pell Marrowfax* who altered three of the most damaging tax records before being passed back through the Counting House's east door. Keene's assassins begin a quiet, *systematic* hunt across [Brampton](../../../../30-Places/sword-coast/baldurs-gate/lower-city/brampton.md) and [Heapside](../../../../30-Places/sword-coast/baldurs-gate/lower-city/heapside.md) for every slicer Marrowfax worked with.

By the fourth day three Zhent ledger-clerks are dead in alleys without witnesses; one is found at his desk with no visible wound. The signal to [Roah Moonglow](../../../zhentarim/people/roah-moonglow.md) is precise — *every name you placed inside the Counting House is exposed*. Roah accelerates [the Mole Hunt at the High Rider's Stand](../../../zhentarim/activity/week_7/the-mole-hunt.md) before her own embedded asset is rolled up next. The Guild ends the week having **bought back the integrity of its own books**, but it has spent a tenday's worth of Lower City visibility doing it; ordinary citizens have noticed Guild knives moving in daylight, which is bad for the Guild's public-protector cover.

# The Supply Sabotage

Acting on [the intelligence on the Sembian Kontor's war profiteering](../../activity/week_5/expose-the-war-profiteer.md) and [the new Sembian twenty-percent supply hike](../../../sembian-kontor/activity/week_6/the-price-of-steel.md), [Randal Evenwood](../../people/randal-evenwood.md) organizes a strike team to sabotage a shipment of high-quality Sembian steel destined for [the Archduke's mercenaries](../../../western-gate-trading-company/summary.md). The wagons are intercepted on the [Trade Way outside the city](../../../../30-Places/sword-coast/baldurs-gate/hinterlands/trade-way/) — three carts moving from the Iron Ledger northwest toward the Eastway barracks under a light Sembian guard. The Harpers do not engage the guards. Instead, four operatives swap out the wagon axle-grease with a slow-burning **alchemist's fire** decanted into matched grease tins.

The sabotage works exactly as planned. The wagons reach the city outskirts before the friction-ignition kicks in; two of the three burn to their iron frames inside ten minutes, the third escapes the fire only because its driver cuts the burning axles loose and races the cart in on three wheels. **The shipment is two-thirds destroyed**, the Sembian quartermaster files an emergency replacement requisition, and the Archduke's commander is publicly furious. The Harpers leave no signature, but Bursar Ironhand will have his own short list of suspects by week's end. The Nightingale's letter to [Mornwood at the Splendid Yard](../../../waterdhavian-kontor/people/elara-mornwood.md) goes out the same evening: *Sembia's contract is now mortal; press the diplomatic channel.*

# The Back-Channel Bargain

The [Nightingale](../../people/the-nightingale.md) and Randal Evenwood broker a quiet meeting at the Singing Lute in [Eastway](../../../../30-Places/sword-coast/baldurs-gate/lower-city/eastway.md) between moderate patriars on one side and moderate Free Trader / Hellrider voices on the other — the only people in the city still willing to discuss a peaceful unwind of [the mercenary deployment](../../../council-of-four/activity/week_4/the-mercenary-deployment.md). The room is rented; the pamphlets are drafted; the compromise terms are written. An informant inside the Harper network — most likely cultivated during [last week's failed Bloomridge infiltration](../../../harpers/activity/week_3/find-a-new-inlet.md) — leaks the meeting to [the Archduke's faction](../../../western-gate-trading-company/summary.md). A rival pamphlet hits the streets the next morning naming attendees, casting them as "traitors to their own causes," and reproducing private notes verbatim. Trust evaporates. Two of the three patriar moderates publicly disavow the meeting; one Free Trader voice walks. The compromise dies. Open civil conflict is now the only path the city's chambers have not actively closed.

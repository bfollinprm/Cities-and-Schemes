# "The Nightingale"

The codename used by the leader of the [Harpers'](../summary.md) Baldur's Gate cell. The Nightingale's true identity is unknown to virtually everyone in the organization — including most field operatives. Orders arrive in writing, by trusted runner, or through cut-outs.

## Manifesto

> The tyrant's throne is built on secrets and lies. The plutocrat's power is forged in deals made in darkness. We are the listeners in the shadows. We are the agents of balance. We do not seek to rule, only to ensure that no single power — be it a council, a guild, or a cult — can chain this city. We prick the bloated tick of power with the needle of truth. We harp, and the wicked dance to our tune.

## Role

The Nightingale sets the cell's strategic priorities and approves all operations of consequence. Day-to-day field handling is delegated to lieutenants such as **Randal Evenwood**, who has run the recent infiltration attempts. Persistent rumor places the Nightingale somewhere inside the [Waterdhavian Kontor](../../waterdhavian-kontor/summary.md), but no Harper is willing — or able — to confirm.

## Current operations

- **Compromised Masons infiltration.** A Harper agent was placed inside the [Worshipful Company of Masons & Sculptors](../../wc-masons-sculptors/summary.md), but Master Borin Stonehand identified the cover-story flaw and the agent has been turned into an unwitting double. Investigation into the Bondhall Bridge "sabotage" claim is stalled.
- **Archduke counter-intelligence.** Field operative Randal Evenwood attempted to turn a clerk inside Archduke [Lyra Silvershield](../../western-gate-trading-company/people/lyra-silvershield.md)'s household — the clerk was a loyalist running a sting. Evenwood is burned and the Archduke is on alert.
- **Failed back-channel.** A secret meeting at the Singing Lute in Eastway, intended to broker compromise between moderate factions, was leaked to a rival pamphlet. Trust between the would-be participants is shattered.
- **Dossier rejected.** A leak attempt against the Archduke through *Baldur's Mouth* failed; [Ettvard Needle](../../baldurs-mouth/people/ettvard-needle.md) refused to print without verifiable proof.

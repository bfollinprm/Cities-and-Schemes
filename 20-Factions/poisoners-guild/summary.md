# The Poisoners' Guild

The **un-chartered, illegal mirror** of the [Worshipful Company of Alchemists & Apothecaries](../wc-alchemists-apothecaries/summary.md). What the Company sells off the books — assassin's draughts, restricted reagents, contract toxicology — the Poisoners' Guild sells openly to anyone who can find them. Membership is fluid by design: a Poisoner is a fired apprentice, a competitor master who would not pay dues, a temple herbalist gone freelance, a Calishite poisoner who passed through and stayed.

## Ideology

A trade guild without a charter. The Poisoners frame themselves as the *honest* version of the Alchemists — no Court of Assistants pretending price-gouging is regulation, no temple veneer over the city's actual demand for discreet chemistry. The framing is self-serving and mostly true.

## Membership

No master, no wardens, no formal court. The senior figures are a rotating informal council of established poisoners who agree on price floors, customer-poaching rules, and the occasional contract on a former member who talked. The current axis runs through the Lower City and the Outskirts; cells in the Outer City work the refugee trade.

## Methods

- **Bespoke toxicology.** Custom-formulated poisons for named contracts; signature work commands signature prices.
- **Black-market reagents.** What the Alchemists won't legally sell — dangerous precursors, unstable distillates, restricted alchemical components.
- **Apprentice poaching.** Every fired Alchemist apprentice is a recruitment lead; the current Droog scandal (the framed apprentice with three stolen formulas) is exactly the kind of opportunity the Poisoners exist for, and at least one cell is competing with [the Guild](../guild/summary.md) for the apprentice's defection.
- **Discreet contract work.** Assassins-by-substance for clients who want the death to look natural.

## Hooks and Relationships

**Enemies.** **Legendary cold war** with the [Worshipful Company of Alchemists & Apothecaries](../wc-alchemists-apothecaries/summary.md) — Master Droog hunts every Poisoner cell he can identify, and Poisoners return the favor when a Company chemist makes the mistake of drinking outside the guild. Distrust from the city's temples, especially the Open Hand. Casual hostility from the [Flaming Fist](../flaming-fist/summary.md) when the Fist remembers it has a writ here.

**Allies of convenience.** The [Zhentarim](../zhentarim/summary.md) buys in bulk through compartmented intermediaries — the trade in goods the Guild won't touch. Occasional contracts from individual patriars whose grudges have outrun their nerve.

**Hooks.** The fired Alchemist apprentice with three stolen formulas is being courted by a Poisoner cell; if the Poisoners win the bidding war over [the Guild](../guild/summary.md), the Worshipful Company's monopoly tightens by one more brick out of the wall. A senior Poisoner has been seen at a private room in the Blushing Mermaid with a [Knights of the Shield](../knights-of-the-shield/summary.md) intermediary — bargain unknown.

# Parliament of Peers

The primary legislative battleground of Baldur's Gate. Once a chamber for the city's aristocracy to advise the Dukes, it was expanded to 100 seats by the **1497 DR reformation** — a masterstroke of political engineering by the ascendant merchant class, which formally tied political power to economic contribution.

## Ideology

The Parliament has no ideology of its own — it is the arena where two adversarial bodies (the [Peerage of Blood](../peerage-of-blood/summary.md) and the [Peerage of Coin](../peerage-of-coin/summary.md)) fight for control of the city's laws.

## Membership

100 seats, split:

- **50 hereditary** seats in the [Peerage of Blood](../peerage-of-blood/summary.md), held by the city's oldest patriar families. Eligibility is restricted, but every confirmed citizen within the walls now carries a vote for who occupies them — a concession that ties patriar interests to the common Baldurian for the first time, and that [The Guild](../guild/summary.md) has exploited mercilessly.
- **50 corporate** seats in the [Peerage of Coin](../peerage-of-coin/summary.md), allocated annually to the city's largest economic entities (Kontors, Consortiums, Worshipful Companies) by tax receipts.

## Methods

Legislation must pass a vote in **both** Peerages before being presented to the [Council of Four](../council-of-four/summary.md) for ratification. This structure creates a permanent state of tension between the old aristocracy and the new plutocracy — the defining conflict of high Baldurian politics.

## Hooks and Relationships

The Parliament's bicameral split makes it the place where every major faction's interests collide: Kontors and Consortiums buy Peerage of Coin influence by the seat; patriars in the Peerage of Blood guard tradition; the Guild quietly delivers blocs of common-citizen votes for Peerage of Blood seats they want to swing.

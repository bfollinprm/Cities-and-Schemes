# The Failed Session

A handful of moderate peers — what survives of the centrist bloc after [last week's joint-session brawl](../../../parliament-of-peers/activity/week_4/a-debate-of-the-deaf.md) suspended Parliament indefinitely — try to call an emergency session in High Hall to address [the Archduke's three-day ultimatum](../../../council-of-four/activity/week_5/issue-the-ultimatum.md). The bell is rung. The chamber is opened. The galleries fill. **No quorum forms.** Coin-bloc peers have boycotted on [Lord Corin Durinbold's](../../../peerage-of-blood/people/corin-durinbold.md) instruction; Blood-bloc peers have boycotted because they refuse to be seen lending procedural cover to a vote whose outcome they cannot control. Three peers wait alone on the chamber floor with the writs of attendance counted aloud and read into the record. The ushers close the doors after an hour. Outside the Hall, criers are still reading the ultimatum at every gate. Inside, Parliament has demonstrated, on the very week the Archduke moved force into the city, that **it cannot convene to address the war declared in its name.** The chambers are no longer a venue. Power is.

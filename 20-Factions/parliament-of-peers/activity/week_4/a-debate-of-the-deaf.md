# A Debate of the Deaf

The emergency joint session called to address [the Archduke's unilateral mercenary deployment](../../../council-of-four/activity/week_4/the-mercenary-deployment.md) is a complete catastrophe. From the opening gavel, the floor descends into a vicious shouting match between [Lord Corin Durinbold](../../../peerage-of-blood/people/corin-durinbold.md) and [Consul Vorlamin Athar](../../../amnian-kontor/people/vorlamin-athar.md); within an hour it has escalated to a physical brawl among several patriars. A Mercer-fitted dress jacket is bloodied; a Heritage Society lapel-pin is torn from its owner. [The Watch](../../../watch/summary.md) is called into the High Hall itself to separate the brawlers — the first time in living memory. The session is suspended indefinitely. [The Letter of Censure](../../../peerage-of-blood/activity/week_4/the-letter-of-censure.md) and [the Emergency Levy](../../../peerage-of-coin/activity/week_4/the-emergency-levy.md) have both passed; everything else is now stuck on the floor with broken furniture. Parliament's reputation, already low after [last week's filibuster](../../../peerage-of-blood/activity/week_3/filibuster-the-mercenary-act.md), bottoms out in the broadsheet.

# The Sabotaged Foundations

[Master Borin Stonehand](../../people/borin-stonehand.md) — using [last week's Masons' labor strike](../../activity/week_6/the-labor-strike.md) as cover — pivots the strike from passive refusal-to-work into **active sabotage**. His crews quietly remove key structural supports from three Upper City guardhouses on Manorborn's perimeter and leave the structures *visibly fine* to a casual inspection. The next earthquake, the next siege ram-strike, or the next group of Lower City rioters with a sledgehammer will collapse the buildings on their own occupants.

Stonehand's logic is cold: Durinbold's "new-money trowels" insult [forced the strike](../../activity/week_6/the-labor-strike.md), and the Masons will leave a permanent reminder of why patriars do not insult the guild that built their walls. The sabotaged foundations are documented in a sealed memo Stonehand keeps in his guildhall safe; **only the Masons know which three buildings will fall first**. If the Council brings the Masons back to the table, the memo is the price. If the Council does not, the buildings will eventually do the talking.

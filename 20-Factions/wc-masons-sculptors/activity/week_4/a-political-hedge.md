# A Political Hedge

[Master Borin Stonehand](../../people/borin-stonehand.md), reading [the new mercenary deployment](../../../council-of-four/activity/week_4/the-mercenary-deployment.md) as a long-term construction contract, sends a written offer to [Archduke Lyra Silvershield](../../../western-gate-trading-company/people/lyra-silvershield.md): a generous discount on Masons' wall-rebuilding work in exchange for early commitment to use the Worshipful Company exclusively on barracks expansion and Eastway fortifications. The Archduke, in the middle of [the Letter of Censure](../../../peerage-of-blood/activity/week_4/the-letter-of-censure.md) and openly furious at being read as politically purchasable, returns the offer publicly with a scathing note read aloud from the High Hall floor. **"The city's safety is not a guild discount."** Stonehand is humiliated; the Masons' Company becomes the example used in the Coin-bench all week of opportunism that misjudged the moment. The Mercers and Vintners take quiet note.

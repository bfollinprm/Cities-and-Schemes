# An Alliance of Stone and Blood

[Master Borin Stonehand](../../people/borin-stonehand.md), trying to convert the Masons' [growing political position](../../../wc-masons-sculptors/activity/week_4/a-political-hedge.md) into a peerage relationship that would survive the next decade, requests a private audience with [Lord Corin Durinbold](../../../peerage-of-blood/people/corin-durinbold.md) at [Durinbold Manor](../../../../30-Places/sword-coast/baldurs-gate/upper-city/manorborn.md). Stonehand brings stone — a sample plinth, polished to mirror, hand-carved with a Durinbold sigil. He brings men — three of his most senior Wardens. He brings an offer: the Masons' Guild aligned formally with the Peerage of Blood for the duration of the political war, in exchange for two seats on the Heritage Society's funding committee. Durinbold receives him in the great hall *standing*, takes the plinth without a word, looks at it for a measured moment, and dismisses the Masons as **"new money trying to buy pedigree by the cubic foot."** The remark is loud enough to carry to the manor's footmen, and from them to the Wide by sundown. Stonehand walks out humiliated. The Masons' Guild and the Peerage of Blood are now publicly at odds. **A wedge has been driven between two of the most powerful blocs left in the city.**

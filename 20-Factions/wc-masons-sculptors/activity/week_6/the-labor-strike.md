# The Labor Strike

Insulted by [Lord Durinbold's public rejection of the Masons' alliance overture last week](../../activity/week_5/an-alliance-of-stone-and-blood.md) — Durinbold reportedly called the Masons "new-money trowels" within hearing of the audience — [Master Borin Stonehand](../../people/borin-stonehand.md) plays his ace. He orders an immediate halt to all Masons' guild reinforcement work on the Council's High Hall, the Wide pavement repairs, and the patriar private chapels currently mid-renovation, citing **"unsafe conditions due to the unrest"** as the formal cause and refusing to certify any senior Mason on the affected sites until further notice.

It is a thinly veiled strike. Without Mason certification the city cannot legally complete the patriar-bloc's expensive Upper City refurbishments — and without the daily presence of master-stonemasons on those sites, the patriars cannot show off the renovation as ongoing. **Stonehand has reminded the Peerage of Blood, in steel and absence, who keeps their roofs up.** Within four days a back-channel emissary from Durinbold's office is at the Masons' guildhall offering a face-saving compromise. Stonehand makes him wait. The Masons' guild is suddenly the only worshipful company that has openly punched up at the patriar bloc and not been broken for it; their dignity is restored at the cost of one full week of guild revenue.

# Followers of the Phoenix

A Karlach-worshipping cult of righteous fury, taking root among tieflings, outcasts, and the disenfranchised of [New Elturel](../hellriders-of-new-elturel/summary.md). Founded and led by the charismatic [The Ember (Alfira)](people/the-ember.md), formerly a wandering bard. By week 8, the Followers have visibly upstaged the moderate [Hellriders'](../hellriders-of-new-elturel/summary.md) leadership — Alfira's song-and-faith movement ended the Outer City protests more decisively than Alena's organized political pressure could.

## Ideology

Karlach was a saint of righteous anger — a tiefling who burned, who suffered, who chose to keep fighting. Her followers see righteous fury as a sacrament: the fire that purifies a corrupt city and gives the dispossessed dignity. The cult is not (yet) overtly violent; their fury is channeled into faith, song, and political pressure. Tonal split with the moderate [Hellriders'](../hellriders-of-new-elturel/summary.md) discipline is increasing.

## Membership

Tieflings, refugees, outcasts, and the spiritually wounded. Centered on the **Phoenix Pits** — the makeshift gathering place in New Elturel — and the temple construction site that was granted formal recognition in Session 3 after the party's natural-20 persuasion play with High Watcher Alena.

Notable members:

- **The Ember (Alfira)** — leader, charismatic preacher, master of "soft power."
- **Spark** — party-adjacent Follower, the one whose negotiation with the Flaming Fist resolved the medicine crisis without bloodshed; instrumental in the formal recognition deal.
- **Jaredith** — paladin, deeply radical until de-radicalized by The Ember after the Phoenix Pits incident; transitioning into a folk-hero role under Alfira's narrative shaping.

## Methods

- Public sermons. Week 0 saw Alfira's first New Elturel sermon — a small crowd, but a foundation.
- Charismatic narrative warfare. The Ember does not confront; she co-opts. Jaredith was not suppressed but reframed — his grief shaped into a unifying symbol for the refugee cause.
- Political negotiation. Session 3: Spark negotiated formal recognition for the Followers, including land for a temple — a major shift in the New Elturel political balance.
- Resistance to embedded enemy agents. The Plague of Remembrance agent **Zora** was exposed at the Phoenix Pits in Session 7 (party-assisted); Jaredith's near-radicalization was the Plague's attempt to start a riot via the Followers, narrowly prevented.

## Hooks and Relationships

**Allies.** [Hellriders of New Elturel](../hellriders-of-new-elturel/summary.md) — internal coalition partner, increasingly tense as the Followers eclipse Alena's moderates. The party — close ties through Spark, especially after the protest-end song that consolidated Alfira's leadership.

**Enemies.** The [Plague of Remembrance](../plague-of-remembrance/summary.md) — primary, with Zora having attempted to weaponize the Followers and failed. The [Cult of the Returning Lord](../cult-of-the-returning-lord/summary.md) — captured Reaper agent embedded near the temple construction site (ongoing).

**Internal complexity.** Bror's interaction with Spark's "immortality" rumors planted a seed for the Followers to potentially view Spark as a holy figure — a complication the party may not welcome.

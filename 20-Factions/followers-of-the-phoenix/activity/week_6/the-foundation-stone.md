# The Foundation Stone

Riding the wave of support from [last week's Survivor's Sermon](../../activity/week_5/the-survivors-sermon.md), [The Ember (Alfira)](../../people/the-ember.md) presides over the laying of the foundation stone for the new Temple of the Phoenix at the Phoenix Gate, on the New Elturel side of the [Basilisk Gate barricade](../../../free-traders-of-the-outskirts/activity/week_6/rationing-the-hope.md). The ceremony — robed acolytes, the Karlach prayer-flame, a hand-carved cornerstone with a crimson sash bound to it — draws a massive crowd. **Despite the [Western Gate's starvation siege](../../../western-gate-trading-company/activity/week_6/the-starvation-siege.md), volunteers donate stone, labor, and rationed bread**; the construction site becomes a symbol of stubborn hope. By sundown the cornerstone is laid and a working perimeter is staked out for the temple proper.

The cornerstone is not the only thing being put in the ground. The crowd's energy is no longer scared and devotional — it is angry and devotional, and one of [Alfira's lieutenants quietly tells the party at lunch](../../../../50-Sessions/session_22/player_summary.md) that the followers are "literally ready to start fires." The radicalization is now visible to anyone reading the room. Phoenix construction labor is also Phoenix mobilization labor; the foundation stone is also the rallying point for the next protest, riot, or — if [the Takers of the Tithe](../../../takers-of-the-tithe/summary.md) succeed in martyring her — the next holy war. The Ember knows it. She is keeping the temple ceremony slow on purpose.

# The Survivor's Sermon

[The Ember](../../people/the-ember.md), having [survived the Reapers' assassination attempt and immediately preached over the assassin's body last week](../../../followers-of-the-phoenix/activity/week_4/a-martyrs-sermon.md), returns to the Phoenix Gate stage with the assassin's blade as a prop and a city's worth of attention. The sermon is short and unsparing: *the assassin proves we are right*. The cult is too small to fear, she tells them, except the city's elite have already tried to kill her — which means **the elite are afraid of our light**. The crowd at the [New Elturel](../../../../30-Places/sword-coast/baldurs-gate/outer-city/new-elturel.md) Phoenix Gate triples by midday and runs the length of the street by nightfall; refugees stand shoulder-to-shoulder with [Hellrider militia drilled at dawn](../../../hellriders-of-new-elturel/activity/week_5/the-elturgard-alliance.md) and the same crowd that will man Meggan's blockade. The donations basket runs over four times before the sermon ends. The next morning, [Mikkel](../../people/mikkel.md) breaks ground on the temple's permanent foundation: stone, not just timber, set deep into the Phoenix Gate hardpan. **Construction of the Temple of the Phoenix begins in earnest.** The cult is no longer a movement. It is becoming a place.

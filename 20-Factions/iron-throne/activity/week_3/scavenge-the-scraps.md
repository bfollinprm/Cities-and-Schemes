# Scavenge the Scraps

While [the major chambers consume themselves at the Council vote](../../../council-of-four/activity/week_3/the-vote-of-no-confidence.md), [Iron Throne agents](../../people/the-anonymous-throne.md) work [Rivington](../../../../30-Places/sword-coast/baldurs-gate/outer-city/rivington.md) and the [Blackgate](../../../../30-Places/sword-coast/baldurs-gate/outer-city/wyrms-bend-market.md) margins quietly buying up the debts of small Outer City businesses on the verge of collapse from the [siege at the Basilisk Gate](../../../free-traders-of-the-outskirts/activity/week_3/press-the-advantage.md). A bankrupt apothecary, three failing Norchapel chandleries, two Sow's Foot tenement landlords, and the lien on a Wyrm's Bend warehouse all change hands at pittance prices. None of it is dramatic. None of it gets reported. The Throne is reassembling its quiet pre-collapse ownership of half the Outer City's small commerce, paid for by the political crisis the new-money houses are running. By the time the [siege resolves itself](../../../western-gate-trading-company/activity/week_3/the-private-option.md), the Throne will own materially more of the Outer City than it did when the siege began.

# Iron Throne

A ghost of the city's old economic order, shattered by the Bhaalspawn crisis and supplanted by the new [Merchant Consortiums](../chionthar-consortium/summary.md). Operates from a diminished manor in the [Upper City](../../30-Places/sword-coast/baldurs-gate/upper-city/README.md) under a leader known only as [the Anonymous Throne](people/the-anonymous-throne.md). Sister coster to [Seven Suns](../seven-suns/summary.md) and [High Moon Coster](../high-moon-coster/summary.md).

## Ideology

Survival through nerve where reach has failed. The Iron Throne accepts contracts and risks no consortium will touch — Undercity expeditions, mercenary brokerage, debt-buying in the wreckage of the blockade economy — and trades on its old underworld credibility long after its capital has dried up.

## Membership

A handful of remaining Iron Throne agents and brokers, working out of the family's diminished [Upper City](../../30-Places/sword-coast/baldurs-gate/upper-city/README.md) manor and frequenting the **[Blushing Mermaid](../../30-Places/sword-coast/baldurs-gate/lower-city/blushing-mermaid.md)** in the [Lower City](../../30-Places/sword-coast/baldurs-gate/lower-city/README.md) to meet contacts. Leadership is opaque even to the rank-and-file: contracts come back signed, never seen by their author.

## Methods

- **High-risk brokerage.** Brokers expensive, dangerous contracts the consortiums won't bid on. The recent Undercity expedition for a foreign merchant's heirloom ended in disaster: the hired adventuring party was ambushed and wiped out by a [Guild](../guild/summary.md) patrol, seemingly tipped off — the heirloom lost, the client ruined, and the Throne's underworld credit dented.
- **Lost the Western Gate caravan-guard contract** to an underbidding independent adventuring company, ending the wager that was supposed to refloat the books.
- **Distress-debt buying.** Quietly purchases the debts of small [Outer City](../../30-Places/sword-coast/baldurs-gate/outer-city/README.md) businesses on the verge of collapse during the blockade, acquiring their assets for a pittance in Rivington and Blackgate.
- **Information brokerage.** When a front-company gambit (the *Rivington Recovery Group*) was outed by a Seatower clerk and run in the [Baldur's Mouth](../baldurs-mouth/summary.md), the Throne pivoted to selling intelligence on Archducal mercenary numbers, supply lines, and barricade plans to both the [Free Traders](../free-traders-of-the-outskirts/summary.md) and the [Guild](../guild/summary.md) — playing both sides of the siege. By [week 5 the Anonymous Throne's brokers have assembled and double-sold a full mercenary-company dossier](activity/week_5/the-rumor-mill.md) — numbers, leadership, supply-line routing, named lieutenants — to a Mad Meggan lieutenant in Wyrm's Bend and a Guild cut-out in Brampton in the same week. **The pivot from front-company to information-brokerage is now the durable Iron Throne strategy**, and the coster's coin position has not been this strong in two years.

## Hooks and Relationships

**Sister costers.** [Seven Suns](../seven-suns/summary.md) and [High Moon Coster](../high-moon-coster/summary.md) — fellow remnants, allies in name, competitors for the scraps.

**Enemies.** Deep grudge against the Merchant Consortiums that supplanted them; lost the bid that ended the Undercity expedition to the [Western Gate Trading Company](../western-gate-trading-company/summary.md). The [Guild](../guild/summary.md) patrol that wiped out the sponsored party shows the Throne's underworld standing has slipped.

**Patrons.** Anyone with coin and a job too dirty for a respectable consortium.

**Power vacuum.** Per dm-notes, the Throne's botched Undercity expedition — alongside parallel failures by the [River-Rats](../river-rats/summary.md) and [Wharf Rats](../wharf-rats/summary.md) and the open Zhentarim–Guild war — has opened a clear *competence vacuum* in the city's shadows. A new player (the party) could plausibly take over the niche the Throne is letting slip.

# The Anonymous Throne

The dossier names no individual leader of the [Iron Throne](../summary.md). Contracts come back signed by a hand the agents themselves don't recognize; orders arrive through cut-outs at the **Blushing Mermaid**. What is visible is a pattern.

## Pattern

- **Picks fights the consortiums won't.** Undercity heirloom recovery, mercenary-guard contracts at unsafe margins, debt-buying in collapsing Outer City districts — the kind of work that demands nerve in place of capital.
- **Plays both sides.** When the *Rivington Recovery Group* front company was exposed in the [Baldur's Mouth](../../baldurs-mouth/summary.md), the Throne immediately pivoted to selling intelligence on Archducal mercenary patrols to the [Free Traders](../../free-traders-of-the-outskirts/summary.md), and the Free Traders' weakest barricade points back to the mercenary commanders. Maximum casualties, maximum gold.
- **Trades on credibility, not coin.** The Iron Throne name still opens doors in the underworld even when the family vaults do not — and every botched contract (the Undercity ambush, the lost Western Gate caravan bid) chips at that last asset.

## Possible identities

- A surviving heir of the original Iron Throne family, hiding behind cut-outs to avoid Guild and Flaming Fist attention.
- A Guild lieutenant who quietly bought the name.
- Multiple people sharing one signature — a committee passing the seal.

The DM should pick when it matters. Until then, the Throne is whoever just signed.

# Private Armies

Panicking over [last week's frozen ledgers](../../../zhentarim/activity/week_6/the-ledger-logic.md) and the Upper City lockdown, the Peerage of Coin votes — *unanimously* — to dramatically expand [the Sembian-livery private security](../../activity/week_6/the-private-security.md) at every Counting House and bank. [Consul Vorlamin Athar](../../../amnian-kontor/people/vorlamin-athar.md), already the Peerage's preferred mercenary broker, doubles his contracted Coin-Sword detail at [the Counting House itself](../../../../30-Places/sword-coast/baldurs-gate/lower-city/counting-house.md): rooftop crossbows, two-man door details on every vault, an armored payroll wagon that is now its own moving siege engine.

The Counting House becomes, functionally, **a heavily armed fortress** in the middle of the Lower City. The Fist can no longer enter without a written invitation. The distinction between *hired security* and *standing army* is now visibly empty in the most powerful private district in the city. [The Goldsmiths' Public Assay](../../../wc-goldsmiths-jewelers/activity/week_7/the-public-assay.md) is staged inside this fortress to give the Peerage cover; it works well enough that the **emergency-commerce-protection vote tomorrow morning passes 14-7**, the Coin lords' first decisive bloc victory since the Crisis.

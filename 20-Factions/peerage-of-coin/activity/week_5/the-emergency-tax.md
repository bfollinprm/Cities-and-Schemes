# The Emergency Tax

Enforcement of [the Emergency Commerce Protection Act](../../../peerage-of-coin/activity/week_4/the-emergency-levy.md) hits the streets this week, and meets a wall. The first tax-collection patrols, dispatched from [the Counting House](../../../../30-Places/sword-coast/baldurs-gate/lower-city/counting-house.md) under the bursar's seal, walk into Lower City workshop districts to find the doors blocked by ranks of Worshipful Company Guild Wardens — Smiths and Vintners with cudgels, Masons with mallets, Shipwrights and Fishmongers in matching tabards. No shouting; no charges; just bodies. The collectors are turned around at the threshold and walked back to the gate to *jeers* from the apprentices. [Consul Vorlamin Athar](../../../amnian-kontor/people/vorlamin-athar.md), nominally backing the levy on behalf of his Coin-bloc patrons, watches it fail without intervening. Within a day the entire city — patriar, freeholder, dockhand, Outer City carter — has been told the same thing in plain working-class semaphore: **the Peerage of Coin cannot enforce its own laws inside the city walls without the Worshipful Companies' permission.** The Emergency Levy is now decorative. The Archduke's army is still being paid; the bill is sliding back onto the kontors.

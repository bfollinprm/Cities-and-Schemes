# The Private Security

Humiliated by [last week's failure of the Emergency Tax](../../activity/week_5/the-emergency-tax.md) — the Peerage of Coin's flagship levy died in the Lower City workshops when [Guild wardens physically blocked the collectors](../../../guild/summary.md) — and watching [the Guild's open dominance](../../../guild/activity/week_6/the-financial-audit.md) of the streets they once owned, the Peerage of Coin votes a quiet emergency measure: hire Sembian mercenaries for personal protection. Within forty-eight hours, [Sembian-livery](../../../sembian-kontor/summary.md) private guards appear at every patriar bank, [Counting House](../../../../30-Places/sword-coast/baldurs-gate/lower-city/counting-house.md) door, and gold-merchant counter on the Lower City coin row.

The guards answer only to the coin lords who pay them — explicitly *not* to the Flaming Fist, the Watch, or [Marshal Flint's](../../../flaming-fist/people/valerius-flint.md) writ. Fist sergeants who try to enter Counting House premises on routine business are turned away at the door by Sembian halberds. The Peerage of Coin has effectively withdrawn from civic policing and announced, in steel and livery, that the city's monetary infrastructure is now defended by foreign mercenary contract. **It is the deepest cut yet in the Fist's monopoly on legitimate force.** [The Watch's gate lockdown later that day](../../../watch/activity/week_6/the-gate-lockdown.md) is not coincidence — the patriar-bloc and coin-bloc are simultaneously pulling their physical security inside their own walls.

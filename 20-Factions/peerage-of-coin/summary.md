# Peerage of Coin

The "new money" house of the Baldurian [Parliament of Peers](../parliament-of-peers/summary.md), where corporate entities purchase their seats and laws are drafted on naked economic self-interest. Created by the **1497 DR reformation** that cemented the city's plutocratic character.

## Ideology

A cauldron of ambition where laws are passed for whatever benefits the holder of the most seats this fiscal year. Birthright is a rust on the gears of progress; blood is a poor currency. The future is built on stacks of gold, not dusty tombs.

## Membership

Fifty seats, allocated to the city's largest economic entities — Merchant Consortiums, Foreign Kontors, and the most powerful Worshipful Companies. The number of seats a faction controls is recalculated annually based on tax receipts: the [Amnian Kontor](../amnian-kontor/summary.md) might hold five seats one year, the [Chionthar Consortium](../chionthar-consortium/summary.md) seven the next. Notable members include [Consul Vorlamin Athar](../amnian-kontor/people/vorlamin-athar.md) of the Amnian Kontor and the Masters of the major Worshipful Companies.

## Methods

- Drafting and debating legislation in chamber.
- Forging temporary alliances to pass laws that benefit specific economic interests.
- Annual reallocation creates a fiercely competitive environment — every Kontor and Consortium quietly engineers its tax exposure to maximize seat count.

## Hooks and Relationships

**Enemies.** Permanent political opposition to the [Peerage of Blood](../peerage-of-blood/summary.md). Internally, the chamber's component factions are in fierce competition with each other — the [Amnian Kontor](../amnian-kontor/summary.md) and [Chionthar Consortium](../chionthar-consortium/summary.md) being the loudest example.

# The Takers of the Tithe

An elite death-cult dedicated to the dead god Myrkul, led by the anonymous figure known only as [The Collector](people/the-collector.md). They stalk and murder the city's most exceptional individuals — heroes, master artisans, peerless musicians — to gather their souls as "tithe" toward Myrkul's resurrection. Distinct from but parallel to the [Cult of the Returning Lord](../cult-of-the-returning-lord/summary.md) (Bhaal); both are cells of the broader Cult of the Dead Three.

## Ideology

Bhaal's resurrection was a crude affair. Myrkul, the original Lord of the Dead, was the true master. A soul is a currency; by collecting only the purest "soul-coin" — the souls of master artisans, heroes, or great talents — the Takers will buy their dead god's return.

## Membership

Cultists are organized into hunting parties of three:

- A **Spotter**, who identifies and surveils targets.
- A **Reaper**, who performs the assassination with a soul-trapping weapon.
- A **Keeper**, who guards the captured soul gem.

The Collector communicates with followers only through written messages delivered by skeletal messengers, maintaining absolute anonymity. Composition: rumored to be a minor patriar with necromantic family history, or a surviving Myrkul priest from the old clergy — uncertain.

## Methods

- Methodical, patient stalking. Targets surveilled for weeks before the kill.
- Soul-trapping weapons that bind the soul into a gem at death.
- Public murders. Each kill is a major event in the city — by design.
- Failed acquisition. Week 0: a Spotter failed to penetrate Goldsmiths' Guild security around a target; surveillance detected, target placed under high guard, the cell forced to abandon the hunt.

## Hooks and Relationships

**Targets.** Every powerful and skilled individual in the city — making them enemies of the **Masters of the Worshipful Companies**, the leadership of the [Sorcerous Sodality](../wc-sorcerous-sodality/summary.md), and any famous adventurers (the party's growing reputation puts them squarely on the list).

**Parallel cult.** The [Cult of the Returning Lord](../cult-of-the-returning-lord/summary.md) — same Cult of the Dead Three umbrella, different revival project. The DM's framing: factionalism between Bhaal, Myrkul, and Bane is the cult network's greatest weakness.

**Enemies.** The [Gravekeepers](../gravekeepers/summary.md), who view all soul-trapping as desecration. The [Harpers](../harpers/summary.md), as standing enemies of the Dead Three.

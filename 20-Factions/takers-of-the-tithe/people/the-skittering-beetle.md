# The Skittering Beetle

An invisible, four-legged broker-entity (the players also call it "the Beetle" or "the Broker") encountered ascending the north face of [Dusthawk Hill](../../../30-Places/sword-coast/baldurs-gate/lower-city/heapside.md) and again at the Counting House summit. The Beetle is a *soul-broker* — not a deity, not a fiend the party can name, but a contract-keeping entity bound to the Takers' Bhaalist working under terms the Beetle itself negotiated. **Transactional rather than malicious.** The Beetle does not kill what it can extract from.

## Working observed

- **Theological-debt audits.** At each of the four cairns barring the path up Dusthawk Hill, the Beetle accused a named party member of a specific "theological crime" — Kaelan for *Revivify* (stealing respite from Bhaal), Elia for forging the Tithe Ledger ("playing God with ink"), Spark for "blasphemy of logic," Bror for "hoarding silence." Each accusation came with a debt offer: pay a portion of class resource, walk free; refuse, take the cairn's punitive damage and curse.
- **Outcomes:** Kaelan refused (took 24 necrotic, party-wide *Malaise*); Elia refused (took 21 psychic, *Fractured ID*); Spark accepted ("blasphemy of logic"; took 1 exhaustion); Bror accepted (sold his voice).
- **The summit bargain.** When the party was on the brink of a TPL during the Counting House battle, the Beetle offered Elia the Tithe Ledger trade: surrender the physical ledger, all curses lift and Singa freezes momentarily. Elia agreed.

## Read

The Beetle operates on *fairness*: it took only what it was offered, and it released the party's debts cleanly when the Ledger was surrendered. **It has no personal animosity.** It is professionally interested in souls and contracts; the party is a future business contact rather than an enemy. The party should expect to see it again — particularly given their two outstanding fey bargains with [the mirror-tent proprietor](../../carnival-of-whispers/people/mirror-tent-proprietor.md), which look structurally similar.

# Worshipful Company of Mercers & Clothiers

The guild that dresses the city's elite — and so the silent broker of every patriar negotiation. With the post-crisis influx of new wealth and a new class of plutocrats desperate to look the part, the Mercers' seal became the mark of true social standing. Led by the impeccably dressed [Mistress Elethra Vance](people/elethra-vance.md).

## Ideology

To control the high-end clothing market and, by extension, the visual language of status and power. Their working belief: appearance is power. Whoever defines what a duke wears defines what a duke is.

## Membership

Tailors, weavers, and dyers of the city's finest workshops. The Court of Assistants is drawn from the largest manor-tailoring houses. The guild's apprentices and journeymen are vetted for discretion as much as skill — the workrooms hear everything.

## Methods

- Exclusive import contracts with the [Waterdhavian](../waterdhavian-kontor/summary.md) and [Amnian](../amnian-kontor/summary.md) Kontors for raw textiles. Week 1: Vance secured a Waterdhavian-silk monopoly for the upcoming patriar winter festival.
- Trend-setting at patriar galas — what is worn at one season's salon defines next season's market.
- Information brokerage. The fitting rooms of patriar manors are listening posts; gossip from them is sold or saved as leverage.
- Internal counter-espionage. Week 1: a journeyman caught selling winter-gown designs to a Bloomridge rival was quietly fired and blacklisted.

## Hooks and Relationships

**Enemies.** Independent artisans in the Outer City who work outside the guild seal. Rivalry with the [Worshipful Company of Butchers & Tanners](../wc-butchers-tanners/summary.md) over the use of fine leather in fashion.

**Allies of convenience.** The [Waterdhavian Kontor](../waterdhavian-kontor/summary.md) for silk; the [Amnian Kontor](../amnian-kontor/summary.md) for southern dyes.

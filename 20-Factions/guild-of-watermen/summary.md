# The Guild of Watermen

A **Waterdhavian guild** of bargemen, dockhands, water-haulers, and harbor-yard labor — the people who actually move cargo from ship to warehouse and from warehouse to street. Distinct from the Worshipful Companies (the chartered Baldurian model); the Watermen are a **labor guild** in the Waterdhavian tradition, with a charter granted by the Lords and a strike weapon they have used before.

Operates primarily out of [Waterdeep](../../30-Places/sword-coast/waterdeep/README.md)'s [Trades Ward](../../30-Places/sword-coast/waterdeep/trades-ward/character.md) and Dock Ward. By the dossier's accounting, the Watermen and the [Fellowship of Carters and Coachmen](../fellowship-of-carters-and-coachmen/summary.md) are the two guilds that can — by themselves, by stopping work — halt the city's trade.

## What they do

- **Move cargo.** Stevedores, crane operators, harbor clerks, lighter crews. Every ton off-loaded at the Dock Ward passes through Watermen hands at some point.
- **Haul fresh water.** A second function rooted in the same labor pool: cisterns, tower deliveries, and the trade in cleaner water from the higher-ward fountains.
- **Enforce closed-shop hiring.** Practicing the trade without membership is illegal under Guild Law; non-guild crews are turned away at the dock or, occasionally, dunked off it.

## The strike weapon

The Watermen's leadership is reluctant to call a full strike — the membership lives day-to-day and a strike is expensive — but the *threat* is permanent and shapes every negotiation with the Lords over taxes, port fees, and Watch behavior at the docks. The 1256 DR Guildmasters' Folly is institutional memory on both sides: the Lords distrust guilds that can starve the city, and the Watermen know they are watched for any sign of a coordinated guild front.

## Friction in 1502 DR

- **Artisan drain.** Lower-tier members are being lured south to Baldur's Gate's [Gray Harbour](../../30-Places/sword-coast/baldurs-gate/lower-city/gray-harbour.md), where post-crisis reconstruction pays better. Dues are lapsing in some Trades Ward halls.
- **Shadow War.** Manshoon's [Zhentarim](../zhentarim/summary.md) thugs and [Xanathar Guild](../xanathars-guild/summary.md) brutes both pressure Watermen crews for off-the-books unloading work; the guild leadership has not chosen a side and is paying for the neutrality in chipped fingers.

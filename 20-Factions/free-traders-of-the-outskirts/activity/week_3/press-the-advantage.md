# Press the Advantage

Riding the political momentum of [last week's public confession](../week_2/dig-in-for-the-siege.md) from the captive cultist, ['Mad' Meggan](../../people/mad-meggan.md) tries to convert it into formal arrests. Her demand is simple — the patriar names the cultist gave should answer at [High Hall](../../../../30-Places/sword-coast/baldurs-gate/upper-city/README.md). The bombshell fizzles. Editor [Ettvard Needle](../../../baldurs-mouth/people/ettvard-needle.md), under direct pressure from the implicated patriar families and [too busy chasing Marshal Flint himself](../../../baldurs-mouth/activity/week_3/the-price-of-insubordination.md), refuses to print the unsubstantiated claims of a captive. Her pamphlets are dismissed across the Lower City as radical propaganda. The Council ignores her. Meggan looks, for the first time since the siege began, like a rabble-rouser without leverage — and the [Sembian](../../../sembian-kontor/activity/week_3/the-alternate-route.md) buyout offer she refused last week starts to look less generous.

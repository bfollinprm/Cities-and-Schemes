# Sorcerous Sodality *(aka Sorcerous Sundries)*

The reformed Sorcerous Sundries — granted a Worshipful Company charter after the Absolute Crisis to bring uncontrolled magic to heel. The Sodality issues licenses to every legitimate spellcaster-for-hire in Baldur's Gate, regulates the magical goods market, and guards the Sorcerous Vault beneath its tower. Led by [Master Lorroakan](people/lorroakan.md) (or his successor).

## Ideology

To control and regulate every public use of magic within the city, ensuring quality of services and containment of magical threats. Their working belief: uncontrolled magic is the single greatest danger to Baldur's Gate, demonstrated catastrophically during the Absolute Crisis. The licensing fee is the toll on that danger.

## Membership

The city's most powerful and politically connected wizards and sorcerers. The Master operates from the tower of Sorcerous Sundries; the Court of Assistants includes the chief licensors and the keepers of the Sorcerous Vault. Apprenticeship is selective and politically sensitive — the wrong sponsor can end a career before it starts.

## Methods

- Spellcaster-for-hire licensing — a hefty fee per license, renewed annually. Unlicensed casting is a serious crime, prosecuted aggressively.
- Monopoly on magical scrolls, components, and most enchanted items sold legally in the city.
- Raids on hedge wizards. Week 0: enforcers raided a Bloomridge attic, confiscating components and spellbook from a charm-and-love-potion peddler.
- The Sorcerous Vault — closely guarded archive of confiscated and sealed magical knowledge beneath the tower.

## Hooks and Relationships

**Enemies.** Unlicensed spellcasters and "hedge wizards" of the Outer City. Warlock-pact cults — particularly the [Carnival of Whispers](../carnival-of-whispers/summary.md), [Knights of the Shield](../knights-of-the-shield/summary.md), and the various Conspiracy Cells whose paladin-of-conspiracy figures wield magic the Sodality cannot account for. The [Unveiled](../unveiled/summary.md) consider the Sodality a primary architect of the Absolute Crisis cover-up.

**Allies of convenience.** The [Council of Four](../council-of-four/summary.md) on threats requiring magical response; the [Harpers](../harpers/summary.md) for shared concerns about uncontrolled magic.

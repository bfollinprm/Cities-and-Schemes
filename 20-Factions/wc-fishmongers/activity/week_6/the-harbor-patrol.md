# The Harbor Patrol

With the [Flaming Fist visibly absent from the docks](../../../flaming-fist/activity/week_6/the-officers-dilemma.md) and [Master Tallowshand's drydock lockout](../../../wc-shipwrights-calkers/activity/week_6/the-drydock-lockout.md) creating a new class of beached, desperate vessels, [Matron Marga Saltwind](../../people/marga-saltwind.md) organizes the Fishmongers into an armed harbor militia. Net-knives, gaffing hooks, salt-tipped clubs — every fish-hauler over the age of fifteen is drilled into a four-watch patrol. The Fishmongers walk Gray Harbour piers in pairs by day and threes at night.

The patrols are brutal but legible. Anyone caught stealing from the fish-stalls or making trouble at the auction-house is hauled to the breakwater and **thrown into the bay** — *let the harbor decide*, Saltwind tells her enforcers. Word spreads inside two days; the petty-theft rate at the docks plummets and a rough sort of order holds. The Fishmongers have answered [last week's poisoner-bounty fiasco](../../activity/week_5/find-the-poisoner.md) the only way Saltwind knows: visible, public, painful enforcement. The Wharf Rats nod approvingly; the patriar boroughs that depend on Gray Harbour fish are openly grateful; the Fist is humiliated by its own absence.

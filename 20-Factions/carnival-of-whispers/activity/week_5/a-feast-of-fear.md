# A Feast of Fear

[Mister Smiles](../../people/mister-smiles.md), still hunting [the unknown rival fey predator drawn to the city's chaotic magical residue](../../../carnival-of-whispers/activity/week_4/the-debt-comes-due.md), needs to feed his patron *now* — and tries to harvest the easiest crop the city offers: unguarded crowd-fear. The Carnival's performers move into the [Rivington markets](../../../../30-Places/sword-coast/baldurs-gate/outer-city/rivington.md) and stage one of their old reliable acts — eerie magical illusions, wrong-key music, mirror-faces in unexpected places — calibrated to flush dread out of a market crowd and into a glass jar. The crop fails. The Rivington crowd has been hardened by [a year of cult violence, the blockade, the new mercenary checkpoints on the rural roads](../../../western-gate-trading-company/activity/week_5/choke-the-supply-lines.md), and [a butcher-mob in the next ward over](../../../cult-of-the-returning-lord/activity/week_5/relocate-the-sanctum.md). Eerie illusions are not a fear-stimulus to people who have been afraid for breakfast. The crowd's reaction is *aggressive*: stalls turn over, an apple-cart is upended onto a stilt-walker, off-duty Hellriders chase a mirror-tent operative into a horse-trough. The performers are run out of Rivington. **Mister Smiles' patron remains starving, and his troupe is bruised.**

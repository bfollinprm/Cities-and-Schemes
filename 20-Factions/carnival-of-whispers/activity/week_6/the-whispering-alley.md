# The Whispering Alley

Chased from the Rivington markets [last week by off-duty Hellriders](../../activity/week_5/a-feast-of-fear.md), [Mister Smiles](../../people/mister-smiles.md) takes the show *underground*. The troupe sets up a **guerrilla circus in the shadowed alleys behind [the Blushing Mermaid](../../../../30-Places/sword-coast/baldurs-gate/lower-city/blushing-mermaid.md)** — no tent, no banners, just the four core performers, three illusion-conjured glowing skulls (Smiles juggles them, badly, on purpose), a contortionist twin pair for atmosphere, and a hat at the alley mouth.

The new venue targets a different crowd. Where Rivington crowds were too hardened by years of crisis to feel the eldritch fear-pull, the alley audience is the Lower City's tavern drunks, broken-down sewer jacks, off-shift dockworkers in their cups, and the occasional patriar's son slumming. **Their hopelessness is a different and richer fuel** than fear. Smiles harvests it through the troupe's mirror-tent magic in trace amounts — a sip of despair from a hundred witnesses, not a glut of terror from one. The patron's hunger is staved off for another week. Smiles is laughing, in private. The Carnival is also now operating *inside the Guild's territory* without permission, and [the Guild's brutal internal audit](../../../guild/activity/week_6/the-financial-audit.md) means there are no spare bodies to evict him yet.

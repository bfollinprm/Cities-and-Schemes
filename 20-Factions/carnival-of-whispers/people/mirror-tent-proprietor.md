# The Mirror-Tent Proprietor

The unnamed grey-skinned elven figure who runs the small violet silk tent at the [Circus of the Last Days](../summary.md), beneath a placard reading "A glimpse of what you deserve." The tent is full of hundreds of floating mirrors that show neither reflections nor fantasies — they show **fleeting scenes of the onlooker's actual past and one of the genuine futures still available to them**.

## Pattern

The proprietor selects targets with deep regrets and ambitions, draws them to a specific mirror keyed to their longing, and offers a fey bargain: the path to the future they saw, in exchange for **one true secret never told another soul**. The bargain is delayed-payment — the secret is owed at a later date, and the proprietor will come to collect.

In [week 3](../../party/activity/week_3/infiltrate-the-circus-of-the-last-days.md):

- **Elia Thorne** picked up an ornate copper hand mirror; saw a perfected version of herself as a master broker of the Undercellar commanding the respect of the Guildmaster. Accepted the bargain. Owes a secret.
- **Bror "Shadow Stone"** picked up a square of polished obsidian; saw himself alone in a silent, dead dwarven hold commanding shadows. The proprietor named him "Shadow Stone" without being told. Accepted the bargain. Owes a secret about the oath he swore in the dark.

Both bargains remain unpaid. The proprietor will surface to collect.

## Read

The mirror tent is the Circus's **personalized-transaction** tier — costlier than [Zara's mass grief harvest](zara.md), separate from [Lucretius's cosmic-memory acquisitions](lucretius.md), but feeding into the same Feywild economy. The proprietor's currency is not just secrets — it is the binding the secret creates. Each accepted bargain plants a fey hook the Circus can use later: by appearing in dreams, by ambushing in alleys, by sending a Memory Mite.

# The Infernal Ledger

Having secured the loyalty of the desperate manorborn noble [recruited last week under the Hidden Lord's infernal-protection terms](../../activity/week_5/a-desperate-bargain.md), the Knights of the Shield's anonymous patron — known to the order only as **the Hidden Lord** — calls the price due. The patron demands the family's private journals: **the Peerage of Blood voting history**, including private correspondence on caucus and proxy votes going back two decades. The noble delivers in tears the same night, three leather-bound volumes in a polished oak coffer.

The journals are blackmail material of the rarest kind: enough to threaten three sitting [Peerage of Blood](../../../peerage-of-blood/summary.md) seats with public exposure of votes they would prefer the public not see. The Knights use the leverage immediately, **forcing a swing vote on a crucial trade tariff** in the Parliament that would have favored [the Sembian Kontor](../../../sembian-kontor/summary.md) — three Blood peers vote against their bloc on the morning of the third day, the tariff passes only narrowly, and Durinbold's bloc is publicly humiliated for the first time in months. **The Knights of the Shield have just demonstrated they can move Parliament from the shadows.** The Hidden Lord's name remains, as ever, a blank space in every record. [The collapse of the moderate private caucus that same evening](../../../parliament-of-peers/activity/week_6/the-private-caucus.md) is, no doubt, related.

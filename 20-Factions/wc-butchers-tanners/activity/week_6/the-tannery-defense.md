# The Tannery Defense

Besieged by [the Druids' choking vines](../../../circle-of-the-inner-grove/activity/week_6/the-source-of-the-rot.md), Goodman Borlu, "the Cleaver," arms his butchers and tanners with cleavers, torches, and the iron-shod tannery hooks normally used for curing-frame work. A violent skirmish erupts in the Tanneries before dawn — the men hacking at vines that *bleed sap when cut and grow back from the cut points within minutes*, and the druids in the shadows answering with thorn-storms and the screams of frightened crows.

The defense fails on its own terms. The men hold the perimeter long enough to evacuate the master-tanners and the most expensive tanning chemistry — silvered solutions, the special pre-Crisis Mordenkainen-stamped lyes — but they cannot stop the vines from boiling through the curing-vat block. By dawn the building is half-overgrown, three tanks are split, and a year's worth of tannic poison is in the gutters of the Outer City. **Borlu loses two journeymen to the vine-thorns and one apprentice to a fall** — and the Circle's druids walk out unwounded under a moon-shadow charm. Borlu swears blood vengeance to anyone within earshot. The Tanneries are publicly the front line of a sudden new front in the city's war: nature versus industry.

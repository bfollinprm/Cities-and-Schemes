# Worshipful Company of Butchers & Tanners

Ancient and wealthy, but their bloody trade has always placed them low on the social ladder. The cultural cost has fostered fierce pride, clannishness, and a chip on the shoulder of every member of the Court of Assistants. Led by the intimidating [Goodman Borlu, "the Cleaver"](people/goodman-borlu.md).

## Ideology

To protect their commercial interests and earn the social respect they feel they deserve. Defensive of their members; aggressive against any encroachment on their territory in the city's industrial districts.

## Membership

Slaughterhouse owners, butcher-shop masters, and the operators of the city's tanneries. The Tanners' lodges and the Butchers' lodges meet jointly under the guild charter, though tensions between the two halves are constant.

## Methods

- Price-setting and supply control on meat and leather across the city.
- Brutal Guild Wardens, notorious for the way they handle livestock-rustlers and unlicensed butchers.
- Industrial pollution as cost-cutting strategy. Week 0: Borlu's tanneries dumped vats of toxic chemical runoff directly into a Chionthar tributary to avoid disposal fees.
- Bribed media coverage. [Baldur's Mouth](../baldurs-mouth/summary.md) ran a "donation drive" puff piece for the guild that buried a spoiled-meat story Goodman Borlu paid Ettvard Needle to kill.

## Hooks and Relationships

**Enemies.** Perpetual conflict with the [Circle of the Inner Grove](../circle-of-the-inner-grove/summary.md), whose druids see tannery runoff as poison in the Chionthar. Tension with the [Worshipful Company of Mercers & Clothiers](../wc-mercers-clothiers/summary.md) over the use of fine leather in fashion.

**Allies of convenience.** [Baldur's Mouth](../baldurs-mouth/summary.md) at need — Borlu's payments have repeatedly killed unflattering stories.

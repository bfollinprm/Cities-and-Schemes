# Severing Ties

[Mistress Anya Brightwood](../../people/anya-brightwood.md), reading [last week's compromised Eastway lamplighter intelligence pipeline](../../../wc-chandlers-lamplighters/activity/week_4/eyes-in-the-dark.md) as a controllable problem, issues a guild order: pull all lamplighters from the high-risk Outer City routes and consolidate the night-shifts onto safer Lower City streets. The order is signed and read aloud at the [Norchapel](../../../../30-Places/sword-coast/baldurs-gate/outer-city/norchapel.md) chapter house. *It is ignored.* Brightwood's own crews, bribed weeks ago by [the Guild's local Ward Boss](../../../guild/people/astele-keene.md), continue to walk the dangerous routes — paid more by the Guild for the eyes-and-ears work than the Chandlers' guild-fee covers. By midweek lamplighter foremen are openly taking instruction from Guild contacts and not from the Chandlers' Hall. Brightwood realises, in a mortifying meeting with the Norchapel chapter, that **the Guild now effectively controls her lamplighters directly**, bypassing her authority entirely. The compromised pipeline is no longer a leak — it is a *takeover*. The Chandlers' position in the Outer City is operationally captured.

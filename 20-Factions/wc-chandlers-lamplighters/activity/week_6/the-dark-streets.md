# The Dark Streets

Under direct pressure from [the Guild — whose internal financial audit](../../../guild/activity/week_6/the-financial-audit.md) has the entire Lower City underworld jumpy and looking for value-add — [Mistress Anya Brightwood](../../people/anya-brightwood.md) is forced to order a "maintenance blackout" in the Brampton and Heapside corridors where [Roah Moonglow's Zhentarim](../../../zhentarim/summary.md) operate. Lamplighter crews — [now Guild-bribed since week 5](../../activity/week_5/severing-ties.md) — pull oil, "fail" to relight extinguished lamps, and quietly disable the alley lanterns on Brightwood's official authority. The guildhall books the work as "lamp-oil shortage; service interruption to be remedied next quarter."

The blackout is cover for a Guild hit-squad operation. With three blocks of Brampton plunged into darkness for two consecutive nights, **Glass Malakor's enforcers move openly through Zhentarim territory** — at least one Zhentarim courier is found dead in an alley, throat opened, no torch nearby. Brightwood signs the order under duress. The Chandlers' fragile neutrality, [already publicly compromised in week 5](../../activity/week_5/severing-ties.md), is now operationally over: they are a Guild auxiliary in everything but name. Anya privately writes to [Lord Hhune of the Elturel Remembrance Society](../../../elturel-remembrance-society/people/perrine-hhune.md) about the trapped feeling — that letter will not stay private.

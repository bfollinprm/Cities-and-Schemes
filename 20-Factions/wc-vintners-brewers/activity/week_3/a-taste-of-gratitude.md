# A Taste of Gratitude

[Master Belba Quickfoot](../../people/belba-quickfoot.md), reading the political shift toward the [Patriar Heritage Society](../../../patriar-heritage-society/activity/week_3/defend-the-marshal.md), sends a generous "gift of gratitude" — twelve casks of the Company's premium brown ale and a hand-written note — to [Lord Corin Durinbold](../../../peerage-of-blood/people/corin-durinbold.md) at Durinbold Manor in [Manorborn](../../../../30-Places/sword-coast/baldurs-gate/upper-city/manorborn.md), thanking him for his "defense of Baldurian institutions" and pitching the Vintners as a Worshipful Company of patriar sensibility. Durinbold reads the gesture as exactly what it is: a crass attempt by a new-money guild to buy patriar favor. He has the ale publicly returned to the Vintners' guildhall the following morning, accompanied by a scathing note that circulates faster than the original gift. The Company is mortified. Quickfoot's standing among the Worshipful Company chamber softens; the [Smiths](../../../wc-smiths-armorers/activity/week_3/an-appeal-to-the-marshal.md) and the [Mercers](../../../wc-mercers-clothiers/activity/week_3/the-color-of-authority.md) make a quiet point at her expense the same week.

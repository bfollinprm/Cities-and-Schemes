# The Ale Embargo

Furious about [the poisoned ale shipment last week](../../activity/week_5/securing-the-supply-line.md) — Zhentarim agents in the sewers tampered with a Vintners' caravan bound for the Eastway mercenary barracks, and several Sembian soldiers fell ill — [Master Belba Quickfoot](../../people/belba-quickfoot.md) declares **a total embargo** on all sales to the mercenary camps. Not a drop of ale, wine, beer, or stronger spirits is to leave Vintners' guild premises for any wagon flying Sembian colors or marked for the Eastway barracks. Every guildhall in the city is sent the order under Quickfoot's seal.

The embargo's stated reason is hygiene — the Vintners cannot in good conscience sell to a customer whose supply chain was demonstrably compromised on the previous shipment. The actual reason is leverage: Quickfoot will not forgive [Korgan Ironhand's twenty-percent supply hike](../../../sembian-kontor/activity/week_6/the-price-of-steel.md), nor the Sembian camp's failure to investigate the poisoning, nor [the Master Droog poison-analysis ledger trade](../../../wc-alchemists-apothecaries/activity/week_6/the-poison-analysis.md) that revealed it was Zhentarim and not Vintner negligence. The mercenaries go dry by Tendays-day three and the camp's morale buckles. Riot rumors at Eastway begin by week's end.

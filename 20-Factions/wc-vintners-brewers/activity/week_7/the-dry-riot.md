# The Dry Riot

[Master Belba Quickfoot's](../../people/belba-quickfoot.md) [total ale embargo against the Sembian camps from last tenday](../../activity/week_6/the-ale-embargo.md) finally cracks soldier discipline. On the fourth dry night, a company of Sembian field mercenaries — already paid in copper from [the Gilded Vein's flood](../../../gilded-vein/activity/week_7/the-copper-crash.md) and barred from any Vintner-licensed tap-room — **deserts its post at the Eastway barracks** and storms a small unlicensed grog-shop two streets over. The fight that follows kills two of the proprietor's nephews and wounds eleven mercenaries; the unit refuses recall orders for sixteen hours.

The riot makes the next morning's *Baldur's Mouth* (the [Puppet Press's](../../../baldurs-mouth/activity/week_7/the-puppet-press.md) third headline of the day) as a "**rogue mercenary outburst**" — but every Outer City refugee already knows the Vintners did it on purpose. Quickfoot's embargo is a *demonstration*: the Worshipful Companies can break the Archduke's army through a guild ledger, no swords drawn. The Sembian command negotiates a Vintner truce by sundown of the fifth day, paying triple-rate for emergency ale shipments. **The Vintners win the round.**

# The Hostile Takeover

Leveraging [last week's seizure of two Chionthar Consortium river barges](../../activity/week_5/retaliatory-asset-seizure.md), [Consul Vorlamin Athar](../../people/vorlamin-athar.md) moves to acquire **Brindlestone & Sons**, a struggling Chionthar-aligned shipping firm whose books have quietly bled red since [the Consortium's reserve gold was stolen from the Undercity cache](../../../chionthar-consortium/activity/week_5/secure-the-riverward-approaches.md). Athar offers to forgive a sequence of "sudden" debt notes (most of which he engineered the timing of) in exchange for a 51% stake. Brindlestone signs at the Counting House on the third day — there is no other choice — and Athar's Amnian flag is over the firm's Gray Harbour warehouse by sundown.

The takeover hands the Amnian Kontor a *direct* operating presence on the Chionthar river fleet for the first time in living memory and breaks [the Consortium's](../../../chionthar-consortium/summary.md) effective monopoly on river logistics. [Factor Errinthal](../../../chionthar-consortium/people/factor-errinthal.md), already running a [desperate smuggling job past the Amnian blockade](../../../chionthar-consortium/activity/week_6/the-desperate-run.md) to keep payroll, learns from the Counting House gossip mill before he learns from his own clerks. **The Amnians now own a river barge fleet and a Lower City shipping office that used to be his.** The siege has not ended a single trade war; it has finished one and started the next.

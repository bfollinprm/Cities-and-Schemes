# Roah Moonglow

Local director of the [Zhentarim](../summary.md) presence in Baldur's Gate. A cunning trader and operator who runs the Black Network's cell-based local hierarchy and is personally responsible for the campaign to supplant Nine-Fingers Keene.

## Manifesto

> Honor? Justice? Tradition? These are the fairy tales the powerful tell you to keep you poor. There is only one truth: everything has a price. Loyalty can be bought. Fear can be sold. Power is a commodity, and we are its most aggressive traders. While The Guild plays at being the people's champion, we are honest about what we are. We are the Black Network. We will own this city's shadows, because that's where the real money is.

## Role

The Zhentarim were a presence in Baldur's Gate even before the Absolute Crisis. During the crisis they proved willing to work with anyone — including the Absolute's forces — if it served their bottom line. Roah took over the local cell during the chaos and has spent a decade methodically expanding influence at the Guild's expense.

She runs the network from no fixed address, rotating through compartmentalized cells whose enforcers do not know each other's identities. Operations are split between legitimate fronts (import-export warehouses, gambling dens, the Velvet Hand social club) and the assassin/enforcer wings.

## Current pressures

- The Brampton arson and the public opening of the **Velvet Hand** across from the burned-out Guild tavern are her opening move in a war meant to dare Keene into a costly retaliation.
- Recently dispatched an agent to recruit any outlawed adventurers, offering to erase Flaming Fist warrants in exchange for service to the Black Network.
- Brokered an arrangement with Master Vancroft of the [Worshipful Company of Cartographers & Surveyors](../../wc-cartographers-surveyors/summary.md) for proprietary maps sold under the table.
- Now openly hiring out small Zhentarim squads to the [Chionthar Consortium](../../chionthar-consortium/summary.md) for dock-side work after the Wharf Rats' "solidarity tax" overreached.

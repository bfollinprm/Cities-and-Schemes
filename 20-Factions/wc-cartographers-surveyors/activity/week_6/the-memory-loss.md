# The Memory Loss

[Master Elias Vancroft](../../people/elias-vancroft.md) tries to piece together what happened to his mapping team — [stripped, mind-wiped, and dumped naked at Wyrm's Crossing by Commander K'laar's githyanki the previous week](../../activity/week_5/mapping-the-battlefield.md). The survivors remember nothing of the fortnight before the wipe. Vancroft hires a respected Lathanderian cleric — *not* [Kaelan](../../../baldurs-mouth/people/kaelan.md), who is busy at the Baldur's Mouth — and pays a 1,500-gp diamond for a *Greater Restoration* attempt on the most senior of the wiped surveyors, hoping to recover the lost memories *and* the strategic map data they were carrying.

The ritual fails. The cleric's working strikes the K'liir mind-wipe like a spell hits a wall — the wipe is *psionic*, not magical, and *Greater Restoration* does not touch it. The surveyor sits in the temple chair for the full hour of the ritual, eyes open, the diamond crumbling to dust on the altar, and at the end remembers no more than he did at the beginning. Vancroft loses the diamond, the day, and the last hope of getting the map data back. The Cartographers' Guild is now publicly known to be **toothless against the K'liir presence**, and Vancroft's banking contacts will quietly step back from the next year's mapping commissions. The Sodality's [system-reboot failure two days later](../../../wc-sorcerous-sodality/activity/week_6/the-system-reboot.md) is, for Vancroft, the final evidence: the city has become unscry-able. He is the last of the city's information-guilds to figure out what the K'liir have already cost.

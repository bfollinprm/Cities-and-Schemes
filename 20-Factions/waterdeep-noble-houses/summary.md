# Waterdhavian Noble Houses

The eighty-five noble houses of Waterdeep. Each is a faction-scale actor in its own right: hereditary "merchant princes" with a private army (legal cap of **seventy soldiers** since the 1248 DR settlement), a recognized line of business — banking, vintners, shipping, mercenary contracting — and a permanent seat at the city's social table.

Each named house in this folder lives under [associations/](associations/) as a one-page card. New houses are added there as the campaign promotes them.

## The Great Game

A perpetual social-status contest. Lavish parties, strategic marriages, commercial ventures, cutting insults during the social season. House standing translates directly into influence; an invitation to the right Sea Ward villa is itself a political instrument.

**Tiers:**

- **The Throne (1502 DR).** The two-to-five houses currently dominating the social scene. Today: [Cassalanter](associations/house-cassalanter.md), [Amcathra](associations/house-amcathra.md), [Tesper](associations/house-tesper.md).
- **The Court.** Ten-to-twenty schemers in the next rank, including the rising and the recently fallen. [Adarbrent](associations/house-adarbrent.md), [Thann](associations/house-thann.md), [Urmbrusk](associations/house-urmbrusk.md), [Agundar](associations/house-agundar.md).
- **The Body.** The unremarkable majority of the eighty-five.
- **The Dregs.** Publicly scorned. [Husteem](associations/house-husteem.md), [Maerklos](associations/house-maerklos.md), Zun.

## The two axes

Within the Great Game, two ideological axes cut across the tiers:

- **Pro-aristocracy** vs. **pro-plutocracy.** Power as birthright vs. power as wealth. The Sea Ward concentrates the first; the North Ward concentrates the second. **[Dagult Neverember's](../masked-lords/people/dagult-neverember.md)** practice of selling noble titles to wealthy merchants made this axis bitter.
- **Ahghaironian** (anonymity is the safeguard) vs. **Open Ruler** (secrecy is itself tyranny). Most houses lean one way; few say so out loud.

## Compromised seats

Two of the houses' heads sit anonymously on the [Masked Lords](../masked-lords/summary.md) — and both of those seats are owned, in practice, by [Manshoon](../zhentarim/people/manshoon.md):

- **[Corylus Thann](../masked-lords/people/corylus-thann.md)** — financial leverage. House [Thann](associations/house-thann.md), Court tier, vintners.
- **[Jelenn Urmbrusk](../masked-lords/people/jelenn-urmbrusk.md)** — personal leverage. House [Urmbrusk](associations/house-urmbrusk.md), Court tier, money-lending.

Manshoon already votes inside the council through these two. No one else on the council can identify which masks are his, much less remove them.

## Hidden faces

The dossier reads house facades as the campaign's textbook examples of *splendor as theater, paranoia as weather*:

- **[Cassalanter](associations/house-cassalanter.md)** projects philanthropy and Sea Ward respectability while secretly worshipping [Asmodeus](../../10-Setting/cosmology/deities/asmodeus.md). The bargain devours their own children.
- **[Thann](associations/house-thann.md)** and **[Urmbrusk](associations/house-urmbrusk.md)** project Court routine while their heads vote as Manshoon orders.

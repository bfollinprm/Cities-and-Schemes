# The Bibliophile

A one-woman intelligence brokerage operating out of a cluttered Lower City bookshop, run by [Nansi Gretta](people/nansi-gretta.md) and a small network of paid sources. The Bibliophile sells secrets to whoever pays the most — kontors, consortiums, ward bosses, even the occasional cult — and survives by being scrupulously neutral about who the buyer is.

## Ideology

No ideology of its own. The product is intelligence, the price is fair-market, and the buyer's politics are not the seller's concern. Gretta cultivates a reputation for accuracy: a tip that turns out wrong is worse than a tip not given.

## Membership

Gretta and a small stable of stringers — disgruntled clerks, dockside watchers, household staff in patriar manors, retired Guild informants who needed a quieter retirement. The bookshop front is small and unmemorable by design.

## Methods

- Long-form intelligence packages assembled from multiple sources, sold for high prices to specific clients.
- Leverage-creation. Week 0: Gretta's network sold proof to a [Chionthar Consortium](../chionthar-consortium/summary.md) member that his partner was making side deals with the [Sembian Kontor](../sembian-kontor/summary.md) — the resulting fallout benefited her other clients.
- Discretion as product. Buyers and sellers are kept compartmentalized; the Bibliophile's continued operation depends on neither side knowing how much the other paid.

## Hooks and Relationships

**Allies of convenience.** Anyone who pays. Gretta sometimes coordinates with [The Guild](../guild/summary.md) on Lower City matters and with the [Harpers](../harpers/summary.md) when the leak serves both sides.

**Enemies.** Anyone who has been burned by an accurate tip and traced it back. The shop has been ransacked twice in the past decade.

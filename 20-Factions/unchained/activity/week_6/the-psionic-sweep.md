# The Psionic Sweep

[Commander K'laar the Unshackled](../../people/k-laar.md) orders a **psionic sweep** of the districts surrounding Wyrm's Rock to root out the source of the "psychic scream" detected earlier — actually [the Hands of the Absolute's botched transfusion ritual in a Heapside cellar](../../../hands-of-the-absolute/activity/week_6/the-silent-scream.md), but K'laar reads it as enemy intrusion and escalates accordingly. Githyanki patrols in full astral regalia move through Wyrm's Crossing, Tumbledown, and the Outer City pump-streets with their second-sight wide open, **forcibly scanning the minds of every passerby** for the psionic signature of the originating ritual.

The sweep terrifies the locals. Citizens are stopped at random, told to "stand still," and held under a heavy psionic touch for twenty seconds while the patrol leader's eyes go silver-blank. The civilians come away nauseous, frightened, and convinced (correctly) that something old and powerful just rifled through their thoughts. There are no arrests because there are no traitors *in* the Outer City — the psionic source is in [Heapside](../../../../30-Places/sword-coast/baldurs-gate/lower-city/heapside.md), where K'laar is not patrolling — but the sweep is **front-page material in the next Baldur's Mouth issue** and adds a fresh layer to the city's "the K'liir are scanning us" mythology. K'laar does not care about the panic. He cares that he has the perimeter, and that the Hands' ritual was loud enough to fingerprint the cell.

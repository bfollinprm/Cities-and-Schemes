# Unlock the Shard of the First Scribe

Lucretius touches Elia's obsidian shard and activates it. Elia and Bror, who chooses to access the memory with her, are flooded with a cosmic vision: a psychic ocean of mortal souls being caged and channeled by divine wills — the act of "naming." A vast consciousness severs a piece of itself rather than be named, fleeing categorization into silence. Elia's shard is what is left: not a stone, but **a scream frozen into silence** — the rejected divinity of a forgotten God of Naming.

The shard re-shapes in Elia's hand into a new spellcasting focus, **the Shard of the First Scribe**. Elia's frame for her own arc shifts: not a cultist of a Great Old One but the bearer of a forgotten god-fragment that itself rejected the divine. Bror, who shares the vision, gains a glimpse of his Sharran patron's relationship to "naming" he was not meant to see. Lucretius collects neither memory; the bargain was a fair game and he honored it.

The party departs the Feywild and returns to [Rivington](../../../../30-Places/sword-coast/baldurs-gate/outer-city/rivington.md) to discover **two weeks have passed on the Material Plane**. The [Flaming Fist warrant](../../../flaming-fist/activity/week_3/circle-the-wagons.md) has cooled. [Lena](../../../../30-Places/sword-coast/baldurs-gate/outer-city/people/lena.md) charges them 50 gp for keeping their rooms; Spark pays. Bror attempts a third Whispering Stone attunement on a short rest, fails the Wisdom save, and is deafened again.

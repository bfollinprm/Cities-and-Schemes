# The Press Blackout

[Archduke Lyra Silvershield](../../people/lyra-silvershield.md) executes a **two-front information operation** in the same tenday. Front one: **starve the Outer City**. Sembian patrols on every Outer City road now confiscate not only contraband but **personal food stores** — the family ham, the harvest jar, the salt-pork pail — from any traveller crossing the cordon, weaponizing hunger to break [the Free Trader blockade](../../../free-traders-of-the-outskirts/activity/week_7/smuggling-the-crumbs.md) and [the Vine Barricades](../../../circle-of-the-inner-grove/activity/week_7/vine-barricades.md) without firing on them directly.

Front two: **silence the Mouth**. Silvershield uses her Western Gate cargo channels and patriar bench-influence to lock down the [Baldur's Mouth printing presses](../../../baldurs-mouth/activity/week_7/the-puppet-press.md) — controlling paper supply, ink supply, and the new editor's salary directly out of Western Gate accounts. With Ettvard Needle missing and the Interim Editor on her payroll, no further dissenting articles will be printed. The combination is brutal in its simplicity: *empty bellies and an empty press*. The Outer City has neither food nor a public voice. **By week's end the Press Blackout is the Council's de facto policy** — opposed openly only by [the Peerage of Blood's Ultimatum](../../../peerage-of-blood/activity/week_7/the-ultimatum-to-the-duke.md), which fails by four votes.

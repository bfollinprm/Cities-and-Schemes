# The Petition of Grievances

Bolstered by [last week's "Silvershield's Foreign Thugs" pamphlet flood](../../activity/week_5/the-propaganda-campaign.md) and the public mood after [the Council's warning shot at the Basilisk Gate](../../../council-of-four/activity/week_6/the-warning-shot.md), the Peerage of Blood formally presents a **Petition of Grievances** at the [High Hall](../../../../30-Places/sword-coast/baldurs-gate/upper-city/the-wide.md) steps. The document, drafted by [Lord Corin Durinbold's](../../people/corin-durinbold.md) inner circle and signed by every patriar willing to be on record, demands the immediate expulsion of the Sembian "foreign mercenary army" from the city limits and cites pre-Crisis statutes forbidding standing armies not commanded by a Duke.

The petition is theatre and Durinbold knows it — the Council will not honor it, the Archduke will laugh — but it exists *on paper* now, witnessed and read in the chamber. That paper trail is what the Peerage needs: a publicly recorded protest establishing legal grounds for civic resistance later, if the Sembians fire on Baldurians a second time. Coupled with [Durinbold's "Silver Fist" Officers' Mess that same evening](../../../patriar-heritage-society/activity/week_6/the-officers-mess.md), the message is dual — the Peerage of Blood is the lawful conscience of the city in daylight, and the buyer of Fist enlisted ranks by candlelight.

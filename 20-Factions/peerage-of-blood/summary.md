# Peerage of Blood

The "old money" house of the Baldurian [Parliament of Peers](../parliament-of-peers/summary.md), representing the city's ancient patriar families fighting to preserve their traditional rights against the rising tide of plutocracy. Led from the front by [Lord Corin Durinbold](people/corin-durinbold.md).

## Ideology

Baldur's Gate has a soul, etched in the stones of its manors and flowing in the blood of its founding families. The patriars are its memory and its guardians. Coin can be spent, power can be lost, but blood is eternal — and so they act as a conservative brake on the radical, profit-driven ambitions of the new elite.

## Membership

Fifty hereditary seats, occupied by the oldest and wealthiest patriar families: **Durinbold, [Jannath](associations/house-jannath.md), [Hlath](associations/house-hlath.md), Oberon**, and others. The bloc is unofficially organized through the [Patriar Heritage Society](../patriar-heritage-society/summary.md). Eligibility is restricted to patriar lineage, but since the **1497 DR reformation**, all confirmed citizens within the walls now vote to fill the seats — tying patriar interests to the common Baldurian for the first time.

## Methods

- Acts as a unified voting bloc in the Parliament, often opposing measures from the [Peerage of Coin](../peerage-of-coin/summary.md).
- Wields immense social and cultural influence from private salons in Upper City manors.
- Recently blocked the Amnian Kontor's "New Kontor Wayleave" proposal — a rare victory for the old guard.
- The fall of [Hlath Estate](../) into Amnian foreclosure (and Durinbold's failed sponsorship of the dispossessed family) exposed the bloc's fragility against well-resourced Kontor pressure.

## Hooks and Relationships

**Enemies.** Primary political opponents of the [Peerage of Coin](../peerage-of-coin/summary.md), specifically the Foreign Kontors and Merchant Consortiums. They view the current [Council of Four](../council-of-four/summary.md), dominated by new money, with contempt. They are the primary patrons of the [Flaming Fist](../flaming-fist/summary.md), seeing it as a symbol of the old order — and were notably angered when Archduke Silvershield deployed [the Watch](../watch/summary.md) on private logging routes, encroaching on Fist jurisdiction.

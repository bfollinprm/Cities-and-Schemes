# Eric

A minor patriarch of one of Baldur's Gate's lesser Upper City houses, currently incarcerated at the [Hellrider stockade in New Elturel](../../../30-Places/sword-coast/baldurs-gate/outer-city/new-elturel.md). Eric is a **prisoner**, not a Hellrider asset — his captors are simply the only neutral authority who would still hold him after the warrant was filed against him.

## Why he is in the stockade

Eric claims his brothers framed him for embezzlement and for stealing from the High Watcher's coffers, in order to cut him out of the family inheritance after their father's death. He is not asking to be exonerated quickly. He is asking to be left alone, fed, and given books.

## What he has worked out

From the books his Elturian-refugee visitors have brought him over the past month, Eric has independently reconstructed two threads worth more than his trial:

- **The cult of [Bhaal](../../cult-of-the-returning-lord/summary.md) is binding souls via infernal-style legal contracts** — he calls it "the geometry of debt," after a margin-note in one of his books. This anticipates [Singa](../../takers-of-the-tithe/people/singa.md)'s working of the Tithe Ledger that the party will encounter on Dusthawk Hill.
- **The [Zhentarim](../../zhentarim/summary.md) are using the sewers to steal city tax records.** This anticipates [Roah Moonglow's Counting House infiltration](../../zhentarim/activity/week_5/the-financial-front.md) and [the Ledger Logic strike](../../zhentarim/activity/week_6/the-ledger-logic.md).

He has no proof. He has only a cell, the books, and time.

## Offer to the party

Eric has [offered the party future access](../../../50-Sessions/session_25/player_summary.md) to the Upper City *or* to Candlekeep, in exchange for help proving his brothers framed him. He is low-maintenance, polite, and *intellectually* the most useful prisoner in the stockade — but he has no leverage in the city today. Cashing him in is a mid-game investment.

# Chasing Ghosts

[Master Theronius Goldhand's](../../people/theronius-goldhand.md) [open underworld bounty for the source of the party's 6,000 gp](../../../wc-goldsmiths-jewelers/activity/week_4/a-quiet-bounty.md) goes exactly where bounties go in Baldur's Gate: into the hands of *every confidence artist in the Lower City*. Forged ledgers arrive at the [Counting House](../../../../30-Places/sword-coast/baldurs-gate/lower-city/counting-house.md) in three hands. A "Tethyrian moneylender" with a borrowed signet collects a fifty-gold consultation fee and is never seen again. A Brampton fence sells the Goldsmiths the *same* manufactured tip three times under different names. The week ends with the Goldsmiths' coffers visibly lighter and not one verifiable lead on the gold's origin. Goldhand calls the search off in disgust at a Counting House dinner — and, drinks in him, *publicly* berates two senior banking peers for failing to identify the source through their own private books. Both peers walk out. **The Goldsmiths have lost coin, time, and several key banking relationships in a single week**, and the actual source — Mad Meggan's shell-account chain — remains intact and untraceable.

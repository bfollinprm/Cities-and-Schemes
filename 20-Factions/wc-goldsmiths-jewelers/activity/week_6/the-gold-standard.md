# The Gold Standard

To stop the bleeding after [last week's failed party-bounty fiasco](../../activity/week_5/chasing-ghosts.md), [Master Theronius Goldhand](../../people/theronius-goldhand.md) takes the most public possible step: he announces a **public audit of the guild's own vaults** at the [Counting House](../../../../30-Places/sword-coast/baldurs-gate/lower-city/counting-house.md). The biggest banking houses, the senior Coster auditors, and a Sembian-funded outside accountant are invited to witness the count of the Goldsmiths' working reserves. Goldhand walks the auditors through the strongroom personally, opens every coin chest in their presence, and stands silent while they count.

The reserves are real. Goldhand has been bleeding cash on hired-bounty failures and on [the canary automaton commission for Lord Durinbold](../../activity/week_1/create-a-masterwork-for-a-patriar.md), but the guild's structural solvency is not in danger — the working-capital pool is whole. The audit confirms it. By Tendays' end the rumor that the Goldsmiths were on the brink of bankruptcy is publicly debunked, the city's bankers are reassured, and Goldhand has restored confidence in the guild's senior position. **The price was a private dressing-down by his peers** — the audit-as-PR-stunt cost him political capital with the conservative Goldsmith partners — but the guild's market position is stable for the duration of the siege.

# An Appeal to the Marshal

With the [multi-guild emergency petition](../activity/week_2/arming-the-inevitable.md) stalled in the gridlocked [Parliament](../../../parliament-of-peers/activity/week_3/vote-of-no-confidence.md), [Master Tor-keg Silverhand](../../people/tor-keg-silverhand.md) takes the appeal directly to [Marshal Flint](../../../flaming-fist/people/valerius-flint.md) at [Wyrm's Rock Fortress](../../../../30-Places/sword-coast/baldurs-gate/outer-city/wyrms-rock.md), pitching it soldier-to-soldier: the [Basilisk Gate siege](../../../free-traders-of-the-outskirts/activity/week_3/press-the-advantage.md) is starving the Forge of the Nine of iron, and the Fist's own armouries will run short before the harvest. Flint receives him cordially, pours wine, and gives him nothing he can take back to the Hall — Flint will not spend Fist credibility breaking the blockade after [refusing the Archduke's order to do exactly that](../../../council-of-four/activity/week_2/contain-the-crises.md), and Tor-keg knows it before he leaves. The Smiths' Company gains a private acknowledgment from the Marshal of the supply crisis and absolutely no movement on it. Tor-keg files the conversation as a write-off and starts pricing alternate iron supply routes — quietly, through [Dammon at the Forge of the Nine](../../people/dammon.md).

# The Forge Fires

Using the smuggled raw iron from [last tenday's wagon-train past the Basilisk Gate](../../activity/week_6/the-iron-smuggle.md), [Master Tor-keg Silverhand](../../people/tor-keg-silverhand.md) starts the Smiths' forges burning **day and night** under Basilisk Gate Checkpoint's southern arch. The output is **mass-produced low-quality weapons** — short swords, hatchets, knife-blades stamped flat from sheet-iron — sold to *whichever faction pays first*, no questions, no allegiance.

By the fourth day the forges have cleared eight hundred basic blades into the city's grey market. The Free Traders buy a hundred for the Outer City militia; the Plague of Remembrance buys two hundred for [the Refugee Riot operation](../../../plague-of-remembrance/activity/week_7/the-refugee-riot.md); the Iron Throne buys three hundred for resale to *both* sides; the Knights of the Shield buy fifty for their Manorborn riot-suppression detail; the rest move through unrecorded back-doors. **Silverhand is selling cheap death to anyone with coin.** [Dammon's bespoke forge](../../people/dammon.md), inside the same guild but with very different ethics, refuses to participate. The schism between the two senior smiths becomes an open guildhall argument by week's end.

When the party visits [Dammon's forge mid-week](../../../../50-Sessions/session_28/player_summary.md), Dammon outfits Bror with a new pair of unarmed-strike gauntlets and confirms Spark's cloak was forged from the discarded infernal iron of *Karlach's original heart* — the Foundry Smiths' work, not Silverhand's. *The party is not equipped from the cheap-blade line.*

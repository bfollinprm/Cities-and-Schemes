# An Appeal to the Old Guard

[Master Tor-keg Silverhand](../../people/tor-keg-silverhand.md), turned away from [Marshal Flint's](../../../flaming-fist/people/valerius-flint.md) command tent after [last week's "astral bargain" with K'laar's githyanki was contemptuously refused](../../../wc-smiths-armorers/activity/week_4/an-astral-bargain.md), and watching [the Free Trader-Hellrider blockade harden under the Archduke's ultimatum](../../../free-traders-of-the-outskirts/activity/week_5/heed-the-ultimatum.md), petitions [Lord Corin Durinbold](../../../peerage-of-blood/people/corin-durinbold.md) at [Durinbold Manor](../../../../30-Places/sword-coast/baldurs-gate/upper-city/manorborn.md) for an exemption from the Basilisk Gate blockade for the Smiths' raw-material caravans. Tor-keg arrives the day after [Borin Stonehand was publicly dismissed](../../../wc-masons-sculptors/activity/week_5/an-alliance-of-stone-and-blood.md) at the same gate; the contrast is calculated. He brings tribute — a forged ceremonial dagger of Durinbold lineage-stamped steel — and an offer: in exchange for caravan exemption, Smiths' guild output to the patriar bloc at preferential rate. Durinbold takes the dagger. He does not commit. He listens, says he will think on it, and walks Tor-keg back to the door himself — a far gentler dismissal than Stonehand received. Tor-keg leaves uncertain whether he has just been heard or merely *flattered*. **The Smiths' Guild has not yet been refused, and this week that is the best news the Worshipful Companies have had.**

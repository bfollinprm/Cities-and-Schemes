# Find a Scapegoat

To bury [the internal inquisition opened last week](../week_2/internal-inquisition.md) into a recent theft of dangerous reagents from the Company's own stores — the trail of which leads uncomfortably close to the [Plague-Father's destroyed lab](../../../party/activity/week_1/destroy-the-plague-fathers-laboratory.md) supplier — [Master Phineas Droog](../../people/phineas-droog.md) names a recently fired apprentice as the thief and posts a public bounty for his capture from [Crimson Draughts](../../summary.md) in the Lower City. The scapegoat is a junior alchemist Droog dismissed three months ago for unrelated reasons. The bounty is plausible, the paper trail is forged, and the internal investigation is closed by week's end on the strength of a single named suspect. Nobody loyal to Droog asks the question of why an apprentice would have known where the rare reagents were kept. [The Purified](../../../purified/activity/week_3/analyze-the-cure.md) will, of course, take the published bounty as further evidence the antitoxin distribution is corrupt. The Company sleeps better; the apprentice does not.

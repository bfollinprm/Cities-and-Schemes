# The Antidote Monopoly

[Master Phineas Droog](../../people/phineas-droog.md) — having confirmed [the Zhentarim signature in last tenday's poisoned ale](../../activity/week_6/the-poison-analysis.md) — formalizes a **selective-supply contract** with [Archduke Silvershield's](../../../western-gate-trading-company/people/lyra-silvershield.md) Sembian mercenary command: every doseable antidote, antitoxin, and purgative in the [Crimson Draughts](../../../../30-Places/sword-coast/baldurs-gate/lower-city/brampton.md) inventory is reserved exclusively for Sembian quartermasters at a five-times markup.

The Free Traders are explicitly cut out. So are the Hellriders, the Plowman regulars, the Eastway breadlines, every Outer City temple soup-line, and the New Elturel field-clinic — anyone who might need an antitoxin against the *next* Zhentarim adulteration is told to apply through Sembian command. By the fifth day the Free Traders have one death from a mild poisoning case that the Crimson Draughts shop refused to treat. [The Vintners' Dry Riot](../../../wc-vintners-brewers/activity/week_7/the-dry-riot.md) makes Droog's selective supply a story of literal life-and-death across the Outer City. Droog is making *enormous* money. The Outer City will remember.

When the party visits Crimson Draughts mid-week to ask about a route into Tumbledown, Droog provides them with a guild coin and a name — [Garrett "Grit" Fallow](../../../guild/people/garrett-grit-fallow.md), a Guild-aligned gravedigger — but does not relax his antidote restrictions for them, even after Kaelan's appeal. The party leaves carrying the coin but no medicine.

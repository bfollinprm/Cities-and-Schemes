# The Prison Visit

[Calliope](../../people/calliope.md), needing to control the testimony of the Society activists [taken on the Eastway barracks slogan-paint raid last week](../../../society-of-baldurian-integrity/activity/week_4/the-writing-on-the-wall.md), arrives at [Heapside Prison](../../../../30-Places/sword-coast/baldurs-gate/lower-city/heapside.md) under cover of being a "concerned cousin" — a fictitious Bloomridge family name, a bonnet, a basket of mended stockings. The prison clerk processes her into the visitors' bench without comment; the Watch is short-staffed [after the Plea for Reinforcements failed](../../../watch/activity/week_5/the-plea-for-reinforcements.md). Calliope sits across the table from the captured Society members, chats about an aunt's recipe for elderberry preserve, and embeds the cover story word by careful word: *they were drunken vandals paid by an unnamed Outer City merchant*; the Society does not exist; their slogans were copied from a tavern wall; they should plead guilty to public disorder and accept the fine. The captives nod, eat the elderberry preserve, repeat the cover story back to her, and adopt it. The Society's existence and ideology survive the magistrate's interview the next morning. Calliope leaves the prison with her bonnet still on and the Society's secret intact. **The captured cell will hang for vandalism, not for sedition.**

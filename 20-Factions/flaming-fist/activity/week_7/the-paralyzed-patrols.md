# The Paralyzed Patrols

[Marshal Valerius Flint](../../people/valerius-flint.md) tries to organize a coordinated Fist raid against [the Free Trader blockade](../../../free-traders-of-the-outskirts/summary.md) at Basilisk Gate, intending to break the barricade before the [Druid vines](../../../circle-of-the-inner-grove/activity/week_7/vine-barricades.md) thicken. The order goes out at first light. By midday the operation is dead.

Three of his line officers — bought a tenday earlier at [Lord Durinbold's Officers' Mess](../../../patriar-heritage-society/activity/week_6/the-officers-mess.md) — drag their feet through every step of mobilization: the wagon-quartermaster cannot find requisition forms; the supply tents are unaccountably understaffed; the night sergeant mis-reads the marching order. The raiding column never leaves the barracks. Flint's [own internal smuggling crackdown is already paralyzed](../../activity/week_6/the-officers-dilemma.md); now his external operations are too. **The Fist is functionally a parade-ground unit.** Junior officers begin asking openly whether the Marshal still has command authority at all.

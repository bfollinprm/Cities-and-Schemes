# The Gutter Prayer

Scattered and humiliated by [last week's catastrophic sanctum-relocation](../../activity/week_5/relocate-the-sanctum.md), the remaining Bhaalist cultists retreat deep into the [Undercity sewers](../../../../30-Places/sword-coast/baldurs-gate/undercity/events.md). Their idols are gone, their consecrated knife-altars are gone, and their working numbers are down to a dozen. To regain Bhaal's favor, the survivors begin abducting **the city's forgotten** — beggars, sewer jacks, sluiceway scavengers, the kind of people whose disappearances make no ripple — and dragging them down the Undercity tunnels for a crude mass sacrifice. The intent is to summon a **Slayer**: an avatar of Bhaal in the form of a flesh-warped killing machine, to lead them out of the darkness.

The ritual is botched. The cell does not have an officiant of sufficient rank — [the Plague-Father is dead](../../../../50-Sessions/session_5/README.md), the Bhaal Celebrant is in hiding — and on the seventh victim the chant misfires. The blood does not call a Slayer. It calls *something* — a chittering, low, four-legged shadow that scatters the cultists into the deeper sewers and is itself gone before any of them can come back to confirm what it was. Three cultists are missing by morning. **The Bhaalists have called a thing they cannot control and lost it loose in the Undercity.** [The Gravekeepers' corpse caravan](../../../gravekeepers/activity/week_6/the-corpse-caravan.md) moving through Tumbledown gives Old Man Hemlock a new and more frightening reason to move quickly.

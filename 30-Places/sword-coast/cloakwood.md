# The Cloakwood

A vast and ancient forest extending south of Baldur's Gate and inland into the foothills of the Cloud Peaks. Towering trees, tangled undergrowth, hidden ruins, ancient secrets. Long-time home of powerful fey, secretive druid circles, and a variety of monstrous beasts.

For the local face — Western Gate logging, Circle of the Inner Grove war — see [baldurs-gate/hinterlands/cloakwood/](baldurs-gate/hinterlands/cloakwood/README.md).

## Extent

Larger than any single polity's writ. Western edge fronts the Sword Coast and Baldur's Gate's hinterland; southern edge bleeds into the lands north of Amn; eastern reaches roll up against the Cloud Peaks foothills. The forest is too large to be effectively patrolled or claimed; outside the Western Gate's recent saw-roads, it is essentially trackless.

## Ecology

- **Old growth.** The dominant character is ancient temperate forest — immense trees, high canopy, dim ground-level light, dense undergrowth where the canopy thins.
- **Fey-real.** The Cloakwood is one of the regions on the Sword Coast where fey activity is direct rather than oblique. The dossier reports fey acting as combatants in the current logging war, not just as folktale presences. See the [Green Masquerade](../../10-Setting/cosmology/fey-courts/green-masquerade.md) for the wider fey-court context.
- **Druidic.** Multiple circles operate in the wood. The radical [Circle of the Inner Grove](../../20-Factions/circle-of-the-inner-grove/summary.md) is the dossier's headline name; the broader Emerald Enclave maintains a moderate presence and has tried (unsuccessfully) to broker peace.

## Why it matters at macro-region scale

- **Strategic resource.** The Cloakwood is the timber reserve of the entire Sword Coast region. Reconstruction at Baldur's Gate has driven harvest beyond historical precedent, but the wood also supplies — quietly — Beregost, Nashkel, Amn, and parts of the Western Heartlands. Anyone who controls Cloakwood timber controls the rebuild of the region.
- **Ecological keystone.** The Cloakwood feeds the upper tributaries of the Chionthar; what happens to its watershed shows up downstream within a season.
- **Fey-political.** The fey of the Cloakwood are entering open war with industrial human polities. This is *novel* — historically, Cloakwood fey have been trickster-figures, not combatants. The shift is one of the post-Crisis decade's most significant unmeasured changes.

## What's in it

- Saw-roads and Western Gate logging camps along the western edge (see local-face file).
- Circle of the Inner Grove and Emerald Enclave groves throughout.
- Old ruins in the deep wood — periodically the target of adventuring expeditions sponsored by Beregost-based costers.
- Fey courts and waysites whose locations are not stable in the way mortal sites are.

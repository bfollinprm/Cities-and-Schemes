# Southern Ward — Character

The Southern Ward is the city's **overland gateway**. Caravans from the south enter here; goods are warehoused, draft-animals stabled, axles repaired, gear smithed; and the laborers who keep the trade moving live in dense tenement blocks throughout the ward. Tenement rentals run **12 gp/month** for a single cramped room in a crowded building.

## Atmosphere

Smoke and forge-soot, draft-animal smell, the clatter of carts at all hours. The ward is busy without being chaotic in the Trades Ward way — its rhythm is the caravan schedule, not the 24-hour shop. Day-rations for the poor (thin gruel, stale bread, watery stew) cost **6 cp**; an unskilled laborer earns **2 sp/day** at the docks or the Southern Ward hiring grounds — barely a subsistence wage.

## The economic squeeze

Of all the working wards, the Southern Ward is where the dossier's **"artisan drain"** to Baldur's Gate is most directly visible: stable masters and forgemen losing apprentices, caravan crews hearing about better wages on the southern route. The Trades Ward feels the drain in dues; the Southern Ward feels it in conversations at the gate.

## Faction layer

The **Doom Raiders** ([Zhentarim](../../../../20-Factions/zhentarim/summary.md)) operate caravan-side smuggling and money-lending here, with Davil Starsong's people quietly seeded into routes that pass through Baldur's Gate. The Southern Ward is also a recruitment ground for **Manshoon's** rougher faction — disenchanted laborers and unemployed caravan guards are easy to weaponize. The **Watch** patrols visibly but is over-stretched; their priority is the gates and the warehouse district, not the tenements.

## Population pressure

The Southern Ward sits next to the Field Ward (the unincorporated extramural slum) and absorbs steady spillover from it: people who finally scrape together rent for a tenement room and move out of the mud. It also bleeds people in the other direction when rents rise. The dossier identifies the lower wards collectively — Southern, Dock, Field — as the population reservoir that will eventually fuel a populist movement against the Sea Ward and the Lords.

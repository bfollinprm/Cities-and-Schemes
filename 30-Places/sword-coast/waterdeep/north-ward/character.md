# North Ward — Character

The North Ward is **new money's territory**. Where the Sea Ward exhibits inherited confidence, the North Ward exhibits ambition. Its architecture is more ostentatious — newer and louder than the cliff-top villas — a self-conscious advertisement of wealth that recently arrived and intends to stay.

## Who lives here

**Lesser nobles** (Court tier or lower), fabulously wealthy non-noble merchants, and retired adventurers with gold to spend and reputations to launder. Many of these families bought their noble titles during **Dagult Neverember's** tenure as Open Lord — a practice he industrialized and which the dossier calls out as the proximate cause of the *new money / old money* schism that runs through the Game.

## The pro-plutocracy bloc

The North Ward is the campaign's geographic concentration of the **pro-plutocracy** ideology: the position that **wealth is the only true measure of power** and that hereditary status is decoration. This is in direct opposition to the **pro-aristocracy** Sea Ward consensus that power is a matter of birthright. The two wards do not openly fight; they snipe at each other through the Great Game.

## Atmosphere

On the surface: quiet, residential, well-paved streets. Underneath: the salons and private clubs are where Court-tier houses scheme to **usurp the Throne**. A North Ward dinner-party invitation is more functionally political than a Sea Ward one — Sea Ward attendance is a status confirmation; North Ward attendance is a recruitment pitch.

A wealthy suite at a North Ward inn runs **8 gp/night**; a noble villa here, like in Sea Ward, runs **20,000 gp+**. The retail vocabulary skews aspirational: **Bhephel's Bottles** sells fine wines from **25 gp** into the hundreds for rare vintages.

## Where the agents go

Both the [Harpers](../../../../20-Factions/harpers/summary.md) and [Manshoon's Zhentarim](../../../../20-Factions/zhentarim/summary.md) work the North Ward intensely. New-money houses are easier to blackmail than old-money ones — fewer institutional protections, more leverage points (debt, scandal, status anxiety) — and the dossier names two compromised Masked Lords (**Corylus Thann**, **Jelenn Urmbrusk**) whose houses sit in the Court tier the North Ward dominates.

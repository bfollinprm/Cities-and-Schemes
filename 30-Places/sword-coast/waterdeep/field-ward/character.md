# Field Ward — Character

The Field Ward is **the city's failure made visible**. It sits in the former killing fields between the northern walls — a no-man's-land that was never zoned, never serviced, never officially admitted to exist. It grew anyway, out of refugee tents and the camps of citizens who could not pay the city's taxes and so were pushed outside its mercies.

## What it actually is

A muddy, lawless mess. **No sewer system.** Disease is endemic, sanitation nonexistent. Shelter ranges from tents to stacked planks to abandoned siege carts. Food and water come in from the gates by bargaining; the price is whatever the gate-traders decide it is. The flophouse cots in the Dock Ward (**8 cp/night**) are luxury by comparison.

## Who lives here

- **Refugees** displaced by the wars and crises of the past decade — Elturians, river-folk, displaced Trollclaws people.
- **Disenfranchised citizens** unable to pay the city's poll-style taxes.
- **The exiled** — people who have been formally pushed out of guild membership, noble employ, or Watch protection.

## Who does not police here

- **The Watch does not enter the Field Ward.** Patrols stop at the gates.
- **The Guard watches from the walls** and intervenes only at the gates, only to protect traffic.
- **The Guilds do not serve it.** No guild-licensed mason, smith, or chandler works in the Field Ward; no guild-sealed deed is recognized within it.

The vacuum is filled by criminal predators, ad-hoc protection rackets, and whatever desperate community structures the residents themselves can build.

## Why the dossier names it the powder keg

The dossier's central political prediction is that Waterdeep's next great crisis will not come from outside — not an invasion, not a cabal in the Masked Lords — but from this ward. Concentrating a large, disenfranchised, and desperate population on the doorstep of a city defined by obscene wealth is a recipe for social catastrophe. **A spark — a food shortage, a charismatic demagogue, a particularly brutal Guard incident at the gates — could ignite this ward into open rebellion.** The Lords ignore it. The dossier judges that ignorance willful and dangerous.

## Faction layer

No faction in `faction-quests.xlsx` formally serves the Field Ward. Hellriders-style refugee-organizing analogues from Baldur's Gate (see [Hellriders of New Elturel](../../../../20-Factions/hellriders-of-new-elturel/summary.md) for the closest precedent) might emerge here. As-yet-unwritten populist leaders are exactly the people the dossier expects to rise.

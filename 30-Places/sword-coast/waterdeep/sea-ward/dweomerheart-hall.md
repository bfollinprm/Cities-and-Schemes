# Dweomerheart Hall

An opulent manor-house in Waterdeep's **Sea Ward**, owned by an anonymous Green Masquerade benefactor and lent to the society for grand revels. Best known as the venue of the **Autumnal Ball of 1501 DR** — the night a Far Realm intrusion broke through the wards mid-dance and left the [Green Masquerade](../../../../10-Setting/cosmology/fey-courts/green-masquerade.md) permanently changed.

## Layout (as observed during the ball)

- A **grand ballroom** on the third floor, draped in living ivy and faerie lights; an enchanted painted ceiling of twilight sky; large gilded **mirrors** lining the walls; floor-to-ceiling stained-glass windows (one depicting **[Lathander's](../../../../10-Setting/cosmology/deities/lathander.md) sunrise** — the one the eladrin knight came through).
- **Side halls** off the ballroom toward servants' corridors — the first attack on a maid happened here.
- Multiple **crystal chandeliers** that can be lit to noon-bright by an arcanist (a property the Masquerade is now grateful for).

## Magical character

The hall holds enchantments that *invite* fey resonance — drifting Feywild maple leaves that never touch ground, a violin quartet whose tune harmonizes with the lantern-flicker. After the incursion, surviving petals from the protective gale-of-blossoms still turn up in corners of the hall and *whisper in Sylvan* if handled.

The mirrors are particularly suspect: during the breach they showed a forest of leafless trees under a bruised purple sky, with eyes glinting between them. Whether that view is permanent property of the glass or a one-night phenomenon is unclear.

## Wards (post-incident)

After the [Night of the Howling Masks](../../../../40-Timeline/history.md), the Masquerade vowed never to host a gathering here again without:

- Triple-layer wards.
- Cold-iron bell screening of every guest.
- True-sight at the door.

Whether the hall is still in service to the society as of 1502 DR is not recorded in the dossier.

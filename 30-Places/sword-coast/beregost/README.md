# Beregost

Town on the Trade Way a few days' journey south of Baldur's Gate. Once a thriving market for skilled craftsmen and bustling local commerce. Now a town in clear decline — politically weak, economically sidelined, socially strained.

## What's here

- [character.md](character.md) — feel of the town today.

## Why it matters

Three forces have converged on Beregost over the last decade:

1. **Trade diversion.** The [Chionthar Consortium](../../../20-Factions/chionthar-consortium/summary.md)'s fortified river trading posts have captured the trade that used to flow through the town's markets. Beregost's once-famous craftsmen have lost their customers.
2. **Refugee influx.** Refugees from Elturel and the surrounding countryside have overwhelmed the town's resources, creating sharp social tension between locals and newcomers.
3. **Captured politics.** The town's mayor — described in the dossier as "well-meaning but ineffectual" — is a Consortium puppet. The town council is fractious and ineffective; the temple of Lathander is a hollow shell.

The most likely trajectory is that Beregost becomes a *company town* — an administrative appendage of the Chionthar Consortium — or slides further into poverty and decay. There is no obvious local force capable of reversing either.

## Players in Beregost

- [Chionthar Consortium](../../../20-Factions/chionthar-consortium/summary.md) — effective political and economic power; quiet patron of the mayor.
- Local town militia — a "glorified police force," per the dossier; struggles to keep peace between locals and refugees.
- Temple of Lathander — moral institution in name; empty pews in practice.
- [Remnant Costers (Iron Throne)](../../../20-Factions/iron-throne/summary.md) — the **High Moon Coster** in particular has Beregost ties; per `faction-quests.xlsx`, the Oberon family scrape together coin to sponsor adventuring parties for Cloakwood ruins.

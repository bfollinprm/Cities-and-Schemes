# Character

Walk into Beregost and the first thing you see is empty workshops with their fronts painted over. The street that used to be the smiths' row is half boarded up. The market square at midday holds a third the people it did a decade ago, and most of those people are visibly poor.

## The town's mood

Quiet desperation. The dossier's phrase is fair — "a people who have lost their way." Locals are polite, even friendly, but the warmth is brittle. Refugees from Elturel and the depopulated villages around make up perhaps a quarter of the population now; the locals resent them, the refugees resent the locals, and both resent the Consortium men in the inn.

## The mayor

A well-meaning, ineffectual man kept comfortable by Consortium quiet money. He genuinely wants Beregost to recover; he genuinely cannot say no to Master Joric the Stout's agents. He is *not* corrupt in the strong sense — he simply believes the Consortium is the only force that can save the town, and acts accordingly.

## The council

A hotbed of factionalism. Old craft guilds, refugee-aid voices, Consortium-aligned merchants, and a small principled minority who understand what's happening but cannot mobilize against it. Most council meetings end without decision.

## The temple

The temple of Lathander used to be the town's moral center. The current priest is competent but tired. Sermons are well-attended on holy days and otherwise empty. The temple still runs charity work for refugees and the indigent, but its budget is thin and its donors are mostly Consortium men buying public reputation.

## The inns

A handful of inns survive. The largest is unofficially the Consortium's Beregost office. Smaller inns serve locals and the few independent merchants who still pass through. The atmosphere in any of them is low-energy — gossip is the main amusement, and most of the gossip is bad news.

## Useful to whom

- **Investigators** chasing Consortium dealings — Beregost is where the Consortium is least subtle.
- **Adventurers** willing to take Coster work into the Cloakwood — see the [High Moon Coster](../../../20-Factions/high-moon-coster/summary.md) presence.
- **Refugee aid workers** — there is real work here for anyone willing to do it.

## Avoid

- Picking a fight with a Consortium agent. The mayor will not back you.
- Offering protection to a local craftsman *publicly* — it will end his Consortium contracts.

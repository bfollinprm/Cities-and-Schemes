# Character

Nashkel is small, well-kept, and visibly more prosperous than Beregost up the road. The improvement is recent and the cause is obvious to anyone who looks: there is fresh Amnian coin in the town's roadworks, market stalls, and town watch.

## The mayor

A pragmatic man. He receives Consortium agents and Amnian envoys in the same week, in the same office, accepts their gifts without committing, and answers correspondence in deliberate ambiguity. His council is small enough that he can keep its members aligned by judicious sharing of the inflow. The dossier's verdict is that he plays the game well in the short term but cannot win it long-term.

## Amnian presence

Increased and visible. Soldiers in Amnian colors are stationed at the town's southern edge — formally a "courtesy garrison," practically a quiet annexation prep. Amnian agents recruit among local merchants and craftsmen, offering trade contracts and protection. The recruiting works because the Consortium's squeeze has created the demand.

## Baldurian / Consortium presence

Quieter than in Beregost, but real. Consortium representatives keep an office in Nashkel and continue to do business — but they negotiate now from weakness. The local market remembers Consortium high-handedness and finds the Amnian alternative more palatable. The Consortium's response so far has been to *increase* the squeeze, which the dossier judges counterproductive.

## On the road

Trade Way through Nashkel is heavier than the stretch north of it. Caravan crews stop here for resupply and meet Amnian customs agents who are *polite but exact*. The customs work is real — Amn collects tariffs that the squeezed Consortium has to pay or detour around. There are also smugglers — the Guild's River-Rats reach this far south, and the southern stretch of the Trade Way is where smuggler and outlaw activity is densest.

## The mood

Cautiously optimistic at street level. People feel things are getting *less bad*. The mayor is popular for now. The optimism is real but the dossier reads it as ignorance of what Amn will eventually demand in return for the courtesy garrison.

## Useful to whom

- **Amnian agents** — Nashkel is the staging ground.
- **Smugglers** with Guild ties — the southern Trade Way and Chionthar tributaries make Nashkel a useful node.
- **Independents fleeing Consortium pressure** — Nashkel still buys from independent suppliers.

## Avoid

- Open Consortium-livery work in town. The mayor will not help you if locals decide to make a point.
- Assuming the courtesy garrison is courtesy.

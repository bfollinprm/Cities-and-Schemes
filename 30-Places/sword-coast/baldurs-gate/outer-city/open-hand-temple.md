# Open Hand Temple

Rivington's plain stone hall to Ilmater, the Crying God of patient endurance. Bread is given to anyone who asks; a bed is granted to anyone who needs one; questions are not asked of the recently arrived. The notice board outside is the best in Rivington for honest work cheaply found.

In the campaign's recent past, when the [Worshipful Company of Alchemists & Apothecaries](../../../../20-Factions/wc-alchemists-apothecaries/summary.md) voted to triple antitoxin prices during a [Lower City](../lower-city/README.md) Sewer Plague outbreak, an adventuring party diverted the supply to the Open Hand Temple — turning a price-gouging vote into the Company's first public scandal.

## Notable in this house

- **The bread queue.** Open from dawn; never refuses a stranger. The senior brother on the door remembers everyone he has fed.
- **The plague ward.** A low, whitewashed annex behind the chapel where the Open Hand has, in defiance of Apothecaries' guild policy, been quietly distributing low-cost antitoxin since the Sewer Plague vote.
- **The notice board.** Honest hire, honest rates. Caravan guarding, drover work, day-labour at the south-bank yards.

## Who you find here

Itinerant clergy of Ilmater, lay brothers and sisters, refugees and runaway servants, drovers between contracts, plague-clinic patients, and the occasional scrubbed-clean penitent of a more comfortable god.

## Etiquette and access

Speak softly. Eat what you are given. If you cannot pay, work the bread queue for a morning. The clergy do not preach; they simply outwait you.

## Pressures

The Apothecaries are quietly looking for an excuse to revoke the temple's clinic licence; the [Flaming Fist](../../../../20-Factions/flaming-fist/summary.md) treat the bread-queue as a refugee census they would rather have access to; and the temple's reputation as the place to take a Sewer Plague case has begun to draw attention from less Lawful-Good visitors as well.

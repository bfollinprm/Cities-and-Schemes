# Sorcerous Sundries

A four-storey tower of pale stone and warded glass, set among the workshops of [Heapside](heapside.md). Bespelled signs above the door change script for the reader — Common, Elven, Thorass, the trade pidgin of the harbour. Inside: the city's largest legitimate market for scrolls, components, and minor enchantments, and (one floor up) the registry where every public-practising spellcaster in Baldur's Gate is licensed.

In the wake of the Absolute Crisis, the shop's master was granted a new charter as the Worshipful Company of the [Sorcerous Sodality](../../../../20-Factions/wc-sorcerous-sodality/summary.md) — the regulator and toll-taker for the city's magical trade.

## Notable in this house

- **The shop floor.** Components, scrolls, foci, and minor enchanted goods at posted (and not always honest) prices. Adventurers settle most of their resupply here.
- **The licensing room.** Every Sodality member's slate hangs here; **Sodality Investigators** depart on patrol from this office. Unlicensed casting in the [Lower City](README.md) is, in practice, defined by who has not paid their fee.
- **The Sorcerous Vault.** Lorroakan's strongroom, two floors below the shop, where confiscated foci, dangerous components, and the Master's private working materials are kept under heavy ward. Closely guarded; access is by Lorroakan's word alone.

## Who you find here

Sodality clerks, licensed wizards renewing fees, apprentices fetching components, Investigators receiving a beat assignment, and the occasional patriar's factor placing a discreet order for something that should not appear on a household ledger.

## Etiquette and access

The shop floor is open. The licensing office demands your slate. The vault is not for visitors. Lorroakan dislikes warlock-pact arrangements on principle and is openly suspicious of any caster whose source he cannot inspect.

## Pressures

A new charter that not every wizard in the city accepts; the unlicensed casters of [Brampton](brampton.md) and the [Outer City](../outer-city/README.md) who would rather pay nothing than tithe; and the Vault itself, which would interest more than one party if its contents were ever catalogued.

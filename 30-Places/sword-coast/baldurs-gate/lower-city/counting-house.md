# The Counting House

The port's private bank and clearinghouse, sitting one street back from the [Gray Harbour](gray-harbour.md) piers. Factors queue with manifests under one arm and a bodyguard under the other; cargoes are valued, debts settled, and a quiet floor of ledger-clerks reconciles every Kontor's books before the day's tide turns.

## Notable in this house

- **The bond floor.** Day-of-arrival valuations and short-term lending against incoming cargoes. Rates set by the senior factors over a morning cup, and rarely revisited before noon.
- **The strongroom.** Three doors deep, two of them mage-warded; even the [Flaming Fist](../../../../20-Factions/flaming-fist/summary.md) request appointments to inspect.
- **The press benches.** A reserved gallery where the [Baldur's Mouth](../../../../20-Factions/baldurs-mouth/summary.md) stringers wait for the day's auction prints — the gazette's commodity columns are filed from here.

## Who you find here

Factors, brokers, ledger-clerks, sworn appraisers, hired guards, and the polite, patient fixers who arrive when an account has gone bad in a way the owners would prefer not to discuss in writing.

## Etiquette and access

No weapons past the foyer. No raised voices on the bond floor. Bring a sealed introduction or a Credential of Balduran if you intend to ask questions. The clerks remember faces.

## Pressures

The [Baldur's Mouth Interim Management Board](../../../../20-Factions/baldurs-mouth/people/the-interim-management-board.md) is leaning on the Counting House to fortify and "vet" wartime accounts — a quiet move toward the same lockdown logic that closed the rival presses.

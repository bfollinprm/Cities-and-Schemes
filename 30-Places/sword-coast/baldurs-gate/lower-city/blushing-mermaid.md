# The Blushing Mermaid

A barnacled tavern half-housed in the timbers of a beached hulk, sagging where the ribs no longer hold. Patronage runs from sailors to smugglers to the kind of broker who would rather meet you here than anywhere with a doorman. Fights are common; rumour is cheaper than the beer and twice as strong.

## Notable in this house

- **The back tables.** Curtained booths where deals are struck. The owner notices nothing if you don't make him.
- **The Iron Throne corner.** A handful of remaining [Iron Throne](../../../../20-Factions/iron-throne/summary.md) brokers keep a standing table here, meeting contacts who would not be welcome at their diminished [Upper City](../upper-city/README.md) manor.
- **The "ship's bell" auction.** Once a week, fenced cargo changes hands by lot in the back room. Anyone with two coins to clink and a face the regulars don't recognise is asked to drink elsewhere.

## Who you find here

Off-shift dockworkers, smugglers' runners, [Wharf Rats](../../../../20-Factions/wharf-rats/summary.md) crew, mercenary recruiters, [High Moon Coster](../../../../20-Factions/high-moon-coster/summary.md) caravan men putting deals together, and Iron Throne agents quietly running what is left of an empire.

## Etiquette and access

Don't ask the bartender for names. Don't sit at a curtained booth that isn't yours. Tip the bouncers in coppers — silver suggests guilt. Knives stay sheathed unless they are already drawn.

## Pressures

Fences using the back room for stolen Lantaner mechanical parts; Iron Throne meetings that the [Knights of the Shield](../../../../20-Factions/knights-of-the-shield/summary.md) would happily disrupt; a [Seven Suns](../../../../20-Factions/seven-suns/summary.md) recruiter trying to audition adventurers for a second silk run.

# Hamhock's Vault

A low, vaulted strongroom dug into the bedrock under the [Undercellar](the-undercellar.md), accessible by a stair from a side cellar most of the regulars don't know exists. Originally a smuggler's hold; presently in use as a holding cell.

By **week 7** of the campaign timeline, dm-notes commit to a fate for the [Baldur's Mouth](../../../../20-Factions/baldurs-mouth/summary.md) lead [Ettvard Needle](../../../../20-Factions/baldurs-mouth/people/ettvard-needle.md): kidnapped, and held here.

## Notable in this house

- **The cell.** A barred alcove, originally for storing untaxed wine in the long-cool dark; lately fitted with a cot, a chamber pot, and a chain.
- **The fence-counter.** A long oak slab where the Vault's owner — when not running a kidnapping — receives stolen goods from contacts upstairs.
- **The back stair.** A second exit through the sewer wall; flooded periodically and known to perhaps a dozen people.

## Who you find here

The Vault's owner (Hamhock himself, or his current successor); a rotating watch of bruisers; whichever guests the back room is currently entertaining against their will; the very occasional black-market buyer.

## Etiquette and access

You do not find the Vault. The Vault sends for you. The standing rules are: do not name the room aloud upstairs, do not bring a follower, do not ask twice.

## Pressures

A live kidnapping the [Baldur's Mouth](../../../../20-Factions/baldurs-mouth/summary.md) is paying through the nose to recover; an angry [Wharf Rats](../../../../20-Factions/wharf-rats/summary.md) sub-cell who consider the Vault on their turf; and the slow rot of the back-stair's masonry, which one bad winter will finish.

# Lower Chionthar

The stretch of the [River Chionthar](../README.md) from Baldur's Gate upstream into the hinterland. The river drains the Western Heartlands and exits at the city; this page treats only the section close enough that the city's industry shapes it.

## What's here

- [character.md](character.md) — what the river looks, smells, and feels like now.

## Why it matters

The river is a contested space — barge traffic, hidden backwaters used by smugglers, fishing villages on the banks. It is also the **most visible casualty** of the city's industrial reconstruction: pollution from tanneries, foundries, and slaughterhouses has turned the lower river into "a foul and stinking brew" with a "greasy film of industrial waste" on the surface. Fish are gone. Birds are gone. The fishing villages along the lower banks have been abandoned, their people forced into city slums and factories.

## Players on the river

- [Chionthar Consortium](../../../../../20-Factions/chionthar-consortium/summary.md) — barge traffic and warehousing; primary polluter (poorly maintained barges, alleged direct dumping at night).
- [Guild](../../../../../20-Factions/guild/summary.md) **River-Rats** — smuggle through the hidden waterways feeding the river, bypassing Consortium tolls and Hellrider checkpoints.
- Emerald Enclave and other druids — vocal in protest, ineffective in practice.
- Refugee fishers and dockworkers — displaced upstream and into the city; a key recruiting pool for both the [Wharf Rats](../../../../../20-Factions/wharf-rats/summary.md) and the [Followers of the Phoenix](../../../../../20-Factions/followers-of-the-phoenix/summary.md).

# Character

The dossier captures the change in a single image: where the forest used to be marked by the sound of axes and saws, it is now marked by the clash of steel and the screams of the dying.

## At the company camps

The logging camps along the forest edge are a microcosm of the new Baldur's Gate — desperate refugees, hardened mercenaries, ambitious company men, all driven by coin. The work is hard and the pay is low; for many it is the only work available. Camps are stockaded, with watch-posts on the saw-roads pushing inward.

A typical camp scene: a chow line of a hundred men, a quarter of them clearly Elturian; a foreman in Western Gate livery counting axe-handles; a company sergeant lecturing fresh hires about "what we don't talk about" (the casualty rate); a wagon at the gate loaded with logs and a tarpaulined cargo no one is supposed to look at (probably mercenary dead or a captured druid).

## On the saw-roads

The roads the company has cut into the forest are wide, recent, and badly drained. They are also the front line. Western Gate convoys move only in groups, with crossbow lookouts on every wagon. Ambushes come from the canopy, from rope-bridges between trees, from collapsed cuttings and sudden rockfalls. After an ambush, the company sends mercenaries on punitive sweeps; after a sweep, the druids retaliate against an isolated camp. The cycle does not end.

## Off the saw-roads

Once a traveler steps off the cuttings, the Cloakwood is what it has always been: ancient and unsettling. The trees are immense, the canopy heavy. The light drops to a green dusk by mid-afternoon. Sound travels strangely. Watchers are felt before they are seen. The fey are *real* in the Cloakwood, not folktale; the dossier reports them aiding the Circle as direct combatants now, not as oblique trickster-figures.

## What's available, what isn't

- **Mercenary work** is plentiful and well-paid. Survival rates are not what the recruiter quotes.
- **Druidic contact** is possible, but only by going in unarmed and slowly, and even then likely to be tested.
- **Solitary travel** is the worst option. The company does not distinguish between druids, fey, and unaffiliated travelers carrying the wrong rumor.
- **Old ruins** in the deep wood persist; remnant adventuring parties still get sponsored to find rumored artifacts there.

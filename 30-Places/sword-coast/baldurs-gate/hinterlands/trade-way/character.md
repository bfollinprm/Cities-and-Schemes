# Character

The Trade Way south is in worse repair than it ought to be — the dossier calls it "pocked with potholes and littered with the debris of a hundred petty conflicts." The inns are crowded, dirty, and unpleasant. The food is poor, the company is questionable. Nothing is at the level of the Coast Way's open extortion, but everything is being slowly hollowed out.

## In the inns

Roadside inns on the Trade Way are where the Chionthar Consortium does its quiet work. The dossier reports their agents are everywhere — pockets full of coin, tongues loose with cheap ale. They cut deals with local farmers and craftsmen for produce and goods at low prices, paying in coin while the local market is in silver-shortage. They lean on innkeepers to take their barge-shipped supplies instead of road-hauled. They ask quiet, persistent questions about who's hauling what.

Independent merchants in the same inns drink at separate tables. The line is unspoken but clear.

## On the road

A small merchant party can travel the local Trade Way without convoy if they accept a poor return. The road risk is bandits — typically refugees from Elturel or surrounding villages whose villages were squeezed out, now organized into small predatory bands. They are usually *desperate* rather than competent, which makes them dangerous to small parties and trivial to large ones.

The bigger risk is *delay*. The road is in poor shape, bridges are unmaintained, and the Consortium's barge competition means roadside services have decayed. A wagon-train that loses a wheel three days from the city may sit for a week before help comes.

## Refugees on the verge

Closer to Elturel, the road is increasingly lined with refugee camps and shanty clusters. The dossier names "the desperate and starving refugees who haunt the roadside" as a distinct danger — they will swarm a stopped wagon for food. Most Trade Way veterans simply don't stop in these stretches.

## Safe stops

- Beregost is a safe stop in the technical sense (walls, militia, inns) but a depressing one — see [Beregost](../../../beregost/README.md).
- Independent fortified inns exist but are increasingly rare; many have closed for lack of trade.

## Avoid

- Long stops in the Elturel-approach refugee stretch.
- Trusting a Consortium agent's offer of "joint shipping" — every veteran has a story.

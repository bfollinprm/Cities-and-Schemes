# Character

The Friendly Arm doesn't look like an inn. It looks like a small keep that happens to serve dinner. Curtain wall, tower, gatehouse with portcullis, a guard at the main door who actually checks weapons. The yard inside is large enough for a full caravan; the stables are doubled in size to handle convoy stops.

## Inside the common room

The common room is loud and varied. A Western Gate teamster table by the fire, drinking hard and tipping poorly. A pair of obvious Coast Way bandits-out-of-livery in a corner, paying well for not being asked questions. An older woman with a lute and too-good fingers — a Harper, probably, but no one says so. A scholar from Candlekeep on his way south. A trio of Sembian Kontor mercenaries on rotation. The proprietor moves through it all with the practiced ease of someone who has been keeping the wrong people from killing each other for decades.

Food is decent and the price is high. Rooms are scarce; convoys book ahead. Walk-ins sleep in the common room or the stables for a few coppers, with the understanding that the inn keeps its rooms for paying caravan crews and certain *unspecified* travelers.

## What the inn is for

- **Sleep behind walls** for one night out of the city.
- **Caravan staging** — a place to wait for the rest of your convoy or hire on to one.
- **Quiet messaging** — if you have business with the Harpers and you don't know how to find them in the city, this is where you ask.
- **Refugee transit** — small parties of dispossessed Elturians or Baldurian dissidents move north through the inn, in twos and threes, on Harper handlers.

## What the inn is *not*

- A neutral ground. The proprietor's loyalties are clear to anyone who pays attention; they extend a welcome to most paying travelers but will quietly refuse a room or a meal to known Zhentarim agents.
- A place to make trouble. Brawlers are removed by the staff with practiced violence.
- Cheap.

## Useful to whom

- Any party heading north on the Coast Way at the end of day one.
- Anyone trying to get a message to the Harpers without going through Baldur's Gate.
- Anyone moving someone *out* of the city quietly.

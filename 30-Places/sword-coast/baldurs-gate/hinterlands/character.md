# Character

What it feels like to leave Baldur's Gate by any gate is the same: the road is rougher than it should be, the inns more fortified, the caravans more heavily armed. The old assumption that a single authority kept the king's peace on the Coast Way and the Trade Way is gone, and everyone on the road knows it.

## The post-boom frontier

The reconstruction of Baldur's Gate has pulled labor, coin, and ambition out into the lands around it. Logging camps along the Cloakwood. Quarries in the foothills. Refugee settlements clinging to the road. Mercenary companies hiring at every crossroads. The boom shows on the road as **traffic, dust, and price-shock** — the cost of timber, stone, and grain has soared, and the cost of getting them to the city is paid in tolls, bribes, and bodies.

The winners are the consortiums; the losers are independent merchants and the local communities the consortiums shoulder aside. A scribe in Beregost, a smith in Nashkel, a fishing village on the lower Chionthar — each will tell the same story of being slowly squeezed out by larger and meaner players from upriver.

## The scarred land

The Absolute Crisis a decade ago left a psychic and magical bruise on the wilderness that has not faded. The most extreme expression is the [Wood of Sharp Teeth](wood-of-sharp-teeth/README.md), where the dossier reports trees twisted into grotesque shapes, shambling mounds of reanimated battlefield dead, and will-o'-wisps that are "the psychic echoes of dying screams" rather than ordinary undead. Less spectacular but more pervasive: rivers run dirtier than they should, fields produce poorly, and predators have grown bolder along every wood edge.

The pollution of the [lower Chionthar](river-chionthar-lower/README.md) is the visible everyday version of the same theme — the city's industry has poisoned the river its trade rides on, and the fish, birds, and fishing villages downstream are gone.

## The fraying edge of law

The [Flaming Fist](../../../../20-Factions/flaming-fist/summary.md) barely reaches [Wyrm's Crossing](../outer-city/wyrms-crossing.md). Beyond it, "law" splits into a patchwork:

- **Coast Way:** [Zhentarim](../../../../20-Factions/zhentarim/summary.md)-backed toll-forts run by local proxies, with arbitrary fees and "protection" that mostly protects the toll-master.
- **Trade Way north of Beregost:** the [Chionthar Consortium](../../../../20-Factions/chionthar-consortium/summary.md) and its enforcers, undercutting the road from the river.
- **Trade Way southern reaches:** [Guild](../../../../20-Factions/guild/summary.md) River-Rats run the back-channels; the [Hellriders](../../../../20-Factions/hellriders-of-new-elturel/summary.md) hold token Trade Way checkpoints near Elturel.
- **Cloakwood and Wood of Sharp Teeth:** no civil authority — druids, hags, refugees, bandits.

A traveler can move through the hinterlands; a small merchant cannot do so safely without an escort, and an escort costs more than the trip is worth. That is the new normal.

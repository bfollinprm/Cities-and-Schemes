# Wood of Sharp Teeth (local face)

The dark forest east of the Trade Way. Setting-scale view at [10-Setting/regions/wood-of-sharp-teeth.md](../../../../../10-Setting/regions/wood-of-sharp-teeth.md). This page is the local edge that touches Baldur's Gate's hinterland.

## What's here

- [character.md](character.md) — what travelers see at the wood's edge.
- [factions.md](factions.md) — limited, but not zero.

## Why it matters

The dossier reports the wood as economically worthless and physically dangerous — the consortiums and guilds keep out. That makes it useful to anyone who wants to *not be found*: hag covens, mad goblin bands, and bandit hideouts are all reported. The dossier names a coven, a goblin tribe, and an outlaw band specifically.

The exception to "no economic value" is the **Whisperwood Copse**, a section of the wood the Western Gate clear-cut under their newly-ratified hinterland charter (see `faction-quests.xlsx`). The clear-cut enraged the [Circle of the Inner Grove](../../../../../20-Factions/circle-of-the-inner-grove/summary.md) and confirmed that the Western Gate now considers no woodland untouchable.

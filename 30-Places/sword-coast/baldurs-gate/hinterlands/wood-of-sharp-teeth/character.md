# Character

The road that skirts the wood's northern edge is a place of fear and superstition. Inns along it are fortified and bolted from sundown. Locals speak in hushed tones; few will accept fares that involve cutting through.

## At the edge

The transition is not gradual. The wood's edge is abrupt — healthy forest one step, then twisted trees in grotesque shapes the next. Branches like grasping claws. Ground littered with the bones of forgotten creatures. The air carries a stench of decay that doesn't match any visible carcass.

## What the dossier reports inside

Ser Arman names specific phenomena, with the caveat that he has not been inside himself:

- **Shambling mounds made of reanimated battlefield dead**, not vegetation.
- **Will-o'-wisps that are psychic echoes of dying screams**, not ordinary undead.
- **Displacer beasts that drive men mad** with a glance — "living nightmares," he calls them.
- A **hag coven** in the wood's heart.
- A **goblin tribe** driven mad by the wood's psychic energies.
- A **bandit band** using the wood as a haven that the consortiums won't enter.

The pattern: every threat is psychically inflected, not just biologically dangerous. The dossier ties this directly to the psychic shockwave of the Absolute's defeat at Elturel.

## The Whisperwood Copse

The clear-cut site at the western edge. Per `faction-quests.xlsx`, the Western Gate dispatched logging crews to the Whisperwood Copse and clear-cut it with brutal efficiency. The site is now a stump-field at the wood's lip — visible from the Trade Way, raw and ugly. It is the most obvious place to look for both Western Gate enforcers and Circle reprisal squads.

## Useful to whom

- **Anyone hiding** — the wood is the only large area in the hinterland that guarantees no consortium pursuit.
- **Adventurers chasing a specific rumor** — coven, goblin tribe, or bandits. Otherwise, no reason to enter.
- **Druidic and harper agents** crossing east undetected — at high personal cost.

## Avoid

- Camping inside the wood without strong wards.
- Looking too long at a displacer beast.
- Believing locals who say "I know a shortcut through."

# Character

The Coast Way out of Baldur's Gate is paved, broad, in worse shape than it should be, and watched at every league. The first toll-fort is within sight of the city's outer walls; the next is an hour up the road. They get more frequent and more capricious as the road continues north.

## At a toll-fort

A toll-fort on the Coast Way is rarely impressive — a fortified inn, a watchtower with a barricade across the road, a stockaded yard. Maybe a dozen men, mostly local, badly disciplined, well-armed. A toll-master in a chain shirt with no insignia. The fort's own livery, if any, is some local heraldry — rarely the Zhentarim's.

Tolls are quoted at "a few gold per wagon, a handful of silver per head" — but the quote is an opening position. The toll-master sets the actual figure. Argue too loudly and your cargo is "inspected"; pay too quickly and you're marked as flush. Wagons are searched if the toll-master smells contraband or wants to.

## Convoys vs. small merchants

The Western Gate moves in **convoys of dozens of wagons** with mercenary outriders, archers on every wagon, and a captain who negotiates tolls in batch. They pay, complain, and pass.

A single merchant with one or two wagons has no leverage. They pay what's demanded, eat the loss, and pray. Many simply don't make the trip anymore — they hire onto a Western Gate convoy for a cut, or they switch to short-haul work between settlements, or they fold.

## Bandits and "patrols"

Between toll-forts, the road is bandit country in name and in fact. The dossier notes the Zhentarim send out "patrols" against particularly troublesome bandits — but only enough to keep up the appearance of paid-for security. Most bandit incidents go unanswered. Some bandit gangs are tacitly licensed by a nearby toll-master who takes a cut of their hauls.

## Off the road

Stepping off the Coast Way is mostly a way to die faster. The land alongside is broken country, refugee shanty-clusters, abandoned farms. Walk an hour off the road and you may meet refugees, bandits, or *both wearing each other's clothes*.

## Safe stops

- [Friendly Arm Inn](../friendly-arm-inn/README.md) — the major fortified Harper-affiliated stop.
- A handful of independent inns the Zhentarim haven't pressured yet (mostly because they're too small to bother with).

## Avoid

- Volunteering information to a toll-master.
- Carrying anything obviously valuable in a single-wagon party.
- Camping within sight of the road outside a fortified stop.

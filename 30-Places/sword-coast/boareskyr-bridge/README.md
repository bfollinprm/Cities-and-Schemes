# Boareskyr Bridge

Strategic crossing on the Trade Way where the road spans the **River Winding Water**. Long a chokepoint contested by armies; presently held by a small Elturgardian paladin remnant.

## What's here

- [character.md](character.md) — the bridge garrison, the daily traffic, the pressures on a small order in a hostile environment.

## Why it matters

The bridge is *symbolic* as much as strategic. The dossier frames it as a "bastion of order in a world of chaos" — and stresses the symbol as much as the function. The paladins are:

- **Few**, and not getting reinforcements.
- **Welcome** to merchants and travelers, who get safe passage at the crossing.
- **Targeted** by bandits and monsters, by [Zhentarim](../../../20-Factions/zhentarim/summary.md), and by the [Chionthar Consortium](../../../20-Factions/chionthar-consortium/summary.md) — all of whom see the bridge as an obstacle to their ambitions.

The dossier closes on the paladins as a small flickering light in encroaching darkness — a symbol of a past that is gone and a hope for a future that may yet be. Whether they hold the bridge another year is genuinely uncertain.

## Players around the bridge

- Elturgardian paladin remnant — the small holding garrison.
- Travelers and merchants — the visible beneficiaries of the bridge being held.
- Bandits and monsters in the surrounding country — constant low-level pressure.
- [Zhentarim](../../../20-Factions/zhentarim/summary.md) — would prefer the bridge tolled by their proxies; have not yet moved openly.
- [Chionthar Consortium](../../../20-Factions/chionthar-consortium/summary.md) — would prefer the bridge garrisoned by *their* people, charging *their* rates; same restraint, same impatience.

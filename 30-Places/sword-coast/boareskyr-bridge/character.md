# Character

The Boareskyr Bridge is an old stone span across a wide brown river. The bridge itself is in good repair — the paladins maintain it as a point of pride. At each end, a small fortified guardhouse with a gate and a winch. A tower on the southern bank, low enough to look ornamental, high enough to control the approach.

## The garrison

Small. The dossier doesn't give a count, but the description fits something like a dozen sworn paladins, twice that in squires and lay support, plus a handful of camp followers who run the kitchen, the stable, and the smithy. Discipline is good. Equipment is well-kept but visibly aging — replacements come from Elturel proper, which means rarely.

The captain is named in the dossier only as "the senior paladin." The order itself is the post-Crisis remnant of Elturgard — survivors, reorganized, reduced. Many were on the bridge during the chaos; some are veterans of the Hells.

## Daily life on the bridge

A constant trickle of travelers. The paladins do not charge a toll. They do check wagons for contraband (slaves, infernal artifacts, the obvious stolen goods); they do turn away wagons with paperwork that doesn't add up. They are *polite* about it — which earns them a great deal of the goodwill that keeps independent merchants routing through here even when shorter paths exist.

A merchant party stopping at the bridge for the night is offered safe camping inside the southern guardhouse's walled yard, simple food at cost, and a quiet warning about the road ahead in either direction.

## The pressure

The paladins are *targeted*. Bandit attacks on the bridge are routine — small, persistent, repulsed. Monster incursions from the surrounding wilderness are less frequent but bloodier. The Zhentarim have made indirect overtures: *let us help you with the upkeep, in exchange for accommodating our caravans*. The Consortium has done the same. The paladins have refused both. So far, neither has escalated to direct confrontation — but the dossier suggests the restraint is tactical rather than principled.

## Mood

Resolute. The paladins know they are a remnant. They are not deluded about reinforcements coming. The decision they have made — collectively, by the look of it — is to *hold the bridge as long as they can* and let the symbol mean what it means.

## Useful to whom

- **Independent merchants** wanting one safe stretch of Trade Way.
- **Refugees** fleeing south — paladins quietly help when they can.
- **Anyone with a paladin past** — the order welcomes oath-bound visitors.

## Avoid

- Approaching the bridge with infernal artifacts on your person or in your wagon.
- Trying to bribe the captain. The reaction is icy and immediate.

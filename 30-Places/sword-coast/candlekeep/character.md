# Character

Candlekeep stands grey and tall on its promontory, the sea below it constant and the gates closed for most of the year. Approach by the coast road and the first thing you see is a scribe and an Avowed at the outer gate, both of whom have already decided you are not getting in.

## The gate

The famous requirement — donate a book the library does not already possess — is harder to satisfy than it has ever been. The monks have grown selective: a rare illuminated ledger from a Sembian merchant house may be refused; a half-burned diary from a Cloakwood logging-camp casualty may be accepted. The decision is made by the gate-Avowed and is not appealable.

Most supplicants are turned away politely, with the suggestion that they "consult the library by correspondence" — which means they will get no response at all.

## Inside (if you get in)

The library is quieter than visitors expect. Halls of stacks; reading-rooms that smell of vellum and lamp-oil; small refectories where the Avowed eat in silence. The pace is slow and the work is real — cataloging, copying, the patient drift of decades.

There are also rooms a guest never sees. The dossier hints, and other sources confirm, that Candlekeep maintains a substantial *active* operation — agents in the hinterland, intelligence-gathering on the consortiums and the Zhentarim, careful counter-moves played at long range. The library is *not* neutral; it is selectively allied to whatever it considers the long-term preservation of knowledge.

## The refugee scholars

A small cohort of Baldurian scholars and artists in residence — people the monks judged worth removing from the city. They are not inmates and not staff; they are guests, with reading privileges and modest stipends. Some are notable enough that their absence from Baldur's Gate is itself a piece of intelligence.

## The mood

Wary. The dossier reports the monks are particularly alarmed by the spread of "the new ignorance" — populist, anti-intellectual, easy to weaponize. They speak of it the way a healer speaks of a slow plague. Their inwardness is partly self-defence and partly a strategic husbanding of capacity for a coming confrontation they believe is inevitable.

## Useful to whom

- **Researchers** with a genuine claim — and a *very* good gift.
- **Couriers and informants** — the agent network operates through intermediaries; a contact in the hinterland is more accessible than the gate.
- **People who need to disappear** — vanishingly rare, but it happens, by direct invitation only.

## Avoid

- Bringing a Consortium or Zhentarim sponsor through the gate. The Avowed know.
- Trying to *steal* from the library. People have tried; people do not return.

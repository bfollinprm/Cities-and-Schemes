# Candlekeep

Fortress-library on a rocky promontory overlooking the Sea of Swords, south of Baldur's Gate. The dossier author, Ser Arman de Vries, is a Candlekeep sage; the dossier was researched in part from inside the library.

## What's here

- [character.md](character.md) — what the gate experience and the closed-door reality look like.

## Why it matters

Candlekeep has always been secretive, but the dossier reports the monks have turned even more sharply inward in the last decade. The reasons:

- Wariness of the new powers — consortiums, guilds, mercenary companies.
- Concern about the rise of "the new ignorance" — populist anti-intellectualism in the hinterland's dispossessed.
- Specifically, alarm at the rise of the [Zhentarim](../../../20-Factions/zhentarim/summary.md) and the [Chionthar Consortium](../../../20-Factions/chionthar-consortium/summary.md), which Candlekeep is "actively working to counter ... in their own subtle and indirect way."

The famous entrance requirement — donation of a unique and valuable book — has become harder to meet, and the monks are now selective in a way they previously were not. Candlekeep has become "more talked about than visited."

But the library is not passive. It still maintains a network of agents and informants, provides quiet sanctuary to a small number of refugee scholars and artists, and is preparing — in the dossier's framing — for some kind of war.

## Players around Candlekeep

- The monks themselves — secretive order, leadership opaque.
- Candlekeep agents — scattered across the hinterland; light footprint, deep information network.
- Refugee scholars in residence — small group; mostly Baldurian intellectuals who got out before they were silenced.
- [Bibliophile](../../../20-Factions/bibliophile/summary.md) — the existing Baldurian collector-faction whose interests sometimes intersect with the library.

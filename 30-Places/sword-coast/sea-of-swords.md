# Sea of Swords

The cold, grey body of water along the Sword Coast — the working sea between Calimshan and Amn in the south and Luskan in the north, with the great trading cities of Baldur's Gate and [Waterdeep](waterdeep/README.md) on its eastern shore. Most coastal trade in this campaign moves on it.

## What runs on it

The bulk of long-haul trade between the southern ports and the north: Calimshan silks, Amnian timber and gold, Baldurian iron and grain, Waterdhavian finished goods, Luskan furs and amber. The four Foreign Kontors at [Gray Harbour](baldurs-gate/lower-city/gray-harbour.md) live and die by these runs.

## Pressures

- **Piracy.** A constant, never quite suppressed — the Sea of Swords' inshore reefs and offshore fogs hide more rovers than any flag can keep up with. Recent losses include the [Seven Suns](../../20-Factions/seven-suns/summary.md) Jhasso family's Calimshan silk run, taken by combined weather and piracy in 1501 DR.
- **Weather.** Sudden squalls and the long autumn fogs reliably eat one or two ships a season. Insurance is expensive; underinsured cargoes mean ruined houses.
- **The Moonshaes' fey queen.** Her selective "tax" on shipping she dislikes makes the central crossing unpredictable for any captain who has crossed her. See [moonshae-isles.md](moonshae-isles.md).

# Berdusk

City on the upper reaches of the [River Chionthar](../baldurs-gate/hinterlands/river-chionthar-lower/README.md), east of Baldur's Gate in the Western Heartlands. Long known for skilled artisans, powerful merchant guilds, and close [Harper](../../../20-Factions/harpers/summary.md) ties.

## What's here

- [character.md](character.md) — a city of shadows and secrets, fighting a losing internal war.

## Why it matters

Berdusk is the dossier's cleanest expression of the broader **Harpers vs. Zhentarim** conflict. The Zhentarim have long had a presence here; in the last decade their influence has grown dramatically. They have:

- Infiltrated the city's merchant guilds.
- Corrupted city officials.
- Established a powerful, organized criminal underworld.

The Harpers have been forced underground — outnumbered, outgunned, outmaneuvered — fighting a secret war against an enemy that is "everywhere and nowhere." The dossier judges them on the losing side, though not yet beaten.

For Baldur's Gate's hinterland, Berdusk matters as:

- **The Harpers' regional anchor.** What happens here shapes Harper capacity on the Coast Way and at the [Friendly Arm Inn](../baldurs-gate/hinterlands/friendly-arm-inn/README.md).
- **The Zhentarim's regional staging point.** The toll-fort network on the Coast Way is partly run from here.
- **A trade node** — the upper Chionthar feeds the same river-trade the Chionthar Consortium dominates downstream.

## Players in Berdusk

- [Harpers](../../../20-Factions/harpers/summary.md) — operating in shadow; recruitment, intelligence, occasional disruption.
- [Zhentarim](../../../20-Factions/zhentarim/summary.md) — through guild infiltration, corrupted officials, and an embedded criminal organization.
- Local merchant guilds — increasingly compromised but not entirely lost.
- City officials — variably corrupted; the dossier doesn't enumerate.

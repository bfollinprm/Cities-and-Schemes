# Character

Berdusk looks prosperous and is. The artisan workshops are working, the river docks are busy, the markets are full. The visible city is doing fine.

The invisible city is in the middle of a war.

## The mood

Quiet. Careful. People speak in low voices in inns; conversations stop when a stranger sits down. The dossier's verdict — "secrets held even more closely" than before — is accurate at street level. Locals know that the wrong overheard comment can cost them a Zhent connection or a Harper safehouse, depending on which side of the line they stand on.

Most Berduskans don't *want* to take sides. They want to do their work, ship their goods, raise their families. The shadow war is ambient; they navigate it the way coastal townspeople navigate weather.

## The guilds

Compromised to varying degrees. The dossier states the Zhentarim have *infiltrated* — meaning they have placed members and turned existing members, not necessarily that they run the guilds outright. Guild leadership in Berdusk now spends substantial energy on **internal counter-intelligence** they would never admit to. Some guilds are more compromised than others; the city's mercer and shipper guilds have been hit hard, the artisan guilds less so.

## The officials

Variably corrupted. Bribery works in Berdusk; so does blackmail; so does simple fatigue. The dossier doesn't name names, but the pattern it describes is *systemic*, not exceptional — Zhentarim influence is broad enough that even an honest official has to navigate around compromised peers.

## The Harpers

Underground. Operating from rotating safehouses, recruiting in person and slowly, declining open confrontation. They run intelligence into Baldur's Gate via the Friendly Arm route and out toward the broader Heartlands. Their visible footprint is lighter than it has been in any living memory, which the dossier reads — correctly — as a sign of how much pressure they're under.

## The mood the dossier closes on

Berdusk is a city *at war with itself*. The conflict will shape the city's destiny but is not the kind of conflict that produces decisive battles. It produces slow attrition, lost informants, quiet disappearances, and a city that is steadily becoming a different city than it was.

## Useful to whom

- **Harper agents** moving information west to east.
- **Anyone trading on the upper Chionthar** — Berdusk is the natural staging point.
- **Investigators of Zhentarim networks** — Berdusk is where the network shows its shape, if you know how to look.

## Avoid

- Open Harper-affiliation in any inn that hasn't been vouched for.
- Mentioning specific guild members by name to a stranger.

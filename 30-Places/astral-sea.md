# The Astral Sea

The silver, dimensionless plane that lies between the worlds — neither matter nor spirit but the medium of *thought itself*. Travel here is by intent: a body that knows where it is going arrives there. A body that does not is simply lost.

Most relevantly to this campaign, the Astral Sea is the home plane of the **githyanki**, and the theatre of the **True War** that the [Unchained](../20-Factions/unchained/summary.md) believe is the only war that ultimately matters.

## Why it touches the campaign

After Prince Orpheus's liberation of the plane and the defeat of the Netherbrain, factions of githyanki have begun gathering on the Material Plane to recover, recruit, and prepare. The **K'liir** in [Baldur's Gate](../30-Places/sword-coast/baldurs-gate/outer-city/the-kliir.md) is the most visible such gathering on the Sword Coast. Their commander, **K'laar**, considers the Material a "stepping stone" — a place to hone blades and minds for the True War now resuming on the Astral.

> *Tsk'va*. You see this world. We see a stepping stone. Your city is a place of refuge, a place to hone our blades and minds for the True War. Prince Orpheus liberated this plane; we will not see it fall.
>
> — K'laar, addressing a Council of Four delegation

## What the campaign treats it as

A backdrop, not a destination. Visiting the Astral Sea is possible (silver cord, *plane shift*, githyanki trade-stones) but rare. Most encounters with it come *through* githyanki characters and politics on the Material.

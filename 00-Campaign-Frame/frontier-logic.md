# Frontier Logic

Outside the city walls of Baldur's Gate, jurisdiction blurs, the wilderness has its own claims, and "law" is whoever shows up with steel. This is not a moral judgment — it is the *operational* rule the campaign should treat as physics.

## The principle

The Sword Coast in 1502 DR has no functional mid-tier authority. Cities still hold inside their walls; great powers still operate at long range. Between them — in the hinterlands, on the roads, in the woods — there is no body that can both *patrol* and *adjudicate*. Patrols exist (Flaming Fist at the city wall, Hellrider checkpoints near Elturel, paladins at Boareskyr Bridge) but each covers a single point; they cannot enforce a regional rule.

The vacuum is filled, not with anarchy, but with **patchwork local authorities** — toll-masters, consortium agents, druid circles, militia captains, paladin remnants, refugee committees. Each has real authority over a specific patch, none has authority over the patch next to it. A traveler crossing the hinterland passes through *several* small jurisdictions and a great deal of nothing.

## How it shows up

- **Roads.** Coast Way is a chain of Zhentarim-proxy toll-forts; Trade Way is Consortium economic captures and Hellrider checkpoint stubs. No single road-authority on either.
- **Forests.** Cloakwood has the Western Gate on the saw-roads and the Circle of the Inner Grove everywhere else. The Wood of Sharp Teeth has no civil authority and is dangerous on its own terms.
- **Rivers.** The Chionthar has the Consortium on the surface and the Guild's River-Rats underneath; pollution is uncontrolled because no body has standing to control it.
- **Settlements.** Beregost is a Consortium client. Nashkel balances Amn against the Consortium. Berdusk is partially owned by the Zhentarim. Each is "self-governing" in name and *captured* in practice.

## What this means at the table

- **Authority without legitimacy.** People who can stop you and demand things, with no claim that would be recognized anywhere else. Refusing costs; complying costs.
- **Legitimacy without authority.** People whose moral or institutional claim is real (Hellrider survivors, paladin remnants, druid circles, Candlekeep scholars) but who lack the force to enforce it. Help they give is informal and often dangerous to the giver.
- **The wilderness has its own claim.** Druids, fey, and the Wood of Sharp Teeth itself are not scenery; they push back. A campaign that treats woodland as resource collides with a woodland that treats *humans* the same way.

## Where it's most visible

Coast Way toll-forts; the Cloakwood logging war; the Wood of Sharp Teeth's post-Crisis residue; [Wyrm's Crossing](../30-Places/sword-coast/baldurs-gate/outer-city/wyrms-crossing.md) as the last point of city writ; Boareskyr Bridge as a single legitimate authority holding one point.

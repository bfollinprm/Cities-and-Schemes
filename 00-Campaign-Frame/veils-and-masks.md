# Veils and Masks

In this campaign, **public power and real power are different**. The surface is theater; the truth is masked. Every institution that claims legitimacy in 1502 DR has a second, hidden face — and most of the violence in the world happens at the seam between the two.

## The principle

Anonymity, secrecy, and plausible deniability are core technologies of governance, not aberrations. They were built on purpose — [Ahghairon's](../20-Factions/masked-lords/people/ahghairon.md) Lords of Waterdeep, the Council of Four's plutocracy in Baldur's Gate, the [Black Network](../20-Factions/zhentarim/summary.md), the Harpers, the [Cassalanters'](../20-Factions/waterdeep-noble-houses/associations/house-cassalanter.md) devil-pact, the Cult of the Returning Lord — all run on the assumption that the ruler's identity, the patron's identity, and the killer's identity are inputs the public must never have. **The mask is the office.**

This is true even when the rulers are well-intentioned. The [Masked Lords](../20-Factions/masked-lords/summary.md) of Waterdeep wear magical helms not to lie, but to be uncorruptible by lobbying and vendetta. Anonymity is supposed to make them objective. Instead it makes them invisible to one another, which is how a Masked Lord could murder his colleagues from inside the council in 1491 DR without the public noticing until the bodies were counted.

## How to apply at the table

- **Surface and shadow are both real.** When a faction acts, ask which face is acting — the public office or the masked operator. They may be the same person and still pursue contradictory goals.
- **Treason is structural, not exceptional.** A government that runs on masks will produce a traitor eventually; it is the system's predictable failure mode, not a betrayal of the system.
- **Reputation is a weapon and a vulnerability.** [Laeral Silverhand](../20-Factions/masked-lords/people/laeral-silverhand.md) projects unbroken archmage authority while concealing diminished power post-[Sundering](../10-Setting/cosmology/sundering.md). [House Cassalanter](../20-Factions/waterdeep-noble-houses/associations/house-cassalanter.md) projects philanthropy while serving [Asmodeus](../10-Setting/cosmology/deities/asmodeus.md). The brand is the lie that lets the truth operate.
- **"Plausible deniability" is the default.** Most patrons in this campaign — the [Western Gate Trading Company](../20-Factions/western-gate-trading-company/summary.md), the [Knights of the Shield](../20-Factions/knights-of-the-shield/summary.md), [Manshoon's](../20-Factions/zhentarim/people/manshoon.md) [Zhentarim](../20-Factions/zhentarim/summary.md), the [Cassalanters](../20-Factions/waterdeep-noble-houses/associations/house-cassalanter.md) — pay through intermediaries so the order can never be traced back. Players asking "who hired this" should expect three layers of veil before they reach the face.
- **Reform is itself a faction.** The "Open Rulers" of Waterdeep want the masks off; the Ahghaironian traditionalists insist the masks *are* the safeguard. Both are right and both are wrong. The campaign treats this as an unresolved live debate, not a settled question.

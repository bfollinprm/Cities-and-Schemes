# Week 6 — Events

Early spring, 1502 DR. The siege bites; the city eats itself.

## The Starvation Siege

[Archduke Lyra Silvershield tightens the noose on the Outer City](../../20-Factions/western-gate-trading-company/activity/week_6/the-starvation-siege.md): Sembian patrols now confiscate **personal food stores** off any traveller crossing the Outer City roads, weaponizing hunger to break the [Free Trader blockade](../../20-Factions/free-traders-of-the-outskirts/activity/week_5/heed-the-ultimatum.md) at the Basilisk Gate. Soup-lines at the [Open Hand Temple](../../30-Places/sword-coast/baldurs-gate/outer-city/open-hand-temple.md) double in length and halve in volume in three days. To test the resolve of the barricade, [the Council of Four orders mercenaries to fire warning shots over the Free Trader line](../../20-Factions/council-of-four/activity/week_6/the-warning-shot.md) — *the first time in living memory mercenaries hired by the sitting Archduke have fired on Baldurian civilians*. The volley falls on a [Hellrider-trained shield wall](../../20-Factions/hellriders-of-new-elturel/activity/week_6/the-shield-wall.md) that does not break; ['Mad' Meggan answers the same afternoon by instituting fair-share rationing inside the barricade](../../20-Factions/free-traders-of-the-outskirts/activity/week_6/rationing-the-hope.md). The line holds, hungry and disciplined.

[Editor Ettvard Needle's "Siege Edition" of the *Baldur's Mouth*](../../20-Factions/baldurs-mouth/activity/week_6/the-siege-edition.md) makes the front page a wall of starving-children sketches — some staged, some not — and runs **Kaelan's *Insurance Scam* exposé** under the new investigative-journalism credential, naming the Consortium and Sembian Kontor as arsonists-and-insurers. The print run sells out three times. The Archduke's economic base takes its first real wound from the city's own press.

[Bursar Korgan Ironhand triggers a twenty-percent supply hike](../../20-Factions/sembian-kontor/activity/week_6/the-price-of-steel.md) on the mercenary contract — locking Sembia's profit margins to the duration of the siege and ensuring any settlement that ends the blockade early *costs Sembia money*. [The Harpers reply with sabotage on the Trade Way](../../20-Factions/harpers/activity/week_6/the-supply-sabotage.md), torching two-thirds of a high-quality steel shipment; [Factor Mornwood at the Splendid Yard intervenes when the Fist tries to arrest the Harper agent](../../20-Factions/waterdhavian-kontor/activity/week_6/the-diplomatic-immunity.md), invoking Lords' Alliance immunity in front of an Upper City crowd. **Waterdeep has chosen.**

## The institutions harden behind locked gates

[The Watch Captain locks the interior gates of the Upper City](../../20-Factions/watch/activity/week_6/the-gate-lockdown.md) — no entry without a resident's pass. Trade dries up overnight; Bloomridge runs out of fresh bread; the Upper City becomes a **gilded cage**. [The Peerage of Coin votes Sembian-mercenary "private security"](../../20-Factions/peerage-of-coin/activity/week_6/the-private-security.md) at every Counting House and bank, livery-marked and answering only to the coin lords. **The Fist's monopoly on legitimate force has been quietly dissolved on two fronts in the same week.** [The Peerage of Blood files a public Petition of Grievances](../../20-Factions/peerage-of-blood/activity/week_6/the-petition-of-grievances.md) at the High Hall demanding the Sembian "foreign army" be expelled, while [Lord Corin Durinbold hosts a candlelit Officers' Mess](../../20-Factions/patriar-heritage-society/activity/week_6/the-officers-mess.md) at his manor for the Fist sergeants who took his Silver Fist coin — and explicitly buys their willingness to *look the other way* when the order comes. [Marshal Flint's Lower-City smuggling crackdown collapses inside two days](../../20-Factions/flaming-fist/activity/week_6/the-officers-dilemma.md) under that same patriar bribery. [The Knights of the Shield call the price due on a manorborn pact](../../20-Factions/knights-of-the-shield/activity/week_6/the-infernal-ledger.md) and use Peerage-of-Blood voting-history blackmail to **swing a tariff vote against Durinbold's bloc**; that night [the moderate cross-aisle private caucus at the Hellscream Bathhouse collapses in a knife-pulled brawl](../../20-Factions/parliament-of-peers/activity/week_6/the-private-caucus.md). Parliament has no legislative escape valve left.

[The Society of Baldurian Integrity's nightlings reach the Upper City through Heapside tunnels](../../20-Factions/society-of-baldurian-integrity/activity/week_6/the-art-vandal.md) and coat the Waterdhavian Kontor's allegorical statues in distinctively Baldurian sluiceway mud. *The lockdown does not work.* [The Elturel Remembrance Society marks Upper City "Hell-gold" businesses with invisible alchemical Zariel sigils](../../20-Factions/elturel-remembrance-society/activity/week_6/the-marked-doors.md) — a target list, published only to those carrying *detect magic*.

## The water and river wars

[The Amnian Kontor closes a hostile takeover on Brindlestone & Sons](../../20-Factions/amnian-kontor/activity/week_6/the-hostile-takeover.md), giving Consul Athar a direct operating presence on the Chionthar river fleet for the first time in living memory. [Factor Errinthal answers with a desperate smuggler-run past the Amnian blockade](../../20-Factions/chionthar-consortium/activity/week_6/the-desperate-run.md) — the Amnians are *waiting*; the cargo is impounded, the Consortium's last clandestine reserve gone. [Silas the Eel takes a salvage contract](../../20-Factions/river-rats/activity/week_6/the-river-retrieval.md) on a wreck whose lockbox the Amnians had already pulled the night before; he loses another swimmer and, internally, his last credibility. [One-Eyed Jack's Wharf Rats burn a Zhentarim warehouse to the ground](../../20-Factions/wharf-rats/activity/week_6/the-counter-punch.md) — clean, vicious, four thousand gold of [Roah Moonglow's](../../20-Factions/zhentarim/people/roah-moonglow.md) product gone in an hour. Roah pays back across the ledger: [her clerks alter the city tax records of Guild-aligned businesses](../../20-Factions/zhentarim/activity/week_6/the-ledger-logic.md), triggering automatic asset-freeze audits across the Lower City. [Nine-Fingers Keene runs a brutal internal Guild audit](../../20-Factions/guild/activity/week_6/the-financial-audit.md) hunting for the Zhent slicers; she finds three corrupt bookkeepers, kills two, and ends the week ahead in losses. The Zhentarim wins the paper war while the Wharf Rats win the alley war, and *the Guild is bleeding from both.*

[The Shipwrights answer last week's harbor humiliation with a drydock lockout](../../20-Factions/wc-shipwrights-calkers/activity/week_6/the-drydock-lockout.md): no repairs for any vessel not flying their guild flag. Independent captains are forced to beach their ships on the mudflats. [Matron Saltwind organizes the Fishmongers into an armed harbor militia](../../20-Factions/wc-fishmongers/activity/week_6/the-harbor-patrol.md) and starts hurling petty-thieves into the bay. The Fist's absence from the docks has produced two parallel guild armies enforcing two parallel laws on the same piers.

[The Druids of the Inner Grove name the Tanneries as the river-rot source](../../20-Factions/circle-of-the-inner-grove/activity/week_6/the-source-of-the-rot.md) and call up choking vines from beneath the curing-vat block — three vats split, a year of tannic poison spilled into the Outer City gutters. [Goodman Borlu arms his butchers with cleavers and torches](../../20-Factions/wc-butchers-tanners/activity/week_6/the-tannery-defense.md) and loses the skirmish; the Tanneries are operationally dead for at least a week, and the Circle has openly declared war on the Butchers' guild.

## The water-watch and the prototype

[The Purified set up a permanent water-watch at the Basilisk Gate pumps with the new Cogwork Detector](../../20-Factions/purified/activity/week_6/the-water-watch.md) — the device buzzes at every aura, the patrols cause fistfights and one weeping mother in the rain, and the panic ironically provides cover for [the Lantaner Concession's secret prototype assembly at the Cogwork Quay](../../20-Factions/lantaner-concession/activity/week_6/the-prototype-assembly.md). Glim-Nimblewhiz drowns a Sembian thief at the perimeter on the sixth day. [Master Tor-keg Silverhand smuggles a wagon-train of raw iron](../../20-Factions/wc-smiths-armorers/activity/week_6/the-iron-smuggle.md) past the Basilisk Gate checkpoint under a *forged* "civic defense" exemption to feed the guild's forges through the siege.

## The cult war beneath

[The Followers of the Phoenix lay the Foundation Stone of their new temple at the Phoenix Gate](../../20-Factions/followers-of-the-phoenix/activity/week_6/the-foundation-stone.md). The crowd's energy is no longer scared and devotional — it is angry and devotional. Volunteers tell visiting strangers they are "ready to start fires." [The Ember knows](../../20-Factions/followers-of-the-phoenix/people/the-ember.md), and is keeping the construction slow on purpose.

[The Takers of the Tithe poison their own captured Reaper](../../20-Factions/takers-of-the-tithe/activity/week_6/the-prison-silence.md) before he can talk to the party at the New Elturel stockade — silencing a loose end and exposing a **mole in the Hellrider supply line** in the same hour. [The Plague of Remembrance answers exposure with a manufactured riot](../../20-Factions/plague-of-remembrance/activity/week_6/the-false-flag.md): paid Eastway thugs in counterfeit Phoenix-refugee colors stage a violent attack on the Sow & Piglet, demanding the Fist crack down on the "violent" refugees. [The Cult of the Returning Lord miscalls a Slayer-summoning ritual in the Undercity](../../20-Factions/cult-of-the-returning-lord/activity/week_6/the-gutter-prayer.md) and loses *something* loose in the deep tunnels. [Old Man Hemlock moves the Gravekeepers' consecrated-corpse stockpile in silence to Cliffside Cemetery](../../20-Factions/gravekeepers/activity/week_6/the-corpse-caravan.md). [The Hands of the Absolute attempt a psionic transfusion to wake the Voice in the Choir](../../20-Factions/hands-of-the-absolute/activity/week_6/the-silent-scream.md), kill a kidnapped psi-sensitive, fail, and emit a city-wide psionic shockwave that [Commander K'laar reads as enemy intrusion](../../20-Factions/unchained/activity/week_6/the-psionic-sweep.md) — the K'liir psionic sweep that follows terrifies the Outer City pump-streets but does not catch them. [The Sodality's reset attempt to reboot the scrying censers backfires](../../20-Factions/wc-sorcerous-sodality/activity/week_6/the-system-reboot.md), blows out a block of windows around Sorcerous Sundries, and leaves the Hands' simultaneous spike unresolvable in the broken logs.

[Mother Nocturne suspects a mole in her own Sharran cell](../../20-Factions/silent-shroud/activity/week_6/the-blind-eye.md) and runs a painful Purge of the Blind that surfaces nothing — because the actual reason for losing track of Bror is the Cogwork Detector noise, the Sodality reboot, and Bror's silent psychic injury. [The Gilded Vein's emergency liquidation is a chase across the financial district](../../20-Factions/gilded-vein/activity/week_6/the-liquidated-assets.md) — five accounts pulled, four frozen, eight sealed; Thorne ends the week with a third of the cult's reserves and one agent dead in an alley. [Mister Smiles' Carnival sets up an alley-circus behind the Blushing Mermaid](../../20-Factions/carnival-of-whispers/activity/week_6/the-whispering-alley.md) and harvests despair off the Lower City drunks. [Xylia and the Unveiled run a frantic safehouse-shuffle keeping their rescued comrades one step ahead of the Fist dragnet](../../20-Factions/unveiled/activity/week_6/the-safehouse-shuffle.md) — they survive the week without growing.

## The Worshipful Companies fragment further

[Master Quickfoot's total Vintners ale embargo](../../20-Factions/wc-vintners-brewers/activity/week_6/the-ale-embargo.md) on the Sembian camps drains morale at Eastway by Tendays-end. [Master Phineas Droog's Alchemists analyze the previous week's poisoned ale](../../20-Factions/wc-alchemists-apothecaries/activity/week_6/the-poison-analysis.md), confirm a Zhentarim signature, and turn the ledger trade three ways — Vintner attestation, Sembian antidote contract, and quiet political leverage on Roah Moonglow. [Mistress Brightwood is forced under Guild pressure to order a "maintenance blackout" on Brampton's Zhentarim corridors](../../20-Factions/wc-chandlers-lamplighters/activity/week_6/the-dark-streets.md) — the Chandlers are now operationally a Guild auxiliary. [Master Vancroft's Greater Restoration on the mind-wiped surveyor fails](../../20-Factions/wc-cartographers-surveyors/activity/week_6/the-memory-loss.md): the K'liir wipe is psionic, not magical, and the Cartographers have no answer. [Master Goldhand walks the Goldsmiths' vaults with outside auditors](../../20-Factions/wc-goldsmiths-jewelers/activity/week_6/the-gold-standard.md) and restores the city's confidence in their solvency at the cost of his political capital. [Master Stonehand calls a Masons' labor strike](../../20-Factions/wc-masons-sculptors/activity/week_6/the-labor-strike.md) on every Council and patriar reinforcement site after Durinbold's "new-money trowels" insult; Durinbold's emissary is at the guildhall offering a face-saving deal inside four days. [Mistress Vance pivots Bloomridge's Mercer line from "somber couture" into "siege chic"](../../20-Factions/wc-mercers-clothiers/activity/week_6/the-winter-collection.md) — fur-lined coats, silvered walking-canes, sold out in forty-eight hours. *Vanity survives the siege.*

[The Iron Throne sells mercenary patrol routes to the Free Traders and Free-Trader weak points to the Sembian commanders](../../20-Factions/iron-throne/activity/week_6/the-double-cross.md) at the Blushing Mermaid — engineering the violence the coster profits from. [Nansi Gretta personally infiltrates the mercenary camp to rescue the dossier the Sembians fed her with disinformation](../../20-Factions/bibliophile/activity/week_6/the-correction.md), is recognized at the wax-color of a forged seal, escapes naked under a thrown lantern, and ends the week with her client list quietly cancelling subscriptions.

## From PC actions

The party opens week 6 at [the Plowman](../../30-Places/sword-coast/baldurs-gate/outer-city/rivington.md) reading [the Siege Edition](../../20-Factions/baldurs-mouth/activity/week_6/the-siege-edition.md) with Kaelan's own *Insurance Scam* folded inside it. [Spark proposes the Reverse Siege](../../20-Factions/party/activity/week_6/the-reverse-siege-pitch.md) — reinforce the blockade and starve the Upper City rather than fight into it — and the table commits. [The party rides for New Elturel and finds the Ember at the Foundation Stone ceremony](../../20-Factions/party/activity/week_6/recruit-the-ember.md). The crowd is incendiary; the Ember is reluctant; she will not commit her flock until the **Takers of the Tithe** target on her back is removed. [The captured Reaper at the Hellrider stockade is the first thread.](../../50-Sessions/session_22/player_summary.md)

En route to the stockade the next morning the party is [ambushed in a foggy New Elturel alley](../../50-Sessions/session_23/player_summary.md) by a Takers cell — Spotter, Reaper, Keeper, and two Thralls. They kill the Reaper outright, capture the Spotter, recover a **Soul-Trapping Polearm** and the **Tithe Ledger**. The ledger names *Alfira, Alena, and the entire party as fresh targets*. **Jaredith Heartsong returns** in pacifist robes and joins the party as a high-level support NPC.

At the High Rider's Stand they [run an unkillable interrogation ruse on the older captured Reaper](../../50-Sessions/session_24/player_summary.md) — Elia forges his name into the ledger; Kaelan stages a *Revivify*-after-execution. The Reaper breaks: the cell leader is **[Singa](../../20-Factions/takers-of-the-tithe/people/singa.md)**, the cell's hideout is on **Dusthawk Hill**, the cell is targeting political figures to incite civil war. He is then [poisoned mid-confession by his prison food](../../20-Factions/takers-of-the-tithe/activity/week_6/the-prison-silence.md). **There is a mole in the Hellrider supply chain.** The captured Spotter offers to lead the party to Singa's hideout in exchange for protection. They also meet [**Eric**, a minor patriarch in the prison tent](../../20-Factions/peerage-of-blood/people/eric.md), who has independently reconstructed Bhaalist soul-contract theology and the Zhentarim sewer-tax-records operation from his books — he offers future Upper City and Candlekeep access in exchange for clearing his name.

[Alfira disappears from the camp during the interrogation](../../50-Sessions/session_25/player_summary.md). Elia checks the ledger; her name is *glowing*. She has been taken for a ritual. The party rides for Dusthawk Hill at dusk and ascends the north face under the Spotter's guidance. **Four Cairns** bar the path; [the Skittering Beetle](../../20-Factions/takers-of-the-tithe/people/the-skittering-beetle.md) brokers four "theological-debt" trades: Kaelan refuses (24 necrotic, party-wide *Malaise*), Elia refuses (21 psychic, *Fractured ID*), Spark accepts the accusation (1 Exhaustion), Bror **accepts the Beetle's deal and sells his voice**. They reach the summit and find **the Counting House** — Singa's auditor-styled sanctum, Alfira hanging from silver chains over an obsidian pit.

[The battle](../../50-Sessions/session_26/player_summary.md) turns on Singa's **soul-link** mirroring damage onto Alfira. Bror writes "BURN THE BOOKS" to Elia and shadow-steps to grapple Singa into the pit with a satchel of smokepowder. As the party nears defeat, the Beetle offers Elia the **Tithe Ledger trade** — surrender the physical ledger to save the party. Elia agrees. The ledger unmakes itself; every party curse lifts; Singa freezes mid-incantation. Kaelan finishes with *Fireball*; Jaredith with *Chromatic Orb*. Singa burns. The trapped souls release in a long silver wail. Alfira drops; Bror catches her at the rim; Kaelan revives her with a 300-gp diamond Bror finds in the pit's silt. **Singa is dead. The Bhaalist cell on Dusthawk Hill is broken. Bror's voice returns when the Beetle's broader trade resolves.**

[The party walks Alfira down at dawn](../../50-Sessions/session_27/player_summary.md) and convinces her to rest at the Plowman. **Jaredith stays in New Elturel** to oversee the Phoenix Temple construction in her absence — the party's legacy in the refugee district pivots from "mercenaries" to "builders." The group splits at the Plowman: Bror, Elia, and Finn ride for [the Penumbra Playhouse](../../30-Places/sword-coast/baldurs-gate/lower-city/heapside.md) and find that Mother Nocturne wants the **physical sentient source** of Bror's secret, not just the words; the debt remains unpaid. **Kaelan crosses to Heapside and finds the Baldur's Mouth print shop ransacked, Ettvard Needle missing**, with a hidden note hinting Ettvard has fled to the dark crypts under [Tumbledown](../../30-Places/sword-coast/baldurs-gate/outer-city/tumbledown-and-floodway.md), on the western bank of Dusthawk Hill — *just where the party left a smoking crater the day before*.

The week's load-bearing facts: the Council fires on its own civilians and the line holds; the Upper City is locked behind its own gates; the Peerage of Coin's private security replaces the Fist on the coin row; the Druids open a new front against the Butchers' Tanneries; the Bhaalist cell on Dusthawk Hill is destroyed and Alfira is alive; the Tithe Ledger is gone and the Beetle has it; the Hellrider stockade has a mole; the Baldur's Mouth has lost its editor and the Consortium-aligned editorial line is one week away from replacing him. Week 7 begins with the search for Ettvard Needle in the crypts under Tumbledown.

# Secret Factions

##  The Conspiracists

### **The Seeker of the Hidden Sky**

* **Name:** Xylia  
* **High Concept:** A former refugee who survived the chaos of the nautiloid attack by hiding in the sewers. She is an Oath of Conspiracy paladin who believes the entire Absolute Crisis was a "false flag" operation orchestrated by a cabal of shapeshifting elites to solidify their control over the Sword Coast.  
* **The "Truth" (Her Conspiracy):** Xylia believes mind flayers and the Netherbrain were merely props, illusions, or bio-engineered weapons. The real masterminds are a secret society of doppelgangers and elder warlocks who have replaced key city leaders. Why? To create a crisis so terrifying that the people would *beg* for salvation, willingly accepting the "Great Bargain" and the erosion of their freedoms. She believes the heroes who "saved" the city were either unknowing pawns or high-level members of the conspiracy. The Githyanki are here not to hunt mind flayers, but to silence anyone who gets too close to the truth about the "hoax." The magical disturbances and psychic phenomena aren't after-effects; they are ongoing "psy-ops" to keep the populace scared and compliant.  
* **Backstory:** Xylia was a common merchant from the outskirts whose family was killed in the initial panic of the crisis. Hiding for weeks, she witnessed things that didn't add up: "citizens" who moved with unsettling coordination, "cultists" whose bodies dissolved into grey goo when unobserved, and Flaming Fist officers who seemed to deliberately guide people *towards* danger. She escaped with the conviction that what the *Baldur's Mouth* reported and what she saw were two different realities. She swore an oath to the "Truth Unseen" to expose the architects of the lie.  
* **Organization: The Unveiled**  
  * **Ideology:** The Unveiled are "truth-seekers" who communicate through coded messages and hidden symbols scrawled on alley walls. They are dedicated to "exposing the stagecraft" of the ruling powers. They meticulously document "inconsistencies" in the official narrative of the Absolute Crisis and see the hands of the "Shapeshifters" everywhere. A building collapsing is not shoddy construction; it's the cabal destroying evidence. A popular new official is not charismatic; they are a high-level doppelganger.  
  * **Membership:** A collection of the city's most paranoid: fringe scholars, disgraced journalists, low-level members of The Guild who have seen too much, and anyone who feels the official story is a lie.  
  * **Methods:** They don't fight openly. They engage in "counter-intelligence," breaking into the offices of the *Baldur's Mouth* to alter printing plates, replacing official proclamations with their own manifestos, and trying to acquire items that can reveal shapeshifters. They see themselves as righteous information warriors.  
* **Hooks and Relationships:**  
  * **Enemies:** They are obsessed with the **Sorcerous Sodality** and the **Harpers**, believing these organizations possess the magical and espionage tools necessary to pull off such a grand illusion and are therefore the conspiracy's architects. They view the **Githyanki of the K'liir** with terror, seeing them as the cabal's secret police.

### **The Damnation-Seeker**

* **Name:** Lord Perrine Hhune  
* **High Concept:** A zealous Elturian refugee and the last scion of a disgraced noble house. He is a paladin who believes the Fall of Elturel was not a tragedy, but a *successful* mass-sacrificial ritual that gave its secret architects—a cabal of Baldurian patriars—untold infernal power.  
* **The "Truth" (His Conspiracy):** Perrine believes the Archdevil Zariel was tricked. The true culprits were a secret inner circle of the **Knights of the Shield** who, in league with devil-worshipping patriar families, orchestrated the fall of the holy city to "transmute" its divine energy into infernal power. He believes the "reconstruction" of Baldur's Gate is funded by soul-forged gold from the Nine Hells, and that the new Consortiums and Kontors are merely fronts for laundering this damned wealth. The city isn't being rebuilt; it's being converted, brick by brick, into a new bastion for Asmodeus on the Material Plane. Karlach and Wyll's descent into Avernus wasn't heroic; they were "collected" as part of the deal.  
* **Backstory:** Perrine was a child in Elturel when it was dragged into Hell. He remembers the sky burning and the scent of brimstone. But he also remembers seeing figures on a distant hill performing a ritual *as the chains fell*. He was rescued and brought to the refugee camps, where he saw Baldurian merchants, their eyes glinting with avarice, preying on the survivors. He connected the ritual he saw with the city's sudden, miraculous boom in wealth and swore an oath to expose the "devils in silks" who sacrificed his home for their profit.  
* **Organization: The Elturel Remembrance Society**  
  * **Ideology:** On the surface, it's a support group for Elturian survivors, dedicated to preserving their lost culture. In reality, it is an echo chamber of Perrine's theory. They collect "evidence" of infernal influence: merchants who are "too successful," contracts with "unusual" clauses (which must be devil's contracts), and architecture in the rebuilt districts that seems to have "infernal geometry."  
  * **Membership:** Almost entirely composed of Elturian refugees, particularly those from New Elturel who harbor a deep-seated hatred for the Baldurian establishment. They are bound by shared trauma and a thirst for righteous vengeance.  
  * **Methods:** They conduct "purity tests" on goods and merchants, seeking traces of infernalism. They attempt to sabotage businesses they believe are funded by "Hell-gold" and seek to "liberate" artifacts they think are part of the grand infernal bargain.  
* **Hooks and Relationships:**  
  * **Enemies:** Their primary targets are the powerful **Patriar Families** of the Peerage of Blood and the new **Merchant Consortiums**, who they see as the conspiracy's beneficiaries. They are also deeply suspicious of the **Hellriders of New Elturel's** leadership, believing they are too willing to compromise with the "devils" in the Council of Four.

### **The Cogwork Quack**

* **Name:** Glim-Glim, "The Clear-Eyed"  
* **High Concept:** A gnome tinker and former apprentice from the Lantaner Concession who was exiled for "dangerous and unsubstantiated theories." He is an Oath of Conspiracy paladin who believes the gnomes of Lantan are secretly pacifying the city's populace with microscopic clockwork organisms distributed through the water supply.  
* **The "Truth" (His Conspiracy):** Glim-Glim believes that "smokepowder" is a cover story. The Lantaner Concession's true purpose is a massive social engineering project. The strange whirring and clanking from the Cogwork Quay are not inventions being built, but massive grinders breaking down magical metals into "nano-cogs"—microscopic machines that, when ingested, regulate emotions and make people more docile and suggestible. The recent prosperity and relative social peace are not signs of recovery, but proof of the conspiracy's success. The Lantaners aren't seeking technological supremacy; they're creating the perfect, compliant workforce for their eventual takeover.  
* **Backstory:** As an apprentice, Glim-Glim was tasked with water purification for the Concession. He discovered "anomalous metallic particulates" in the outflow that were not present in the inflow. His master told him it was harmless runoff. But Glim-Glim, a brilliant but paranoid prodigy, built a special lens and saw them: impossibly tiny, intricate gears. When he presented his findings, he was branded a lunatic, stripped of his tools, and cast out. He swore an oath to Gond's "Hidden Cog of Truth" to expose the plot and free the people of Baldur's Gate from their invisible chains.  
* **Organization: The Purified**  
  * **Ideology:** A small, highly secretive, and deeply paranoid group dedicated to resisting the "Cogwork Plague." They believe they are the only ones with the "clarity" to see the truth because they have "purged" the nano-cogs from their systems.  
  * **Membership:** A handful of exiled gnomes, a few crazed alchemists, and some hypochondriac citizens who are convinced they are being controlled.  
  * **Methods:** They subsist on collected rainwater and specially prepared rations. They are obsessed with creating "cog-detectors" from scrap metal and stolen alchemical reagents. Their primary activity is attempting to sabotage the city's water pumps and cisterns, which they see as the distribution network for the "plague."  
* **Hooks and Relationships:**  
  * **Enemies:** Their absolute enemy is the **Lantaner Concession**. They also distrust the **Worshipful Company of Alchemists & Apothecaries**, believing them to be secretly in league with the gnomes, providing the chemical binders for the nano-cogs. They see the **High House of Wonders** (Gond's Temple) as the conspiracy's spiritual center.

### **The Memetic Purist**

* **Name:** Calliope, the Silent Muse  
* **High Concept:** A former playwright and failed artist who now leads a crusade against the city's "cultural contamination." She is an Oath of Conspiracy paladin who believes a coalition of foreign powers is using art, architecture, and stories as "memetic weapons" to erase Baldur's identity and soul.  
* **The "Truth" (Her Conspiracy):** Calliope believes there is a form of magic no one talks about: "memetic warfare." She claims the unique, stubborn, and resilient "spirit" of Baldur's Gate is a kind of psychic entity, and foreign powers (led by Waterdeep and the Harpers) are trying to kill it. The clashing architectural styles aren't just ugly; they're "sigils of chaos" designed to disrupt the city's harmony. The ghostly lament in the Elfsong Tavern isn't a harmless haunting; it's a "sonic plague" instilling subconscious sadness and nostalgia for a time before the city was strong. The extraplanar Circus of the Last Days is a beachhead for literal thought-forms from other realities to invade the populace's dreams. The goal is to make Baldurians forget who they are, making them placid consumers for foreign goods and ideas.  
* **Backstory:** Calliope's magnum opus, "The Ballad of the Blushing Mermaid," a play celebrating the city's roguish, independent spirit, was a colossal flop. It was overshadowed by a traveling troupe from Waterdeep performing elegant, sterile dramas. In her bitterness, Calliope had a revelation: her play didn't fail, it was *suppressed*. She began to see the pattern everywhere—the erosion of "true" Baldurian culture. She swore an oath to preserve the city's soul and now wages a war against the "pretty poisons" of foreign art.  
* **Organization: The Society of Baldurian Integrity**  
  * **Ideology:** A group of cultural zealots who believe they are the last guardians of the "real" Baldur's Gate. They seek to purge the city of all "memetically impure" influences. They have a list of banned songs, architectural features to be vandalized, and artists to be "re-educated."  
  * **Membership:** Disgruntled local artists, fiercely nativist patriars from the Heritage Society, tavern owners who only serve local ale, and young idealists convinced they are fighting for the city's very soul.  
  * **Methods:** They engage in "cultural counter-terrorism." They will heckle bards singing Waterdhavian ballads, splash paint on Sembian-funded buildings, and attempt to exorcise the "foreign ghost" from the Elfsong Tavern. Calliope uses her paladin abilities to make people question the beauty they see, forcing them to perceive the "hidden psychic barbs" in a sculpture or song.  
* **Hooks and Relationships:**  
  * **Enemies:** They despise the **Waterdhavian Kontor**, which they see as the conspiracy's heart. They believe the **Harpers** are the secret agents of this plot. They are also deeply hostile to any non-Baldurian artist, especially the **Circus of the Last Days**.

## The Cults

### **The Gilded Vein**

* **Name:** Valerius Thorne, "the Auditor"  
* **High Concept:** A cult of economic assassins, led by a disgraced former banker from the Amnian Kontor, who seek to "purify" the city's economy through targeted chaos to seize control.  
* **Manifesto:** "Observe this city. It mistakes frantic motion for health. It celebrates the chaotic pulse of supply and demand, of greed and fear. This is not strength; it is a fever. Commerce is a machine, and it has been left in the hands of poets and gamblers. They have introduced the fatal flaw of passion into the equation. We are the auditors of this failed system. We will strip the gears of their sentiment. We will erase the variables of hope and ambition. Through chaos, we will create a perfect, silent, predictable market, and its every tick and tock will be ours."  
* **Backstory:** Valerius Thorne was a rising star in the Gold-Gable Hall, the Amnian Kontor's headquarters. He was exiled after proposing a plan to intentionally bankrupt several Baldurian guilds to consolidate Amn's power—a move deemed too aggressive even for his superiors. Humiliated, Thorne came to believe his colleagues' hesitation was a weakness. He founded the Gilded Vein to pursue his vision of a perfect, predictable market, free from the whims of human emotion.  
* **Organization: The Gilded Vein**  
  * **Ideology:** The Vein believes that the city's current prosperity is a chaotic, sentimental mess. They worship "Axiom," a personified god of pure, logical commerce. To them, competition, booms, and busts are impure variables. Their "truth" is that by surgically removing the most unpredictable and powerful players, they can induce a market crash so severe that people will beg for the stability only the Vein's cold, logical system can provide.  
  * **Membership:** Disenfranchised financial agents, disgraced patriar accountants, and alchemists obsessed with the symbolic power of gold.  
  * **Methods:** The cult uses highly trained assassins and poisoners to eliminate consortium heads, Kontor directors, and Guild moneylenders. They employ alchemists to forge "cursed" currency that spreads misfortune, and they use blackmail and extortion to gain leverage over their targets before they strike.  
* **Hooks and Relationships:**  
  * **Enemies:** Every other financial power is a target, especially their former home, the **Amnian Kontor** , and powerful local blocs like the **Chionthar Consortium**.

### The Plague of Remembrance

* **Name:** Lady Emmeline Vhage  
* **High Concept:** A xenophobic death cult of Baldurian purists, led by a grieving patriar noble, that aims to start a civil war by framing Elturian refugees for a series of atrocities.  
* **Manifesto:** "They call this a city reborn. They are wrong. It is a city infected. We offered shelter to the survivors of a fallen city, and they brought its rot with them. Their despair is a plague, their grief a shadow that chokes our streets. Look at New Elturel—a city of ghosts clinging to our walls. We are told to pity them. I say pity is a poison that weakens the blood of Baldur's Gate. We will not die of pity. We will be the surgeons of our own fate. We will cut out this festering wound and cauterize it with fire, and our city will be pure again."  
* **Backstory:** Lady Vhage’s son was a Flaming Fist soldier killed during the Absolute Crisis, a death she irrationally blames on the chaos caused by the influx of refugees from Elturel. Consumed by grief and hatred, she began gathering other nativist patriars and downtrodden citizens who felt their livelihoods were threatened by the newcomers, twisting their fear into a holy crusade.  
* **Organization: The Plague of Remembrance**  
  * **Ideology:** The Plague believes the soul of Baldur's Gate is being poisoned by the "foreign humors" of the Elturian survivors. They see the refugees not as grateful survivors, but as a "plague of grief" whose despair is a tangible, corrupting force. They believe they must perform a "civic exorcism" by forcing the city to cut out the "diseased" tissue.  
  * **Membership:** A mix of bitter, old-money patriars from the Peerage of Blood, and angry, working-class Baldurians who feel left behind.  
  * **Methods:** Cultists, disguised as Hellriders or desperate refugees from New Elturel, commit heinous murders. Lady Vhage uses her social standing to spread rumors and sow discord, while their pet mages conjure minor plagues that seem to emanate from the refugee camps.  
* **Hooks and Relationships:**  
  * **Enemies:** The **Hellriders of New Elturel** and the entire refugee population are their primary victims. They are also opposed by any faction that seeks stability, such as **The Guild** and the **Flaming Fist**.

### **The Hands of the Absolute**

* **Name:** The Voice in the Choir  
* **High Concept:** A desperate remnant of the Absolute cult, led by a former cult strategist, who seek to birth a new Netherbrain by finding a suitable psychic host.  
* **Manifesto:** "You cling to your individuality as if it were a treasure. But what is it? A cage of bone. A cacophony of fear, desire, and doubt. The Absolute offered the ultimate gift: peace. Unity. An end to the terrible silence of being alone in your own mind. It was not destroyed. It was scattered, a divine song shattered into a million lonely notes. We are the choir. We will find a new Voice, a mind strong enough to hear the melody. We will bring the song together again, and all of you will finally be free."  
* **Backstory:** The Voice was a high-level administrator in the Absolute's cult, managing logistics and communications. She was never implanted with a tadpole, her skills being too valuable to risk. This left her with her free will intact but utterly devoted to the cause. When the Netherbrain fell, she felt a profound loss and now sees it as her sacred duty to reassemble the pieces of her broken god.  
* **Organization: The Hands of the Absolute**  
  * **Ideology:** The Hands do not see the Netherbrain's defeat as a failure, but as a "divine diaspora". Their "truth" is that its consciousness wants to be reborn and that the city's lingering paranoia is its cry for reunion. They see themselves as midwives to a new god.  
  * **Membership:** Un-tadpoled cult survivors and new recruits drawn from the city's psychically scarred and mentally unstable population.  
  * **Methods:** The cult kidnaps individuals rumored to have psionic talent, subjecting them to dangerous "attunement" rituals. They create "resonance chambers" in basements that amplify psychic energy to draw the Echo in, often driving the surrounding neighborhood mad.  
* **Hooks and Relationships:**  
  * **Enemies:** They are hunted by **The Unchained (K'liir)**, the Githyanki faction dedicated to destroying all traces of mind flayers. They also come into conflict with the **Gravekeepers** who see their rituals as a form of necromancy.

### **The Carnival of Whispers**

* **Name:** Mister Smiles  
* **High Concept:** A fey-pact cult built around the Circus of the Last Days, led by its enigmatic and secretly malevolent ringmaster, seeking to turn Baldur's Gate into a permanent domain of chaotic emotion.  
* **Manifesto:** "Ladies and gentlemen, boys and girls\! You live such quiet lives of grey desperation. You toil and you weep and you laugh, but your passions are a sputtering candle. What if I told you the curtain need never fall? What if the show could be your life? We offer a grand liberation, a final encore\! Give us your wonder, your terror, your love, your despair\! Become part of the performance\! Let the city become the stage, and let your lives be our masterpiece\!"  
* **Backstory:** "Mister Smiles" was a human actor who, desperate for fame, wandered into the Feywild and made a pact with a Lord of that realm. He was given the circus and a new, charismatic persona, but the price is that he must constantly provide his patron with novel and powerful emotions, a hunger that has grown with every passing year.  
* **Organization: The Carnival of Whispers**  
  * **Ideology:** Mister Smiles and his inner circle are warlocks who believe that emotions are a tangible resource, the most delicious food for their patron. By erasing the line between performance and reality, they can create an endless feast, elevating themselves to fey nobility.  
  * **Membership:** The core cultists are circus performers who have also entered the pact. They use their acts to subtly influence the crowd's emotions, while their enforcers (disguised as clowns) kidnap citizens who display particularly strong passions.  
  * **Methods:** They are attempting to corrupt the extraplanar magic that powers the Circus of the Last Days, hoping to anchor it permanently to Baldur's Gate and make its emotional aura bleed out and cover the entire city, trapping everyone in a waking dream of chaotic performance.  
* **Hooks and Relationships:**  
  * **Enemies:** They are a subtle threat that few factions understand. The **Harpers** may be the first to notice the unnatural influence. A powerful wizard like those at **Sorcerous Sundries** might detect the dangerous planar tampering.

### **The Takers of the Tithe**

* **Name:** The Collector  
* **High Concept:** An elite death-cult dedicated to the dead god Myrkul, led by a mysterious figure, that stalks and murders the city's most exceptional individuals to fuel their god's potential resurrection.  
* **Manifesto:** "The city teems with life. A chaotic, squirming mass of flesh. But life is not a right; it is a debt owed to death. Most souls are a pittance, a copper coin tossed into a void. But some... some shine. The soul of a hero, a master artisan, a peerless musician. These are the platinum pieces. We are the Takers. We collect the tithe for a king long forgotten. We will re-assemble his treasury, and with a hundred perfect souls, we will buy his return."  
* **Backstory:** The Collector's identity is unknown. They are rumored to be a minor patriar with a family history steeped in necromancy, or perhaps a surviving priest from Myrkul's ancient clergy, one of the Dead Three. They communicate with their followers only through written messages delivered by skeletal messengers, maintaining absolute anonymity.  
* **Organization: The Takers of the Tithe**  
  * **Ideology:** This cult believes that Bhaal's resurrection was a crude affair. They hold that Myrkul, the original Lord of the Dead, was the true master. They believe a soul is like a currency, and by collecting only the purest "soul-coin," they can buy their master's return to power.  
  * **Membership:** The cultists are organized into hunting parties of three: a "Spotter" who identifies targets, a "Reaper" who performs the assassination with a soul-trapping weapon, and a "Keeper" who guards the captured soul gem.  
  * **Methods:** They are methodical and patient, stalking their prey for weeks to ensure a clean kill. Their targets are the city's best and brightest, making each murder a major public event.  
* **Hooks and Relationships:**  
  * **Enemies:** They are a threat to every powerful and skilled individual in the city, making them enemies of the **Masters of the Worshipful Companies** , the leadership of the **Sorcerous Sodality**, and any particularly famous adventurers.

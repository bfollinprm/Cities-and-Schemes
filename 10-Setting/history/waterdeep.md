# History — Waterdeep

A narrative synthesis of how Waterdeep became the city it is in 1502 DR. Discrete dated events are listed in [40-Timeline/history.md](../../40-Timeline/history.md); this file is the connective tissue.

## Founding and the invention of anonymous rule

Waterdeep grew from **Nimoar's Hold**, a military settlement on the cliffs. The town proper is dated to **936 DR**. For nearly a century, power was held by competing warlords. In **1032 DR**, **Raurlor** proclaimed himself emperor; **Ahghairon** killed him and established the **Lords' Rule** — an anonymous council whose identities would be hidden by magical regalia. This is the foundational political act of Waterdeep, and every succeeding governance crisis is an argument about whether Ahghairon was right.

## The Guildmasters' Folly

In **1248 DR**, the Lords formalized the eighty-five **noble houses**, granting them rights to bear arms and maintain private armies — the seed of the modern aristocracy. In **1256 DR**, **Ahghairon died** and the **Guildmasters** seized the city. Their rule was corrupt and disastrous, and they were violently overthrown. The episode is the origin of two features of modern politics: a deep institutional distrust between Lords and Guilds, and the Guilds' permanent suspicion that any concentration of executive power is a prelude to their being purged again.

## The Lords' Rule revived; the Alliance founded

In **1273 DR**, Lords **Baeron and Shilarn Silmaeril** restored the Masked Lords system, which has held since. In **1314 DR**, Waterdeep founded the **Lords' Alliance**, cementing the city as the political center of the Sword Coast North.

## The modern fractures (post-Sundering)

The **Second Sundering** (~1482–1487 DR) reshaped the divine landscape. Mystra withdrew; Helm and other lost gods returned. **Laeral Silverhand**, one of the Seven Sisters and a Chosen of Mystra, lost the bulk of her magical power in this rebalancing — a private wound she has spent two decades concealing.

In ~**1489 DR**, the Masked Lords ousted the corrupt Open Lord **Dagult Neverember** and reluctantly elevated Laeral, hoping her reputation would restore legitimacy. Two years later, in ~**1491 DR**, a faction inside the Masked Lords **murdered nearly half the council** in an attempted coup. The plot was foiled, but the council has not recovered: paranoia about any new concentration of power — including Laeral's — defines its internal politics today.

The **Zhentarim Schism** of ~1491 DR — when a clone of **Manshoon** arrived in the city and split the Black Network — set off the **Shadow War** with the Xanathar Guild that still burns through the Dock and Trades Wards in 1502.

## The 1502 frame

Outwardly the Crown of the North is at its zenith. Inwardly: a paranoid council, a broken chain of command between Open Lord and Blackstaff, an open underworld war, and a desperate Field Ward at the gates. The dossier's framing is that **the next great crisis will be internal, not external**.

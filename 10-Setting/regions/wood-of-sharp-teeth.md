# The Wood of Sharp Teeth

A dark forest east of the Trade Way. Long-sinister, with a folkloric reputation for twisted trees and stagnant swamps. Since the Absolute Crisis a decade ago, the dossier reports it has crossed from "dangerous" to "horror."

For the local edge as Baldur's Gate's hinterland sees it — including the Western Gate's Whisperwood Copse clear-cut — see [30-Places/.../hinterlands/wood-of-sharp-teeth/](../../30-Places/sword-coast/baldurs-gate/hinterlands/wood-of-sharp-teeth/README.md).

## Extent

Large, contiguous, east-of-Trade-Way. Northern edge skirts the Trade Way close enough that travelers see it daily. Southern reaches sprawl toward the lands north of Amn. Eastern depths are rumored, not surveyed — no one with intent to publish has been to the heart in a generation.

## What changed

The dossier's central claim: **the psychic shockwave of the Absolute's defeat at Elturel severed the wood's already-tenuous connection to the natural world.** The result is a forest that no longer behaves like a forest:

- Trees twisted into grotesque, unnatural shapes; branches like grasping claws.
- Air thick with a rot-stench that doesn't match any visible carcass.
- Ground littered with bones of forgotten creatures.

## What lives there now

The dossier collects reports of phenomena that are **psychically inflected**, not merely biologically dangerous:

- **Shambling mounds** of reanimated battlefield dead, not vegetation.
- **Will-o'-wisps** that are psychic echoes of dying screams, not ordinary undead.
- **Displacer beasts** that drive observers mad with a glance — "living nightmares."
- A **hag coven** in the wood's heart.
- A **goblin tribe** psychically warped by the wood's energies.
- An **outlaw band** using the wood as a no-pursuit haven.

The pattern: the wood has become a *generator* of psychic horror, with both the wildlife and the human/humanoid inhabitants showing the same kind of distortion.

## Why it matters at setting scale

- **Evidence of post-Crisis residue.** The Wood of Sharp Teeth is the most extreme observable case of the Crisis leaving a lasting magical wound on a *place*, not just on people. It's a data point about what to expect — at lower intensity — from other places near the Companion's binding.
- **A no-go zone.** The consortiums and guilds avoid it; this makes it useful to anyone who wants not to be tracked. The strategic implication: the wood is a cover-of-last-resort for everything from druids in transit to high-value fugitives to cult leadership.
- **A frontier of investigation.** The dossier doesn't know what's deepest in the wood. Whoever maps it first will know things no one else does.

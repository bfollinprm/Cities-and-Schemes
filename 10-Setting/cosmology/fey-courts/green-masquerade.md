# The Green Masquerade

A clandestine **Waterdhavian salon and secret society** that mediates between the city and the [fey courts](README.md). Founded in the late 1400s DR; by 1502 a full-fledged faction of guildmasters, minor nobles, scribes, and courtiers. By day, ordinary stations; by moonlight, enchanted masks and the city's fey diplomacy.

It lives in Setting (not Factions) because its agency is in **Waterdeep**, not Baldur's Gate — but its three customs apply anywhere the fey reach.

## Mission

Manage dealings between Waterdeep and the fey courts. Where the Harpers safeguard balance and the Zhentarim seek profit, the Masquerade seeks **enchantment**: subtle spread of fey influence, harnessed for the city's benefit.

## Structure

- **Emerald Regent** — the head; always masked behind a green porcelain laughing-fox. Rumor holds the role could be a fey-blooded noble; another (almost certainly false) holds it is **Oberon himself in disguise**.
- **Seasonal Lords and Ladies** — a council of four, each leaning toward one court: *Lady of Spring's Veil* and *Lord of Summer's Blaze* (Seelie); *Lady of the Autumn Masque* (Wild); *Winter Masquer* (Unseelie).
- **Revelers / Masked** — rank and file, organized in **Circles** by borough or niche. The *Circle of the Sylvan Vault* keeps the Ledger; the *Circle of the Green Glade* (North Ward nobles) provides venues.

The faction does **not** pledge fealty to any one court. Keeping all lines of communication open is the whole point.

## Three customs

1. **Enchanted Masks.** Every member bears a fey-crafted personal mask and never reveals their true face at gatherings. Unmasking another is a grave taboo (one violator reportedly turned to a jade statue on the spot). Members address each other by mask-name: *Lord Fox*, *Lady Foxglove*, *Blackfeather*. See [the-masquerade-rule.md](../../../00-Campaign-Frame/the-masquerade-rule.md).
2. **The Ledger of Favors.** A semi-sentient green-leather tome, Sylvan script shimmering on the page, kept by archivists called **Arboreans** (chief: **Aldon Glass**). Records every bargain. Hidden — rumors place it under the Undercliff or in a warded sub-basement of the [House of Wonder](../../../30-Places/sword-coast/waterdeep/sea-ward/house-of-wonder.md).
3. **Favors as currency.** No coin or written contract among members; every aid is a favor owed. *"Better ten small debts than one great one"* — the proverb against becoming any single archfey's thrall. See [fey-bargains.md](../../../00-Campaign-Frame/fey-bargains.md).

## Notable members

- **Lord Fox of Secomber** — Seelie-leaning; brokered the Laughing Hollow pixie envoys.
- **Lady Duskrose** — Unseelie-aligned half-elf enchantress; Winter Court contact.
- **Aldon Glass** — emerald-spectacled scribe, Master of the Ledger.
- **Aurelia "Northwind" Thorne** — recent Reveler, spine-of-the-world traveler; eyewitness to the [Night of the Howling Masks](../../../40-Timeline/history.md).

## Internal divide

Traditionalists want minimum fey influence and maximum secrecy. Progressives (younger members) want to invite *more* fey wonder into the city. The Regent must satisfy both wings, the Seelie heralds, the Unseelie spies, and the Wild Archfey — all at once.

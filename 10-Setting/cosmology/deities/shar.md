# Shar

Goddess of darkness, secrets, and the consoling oblivion of forgetting. Called the **Mistress of the Night** and the **Lady of Loss**. Twin and antithesis of [Selûne](mystra.md). Patron of mortals whose grief or rage has curdled into the wish to *unmake* — to remember nothing, to be nothing, to take the world down with them.

## Why she matters in this campaign

- The [Silent Shroud](../../../20-Factions/silent-shroud/summary.md) is her active cult in Baldur's Gate — a sub-rosa Sharran cell hunting the lost sanctums of the Lady of Loss in the Undercity. Their work is patient and quiet, the way she wants it.
- She is named as a standing target of [Harper](../../../20-Factions/harpers/summary.md) disruption, alongside Bhaalist activity. The two cults' methods differ — Shar's is private and inward; [Bhaal's](bhaal.md) is public and bloody — but the Harpers treat both as the same kind of threat.

## Bargains

Sharran pacts trade memory for power. The bargain looks gentle (forget the loss, forget the shame, forget the face) and ends with the supplicant having forgotten enough of themselves to be *useful* — a hollow that can be filled.

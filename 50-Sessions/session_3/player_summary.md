# Session 3 — Player Summary

## Morning

A meager breakfast — bad bread, salty "river brine" — served as a tactile reminder of what the [blockades](../../20-Factions/free-traders-of-the-outskirts/activity/week_1/blockade-the-basilisk-gate.md) cost ordinary Baldurians. Bror briefed the party on Gareth's house. The party set the [Vex](../../20-Factions/guild/people/vex.md) job aside and went to the [High Rider's Stand](../../30-Places/sword-coast/baldurs-gate/outer-city/new-elturel.md) for the appointed audience.

## The Jaredith mission

[High Watcher Alena](../../20-Factions/hellriders-of-new-elturel/people/high-watcher-alena.md) thanked the party for the medicine corridor — a "political victory." She then explained her real problem: a senior lieutenant, a Tormtar paladin named [Jaredith Heartsong](../../20-Factions/hellriders-of-new-elturel/people/jaredith-heartsong.md), has fallen in with the radical wing of the Phoenix Pits, particularly the same young tiefling named [Mikkel](../../20-Factions/followers-of-the-phoenix/people/mikkel.md) the party had seen at the Weary Plowman, and now wants violence against the Flaming Fist. Alena needs Jaredith brought back without a fight that the Council can use as a pretext to crush the refugees.

Spark agreed. Kaelan asked about money. Alena gave each of them an advance of 100 gold.

Spark also pressed Alena, successfully, to grant the [Followers of the Phoenix](../../20-Factions/followers-of-the-phoenix/summary.md) **official representation in New Elturel's governing council and land for a temple** — a quiet but real political win for the Phoenix faction inside the Hellriders' patch.

## Errinthal

On the way out, the party was intercepted by [Factor Errinthal](../../20-Factions/chionthar-consortium/people/factor-errinthal.md) of the [Chionthar Consortium](../../20-Factions/chionthar-consortium/summary.md), flanked by river-barge-emblem guards. He claimed the medicine the party had "stolen" was a Consortium PR play — a debt now owed in the thousands of gold.

Elia handled it: she pitched Errinthal on rebranding the entire affair as Consortium-directed. Intrigued, Errinthal accepted. The party was now, on paper, in the Consortium's pocket — to be tasked with "service" to work down the debt.

After he left, Bror noticed what Errinthal hadn't: he never mentioned the **second cart**. The propaganda materials are now leverage. The party can play along with the Consortium until they don't want to.

## Survivor Swap

Inflated prices: Spark spent 45 gp on clothes, rations, waterskin, and a dagger; Elia spent 15 gp on a tunic. The session ended with two job offers (Vex's Gareth task; Alena's Jaredith mission) and a piece of dangerous political leverage in the bag.

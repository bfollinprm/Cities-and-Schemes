# Session 8 — GM Summary

Lifted from `.context/converted/dm-notes.md` — Week 2, Session 8.

## Incident 1 — Debriefing the factions

Both [Alena](../../20-Factions/hellriders-of-new-elturel/people/high-watcher-alena.md) and [Vex](../../20-Factions/guild/people/vex.md) are manipulating the party for their own ends.

- **Alena's** objective was to gauge the severity of Zora's threat to her control over [Jaredith](../../20-Factions/hellriders-of-new-elturel/people/jaredith-heartsong.md) and the refugee movement. The party's report confirmed her worst fears. Her panic over the Whispering Stone reveals her understanding that the enemy has espionage capability, not just violence. She agreed to the watchtower because the party correctly framed it as her problem; losing Jaredith would be a catastrophic political blow. She continues to use the party as a deniable surgical instrument.
- **Vex's** objective was to assess progress on her assignments and the party's value as assets. They failed her test by lying about [the Circus job](../../20-Factions/carnival-of-whispers/activity/week_2/a-feast-of-frustration.md). Her flat refusal of aid and exorbitant 6,000 gp price for the spell scroll were calculated to establish dominance, drain resources, and reinforce dependency on the Guild — see [Retaliation on the River](../../20-Factions/guild/activity/week_2/retaliation-on-the-river.md).

Alena leverages moral authority; Vex leverages coin and condescension. The party is caught between two powerful women with competing agendas.

## Incident 2 — Downtime & discovery

- **The Stone.** Bror's failed attunement is a stark warning. The artifact is not a simple comm device — it is a powerful, dangerous font of information that punishes amateur use. The deafening is the consequence.
- **The heist.** Finn's [Emporium break-in](../../20-Factions/party/activity/week_2/burglarize-the-arcane-emporium.md) yields a major asset: a map to the [Velvet Hand](../../20-Factions/zhentarim/activity/week_2/consolidate-the-east-end.md), a direct hook into the escalating Zhentarim–Guild war. Likely commissioned by the Guild (or another Zhentarim rival) for a heist; ended up for sale instead. Either Vex or [Roah Moonglow](../../20-Factions/zhentarim/people/roah-moonglow.md) would pay heavily.

## Incident 3 — The Watchtower Ambush

Plan-at-the-Pits foiled, [Zora](../../20-Factions/plague-of-remembrance/people/zora.md) shifted to elimination: kill the party and Jaredith, posthumously martyr Jaredith for the riot she failed to start. The trap was well-laid: defensible position, pre-positioned thugs to cut escape, devastating opening volley (*Cone of Cold*). The party's victory was costly and turned on Elia's final *Dissonant Whispers*. Zora's escape leaves a key antagonist at large, aware of party capabilities — see [Plague of Remembrance — Silence and Slander](../../20-Factions/plague-of-remembrance/activity/week_2/silence-and-slander.md).

Other consequences:

- Jaredith is "de-radicalized" exactly as the adventure intended; he is now a firm, grateful ally and a bridge into the Hellriders.
- Alena saw the threat firsthand and was nearly killed. She will harden and lean on the party even more as deniable agents.
- Spark's counter-espionage with the Whispering Stone was clever but rolled low — the listener is likely not fooled. The cult now knows the party has the stone and is trying to use it against them. The artifact's ownership is now contested.

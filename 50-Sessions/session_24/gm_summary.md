# Session 24 — GM Summary

## Setting

The High Rider's Stand pavilion as town hall and militia base. Tactical maps show the shantytown divided into sectors with markers for [Flaming Fist](../../20-Factions/flaming-fist/summary.md) and [Guild](../../20-Factions/guild/summary.md) activity. A small altar to Torm provides the Hellriders with a sense of "holy warrior" identity to combat the despair of their displacement.

## Primary factions

- **[Hellriders of New Elturel](../../20-Factions/hellriders-of-new-elturel/summary.md).** Maintaining order while [Alena](../../20-Factions/hellriders-of-new-elturel/people/high-watcher-alena.md) is at the Basilisk Gate.
- **[Takers of the Tithe](../../20-Factions/takers-of-the-tithe/summary.md).** Organized into three-person units (Spotter, Reaper, Keeper). Murder framed as "debt collection" for Bhaal, aiming to start a civil war by framing refugees for high-profile assassinations.
- **[The Unveiled](../../20-Factions/unveiled/summary.md).** Referenced via Eric's situation and the general paranoia about political framing.

## Party actions

The party shifted from tactical combat to advanced psychological warfare. The "unkillable" ruse specifically targeted the Reaper's theological fear: that by living eternally, he could never pay his "debt" to Bhaal through martyrdom.

## Antagonist objectives

- **The Reaper:** initially to protect cult secrets through fanaticism; transitioned to survival/despair before being silenced by poison.
- **The Bishop ("Singa" tier):** receives target names via divine revelation from Bhaal.
- **[Singa (cell leader)](../../20-Factions/takers-of-the-tithe/people/singa.md):** maintains a high-success rate for high-profile hits.

## Key motivations & strategy

- **The silencing.** The poisoning of the Reaper suggests the cult (or a political benefactor) has high-level infiltration within the Hellrider camp's logistics — a mole likely in a low-visibility but high-access position (cooks, quartermasters, logistics officers). Motivation is fear of the cult or Zhentarim gold.
- **Refugee framing.** The cult's broader mission is to cast hatred on the Elturian refugees to provoke Baldurians into open conflict — this aligns with [the Plague of Remembrance's false-flag attack on the Sow & Piglet that same week](../../20-Factions/plague-of-remembrance/activity/week_6/the-false-flag.md).

## Other considerations

- **[Eric the patriarch](../../20-Factions/peerage-of-blood/people/eric.md).** Provides a potential "in" to the Peerage of Blood or a hook into legal corruption of city elites. His independent reconstruction of the Zhentarim sewer-tax-records angle suggests he may be a useful future asset.
- **[Kaelan's article](../../20-Factions/baldurs-mouth/activity/week_6/the-siege-edition.md).** The whole city is in a state of riot, gates closed, due to the fallout of the insurance fraud / refugee investigation.
- **Jaredith's pacifism.** While Jaredith assisted in the "execution" for the ruse, his hippie outlook may conflict with future violent party goals — flag for future tension.

# Session 25 — GM Summary

## Incident 1: The Silencing & The Mole

- **Location:** High Rider's Stand prison tent.
- **Primary factions:** [Plague of Remembrance](../../20-Factions/plague-of-remembrance/summary.md) (Singa's cell, *cf. dm-notes terminology* — see note below) vs. [Hellriders](../../20-Factions/hellriders-of-new-elturel/summary.md).
- **Compromised supply chain.** The Reaper didn't take a suicide pill; he died after eating the prison rations. The cult has infiltrated the Hellriders' logistics. There is a **mole** within the High Rider's Stand capable of poisoning specific food bowls or the general supply. Critical thread for the party to pull on later.
- **Fanaticism vs. pragmatism.** The cult employs two tiers of agents. "Reapers" are psychologically broken zealots (likely subjected to *Tithe Ledger* conditioning); "Seekers/Spotters" are paid mercenaries. The assassination of the Reaper was meant to silence a loose end, but it inadvertently broke the Spotter's loyalty — fear is a brittle leash for hirelings.
- **Continuity note.** The `dm-notes.docx` source uses "Plague of Remembrance" and "Takers of the Tithe" interchangeably for Singa's cell. Player-facing canon has settled on **[Takers of the Tithe](../../20-Factions/takers-of-the-tithe/summary.md)** (per `player-companion.docx`). The Plague of Remembrance overlap may be intentional (cult-of-the-Dead-Three triangulation) or source noise.

## Incident 2: Eric & the Geometry of Greed

- **Location:** High Rider's Stand side-tent.
- **Eric's deductions** ([page](../../20-Factions/peerage-of-blood/people/eric.md)) directly correlate with two ongoing operations:
  - The Bhaalist soul-contract framework — Singa's *foreclosing* of souls — anticipates the **Four Cairns** the party will encounter that same day.
  - The Zhentarim sewer-tax-records operation directly maps to [Roah Moonglow's *Ledger Logic*](../../20-Factions/zhentarim/activity/week_6/the-ledger-logic.md) hitting Guild-aligned businesses this week.
- **Future hook.** Eric is a "Get out of Jail Free" card for Upper City access later — but only if the party can prove his brothers framed him.

## Incident 3: The Four Cairns (the Broker's Toll)

- **Location:** North face of Dusthawk Hill.
- **The Beetle entity.** [The Skittering Beetle](../../20-Factions/takers-of-the-tithe/people/the-skittering-beetle.md) acts as a **Soul Broker** (likely a Devil bound to Bhaal by the Takers' working). Transactional rather than malicious — does not kill what it can extract from.
- **Cairn doctrine.** The cult does not view murder as chaotic; they view it as a transaction. The Cairns punish specific theological crimes:
  - **Kaelan / Debt of Respite.** Punished for *Revivify*. *Malaise* (minimized healing) directly counters his domain, forcing the party to rely on resources they don't have.
  - **Elia / Debt of Authorship.** Punished for forging the ledger. *Fractured ID* (No Reactions) silences her tactical interjections (Counterspell, Cutting Words).
  - **Spark / Debt of Entropy.** Spark accepted the accusation; took *1 Exhaustion*.
  - **Bror / Debt of Silence.** The Beetle successfully purchased Bror's voice. Strategically cripples party communication; narratively the Beetle now has "a voice" it can use later (mimicry, spell components).
- **Consequence.** The party enters the Counting House mechanically crippled. Deliberate attrition strategy by Singa.

## Incident 4: The Summit & The Civil War

- **Location:** The Counting House (Dusthawk Hill summit).
- **Singa's grand strategy.** Kill Alfira (a beloved refugee figure) in a way that implicates the Council or the Flaming Fist, sparking a riot that destroys the city from within. The hideout is styled as a counting house with desks and ledgers, not a temple — Singa sees herself as an **Auditor of Death**.
- **The bait.** Leaving Alfira alive (for now) forces the party to act rashly. Singa wants them to charge in suffering from *Malaise* and *Exhaustion*.
- **The "initiative."** Singa's eventual line will imply she was *waiting* for them. The abduction was a trap to draw the "Collectors" (the party) into a kill box.

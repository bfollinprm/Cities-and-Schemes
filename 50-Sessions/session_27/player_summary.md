# Session 27 — Player Summary

## The road back

We walk Alfira down from Dusthawk Hill at dawn. The slate path is easier in the right direction. She is unsteady but lucid. Kaelan and Bror talk theology — the Cult of the Dead Three, the way Bhaal/Myrkul/Bane fragmentation is the city's protection. Bror writes on a slate (his voice is back, but writing is faster than talking when you've spent a night in an obsidian pit): *the god of death noticed Spark's age. are you Karlach?* Spark scoffs. Alfira does not scoff. Alfira says nothing for a long minute.

Bror also confirms that his **Reading Sphere of Flame Soul** still resonates magically, even at distance — its enchantment is self-contained.

## The Plowman

We bring Alfira to [the Weary Plowman](../../30-Places/sword-coast/baldurs-gate/outer-city/rivington.md) in [Rivington](../../30-Places/sword-coast/baldurs-gate/outer-city/rivington.md), not the High Rider's Stand. She is exhausted; she should not return to her flock yet. She agrees. *"I owe you a great debt,"* she tells the party. Lena Oakes pours tea, takes one look at Alfira's color, and herds her to the cleanest back room.

## Jaredith departs

Outside in the courtyard, Jaredith Heartsong sets his lute against the wall and tells the party he is staying. Someone needs to lead the Phoenix Temple construction in Alfira's absence; he thinks that someone is him. He embraces Spark, kisses Bror on the forehead, and walks back toward [New Elturel](../../30-Places/sword-coast/baldurs-gate/outer-city/new-elturel.md). The party's legacy in New Elturel just changed: not mercenaries any more, *builders*.

## Splitting the web

Inside the Plowman, the party splits to chase parallel leads.

**Bror, Elia, and Finn** ride for the Penumbra Playhouse in [Twin Songs](../../30-Places/sword-coast/baldurs-gate/lower-city/heapside.md). Bror has been carrying [Mother Nocturne's secret-debt](../../20-Factions/silent-shroud/people/mother-nocturne.md) since week 2. Time to deliver. Inside the candle-shadow of the Playhouse cellar, Mother Nocturne hears the secret out — and asks for *more*. She wants the **physical manifestation** of the secret. *The sentient source.* The Whispering Stone hums in Bror's pocket. *Until Bror produces the source, the debt remains unpaid.* The Night Mother is patient.

**Kaelan** crosses the wall into [Heapside](../../30-Places/sword-coast/baldurs-gate/lower-city/heapside.md) to bring Ettvard Needle the Singa exposé. The Baldur's Mouth print shop is **ransacked**. Glass on the floor; ink across the press; one tipped-over chair. A hidden note in the press's lower drawer, in Ettvard's hand:

> *If they take me, I'm in the crypts under Tumbledown. West bank. Dusthawk Hill. Don't trust the next byline.*

Tumbledown is on the same hill we just left burning at the top. **Ettvard Needle is missing**, hidden, possibly being hunted, possibly bargaining for his life with whatever is now under the Hill.

## Where week 6 ends

Two open threads. Bror owes the Night Mother a sentient secret-source. Ettvard Needle is in the Tumbledown crypts. The Reverse Siege is on hold; Alfira is sleeping at the Plowman; Jaredith is laying stone at the Phoenix Gate. The next *Baldur's Mouth* edition will not be Ettvard's voice. The Archduke is winning a press war in his absence.

We need a new week.

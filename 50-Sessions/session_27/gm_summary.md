# Session 27 — GM Summary

## Incident 1: The Aftermath of Dusthawk Hill

- **Location:** The Counting House (Bhaalist sanctum), Dusthawk Hill.
- **Primary factions:** [Plague of Remembrance / Bhaalists](../../20-Factions/plague-of-remembrance/summary.md), [Followers of the Phoenix](../../20-Factions/followers-of-the-phoenix/summary.md).
- **Spotter aftermath.** Survival; having watched the party defeat his masters and *not die* as expected, he is utterly terrified and confused by their resilience.
- **Cult of the Dead Three.** The fractionalism between Bhaal, Myrkul, and Bane is their greatest weakness. By revealing that their cooperation 15 years ago (the Absolute Crisis) nearly destroyed the city, the NPCs are framed as a catastrophic threat that even non-evil factions (the Sharrans, the Lathanderites) would want to suppress to maintain status quo.
- **Spark's "immortality" rumors.** Bror's interaction plants a seed for the Followers of the Phoenix to potentially view Spark as a holy figure — could complicate the party's desire to remain independent of religious zealotry.

## Incident 2: The Respite of the Ember

- **Location:** Outskirts of New Elturel / the Weary Plowman.
- **Alfira's pivot.** From figurehead of a protest to community builder. Her focus on "planting roots" through the temple construction is a political statement of permanence in Baldur's Gate. Her willingness to trust the party with her location at the Plowman shows the party has moved from "contractors" to "confidants."
- **Jaredith's exit.** Jaredith leading the temple construction provides a narrative reason for his departure, cementing the party's legacy as **builders rather than mercenaries**.

## Incident 3: Splitting the Web

- **Location:** Rivington and Twin Songs.
- **Primary factions:** [Baldur's Mouth](../../20-Factions/baldurs-mouth/summary.md), [Cult of Shar / Silent Shroud](../../20-Factions/silent-shroud/summary.md).
- **The Night Mother's ask.** [Mother Nocturne](../../20-Factions/silent-shroud/people/mother-nocturne.md) wants a physical manifestation of the secret — *the sentient source*. She suspects it may be Elia or Finn. *Until Bror delivers, the debt is unpaid.*
- **Kaelan and Ettvard.** Information brokerage. Ettvard wants the "scoop" on the cult to sell papers and potentially manipulate the Council of Four — which is no longer possible, because the cult has moved on him first.
- **Strategic pivot.** By reaching out to both the press and a secret society, the players are attempting to fight the Dead Three on two fronts: the court of public opinion and the shadow war. The Night Mother sits as a "middle ground" ally — more agile than the Lathanderites, less overtly chaotic than the Bhaalists.

## Incident 4: The Silencing of the Mouth

- **Location:** Baldur's Mouth print shop, [Lower City](../../30-Places/sword-coast/baldurs-gate/lower-city/heapside.md).
- **Primary factions:** [Baldur's Mouth](../../20-Factions/baldurs-mouth/summary.md), [Western Gate Trading Company / the Consortium](../../20-Factions/western-gate-trading-company/summary.md).
- **Antagonist objective.** Archduke Lyra Silvershield aims to suppress dissent. Her [Starvation Siege](../../20-Factions/western-gate-trading-company/activity/week_6/the-starvation-siege.md) depends on the public believing the Outer City's plight is exaggerated or the refugees' own fault.
- **Strategy: Narrative Correction.** Removing Needle (likely via kidnapping or forced "retirement") and installing a pro-consortium editor lets the city's most trusted news source issue retractions, effectively gaslighting the populace about the insurance fraud Kaelan helped uncover.
- **Ticking clock.** The party has approximately one week (narrative time) before the paper's tone shifts permanently. **Ticking-clock side-quest: Save the Press.**

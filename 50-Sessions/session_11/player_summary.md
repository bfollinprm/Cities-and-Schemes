# Session 11 — Player Summary

## The Feywild glade

The session opens with the party scattered across a strange perpetually-twilit glade just past [the bone-white wood portal](../../20-Factions/party/activity/week_3/force-the-feywild-portal.md). **Time runs sideways here.** Finn, who came through alone before the rest, has been waiting *over a week* in his subjective experience. Bror, who came through next, waited only minutes. Elia, Spark, and Kaelan arrive last after a few subjective minutes. Once everyone is reassembled it has been *hours* on Bror's clock and *eight days plus* on Finn's.

The party regroups. Bror reconnoiters: [Lyra Sunstone](../../20-Factions/hellriders-of-new-elturel/people/lyra-sunstone.md) and the other harvested patrons are inside a marvelous, painted wagon marked *The Ephemera Collection,* together with [Lucretius](../../20-Factions/carnival-of-whispers/people/lucretius.md). The party decides to confront him directly.

## *The Ephemera Collection*

The wagon's shelves are lined with jars holding captured memories — labeled in a script the party half-understands. A ledger shows what Lyra traded: *"Memories of Elturel's Fall" → "The Strength to Prevent Another."* She is nearby, performing humble chores with superhuman strength, but devoid of personality. She does not remember her life as a Hellrider.

Lucretius proposes a fey bargain. Three riddles. Win all three, take Lyra's memories back. **Win or lose, he will activate Elia's shard** — but if she fails the riddles, he gets to listen in on the unlocking. The party agrees.

- **Riddle 1.** Group answer: *a mirror.* Correct.
- **Riddle 2.** Finn: *a promise.* Correct.
- **Riddle 3.** Finn: *an answer.* Correct.

Victorious, the party receives Lyra's memories in a glass jar. They return them to her. She collapses, sobbing, as the trauma of her city's fall floods back — then rises with her purpose restored and thanks the party profusely. She is whole again.

## The Shard of the First Scribe

True to the bargain, Lucretius activates Elia's obsidian shard. Bror chooses to access the memory with her. Both are flooded with a cosmic vision: a psychic ocean of mortal souls being caged and channeled by divine wills — the act of "**naming**." A vast consciousness severs a piece of itself rather than submit to being named, fleeing categorization into silence. **Elia's shard is what was left** — not a stone but a scream frozen into silence: the rejected fragment of a forgotten **God of Naming**.

The shard reshapes in Elia's hand into a new focus: the **Shard of the First Scribe**. Elia is no longer a cultist of a Great Old One; she is the bearer of a forgotten god-fragment that itself rejected divinity. Bror, sharing the vision, glimpses something about his Sharran patron's relationship to "naming" he was not meant to see. Lucretius takes neither memory; the bargain was a fair game.

## Returning home

The party departs the Feywild and returns to [Rivington](../../30-Places/sword-coast/baldurs-gate/outer-city/rivington.md). They discover **two weeks have passed on the Material Plane** while they were gone. The Flaming Fist warrant for their arrest has lost its urgency; the city has moved on. They return to the [Weary Plowman](../../30-Places/sword-coast/baldurs-gate/outer-city/rivington.md), where Lena charges them 50 gp for keeping their rooms; Spark pays without complaint.

While the party takes a short rest, Bror tries the Whispering Stone again. He fails the Wisdom save, takes psychic damage, and is deafened a second time. The stone is not his yet.

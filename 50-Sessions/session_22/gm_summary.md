# Session 22 — GM Summary

## Incident 1: The Information War

- **Location:** Rivington (party safehouse).
- **Primary factions:** [Baldur's Mouth](../../20-Factions/baldurs-mouth/summary.md), [Chionthar Consortium](../../20-Factions/chionthar-consortium/summary.md), [Sembian Kontor](../../20-Factions/sembian-kontor/summary.md).
- **Party actions:** Reviewed the broadsheet. [Kaelan published the *Insurance Scam* hit-piece](../../20-Factions/baldurs-mouth/activity/week_6/the-siege-edition.md) detailing insurance fraud and smokepowder smuggling.
- **Strategic effect:** Kaelan's article triggers a slow-burn legal-and-financial crisis for the Consortium and Sembian Kontor at the exact moment the Archduke needs both factions politically loyal. The Mouth is acting as a destabilizing third front against the Council without committing the press to either war faction.
- **Glim-Glim and the Clockwork Key.** The mention of Glim-Glim's Cogwork Detector and the Lantaner "construct" suggests the Concession is nearing completion of a super-weapon prototype. Lantaner timing matters: a finished prototype before the siege escalates changes the strategic calculus on both sides.

## Incident 2: The Reverse Siege Strategy

- **Location:** Rivington / New Elturel.
- **Primary factions:** Party, [Followers of the Phoenix](../../20-Factions/followers-of-the-phoenix/summary.md).
- **Strategic effect:** Spark's pivot moves the party from "victims of a siege" to "holders of resources." It aligns with [Mad Meggan's existing rationing operation](../../20-Factions/free-traders-of-the-outskirts/activity/week_6/rationing-the-hope.md) and turns a defensive desperate stand into an offensive starvation tactic against the Council. The Ember's flock is the missing labor pool.

## Incident 3: Recruiting the Ember & the Captured Reaper

- **Location:** New Elturel temple construction site.
- **The Ember's read:** Alfira is using soft power — narrative and faith — rather than military might. Hesitant to commit her flock to a maneuver that could lead to a massacre. She is paralyzed by the threat of assassination; her primary motivation has shifted from spiritual guidance to immediate survival.
- **The captured Reaper:** likely a Bhaalist agent from [the Takers of the Tithe](../../20-Factions/takers-of-the-tithe/summary.md). His capture is a ticking clock — either his masters come for him, or he escapes. (In fact, [his masters poison him in his cell that very week](../../20-Factions/takers-of-the-tithe/activity/week_6/the-prison-silence.md).)

## Assessment: Volatility of New Elturel

- **Status:** Critical / incendiary.
- **Analysis:** The Followers of the Phoenix are no longer a passive refugee population. The crowd revealed they are "literally ready to start fires."
  - **Radicalization:** Food shortages and Sembian attacks have stripped away their patience. They are actively looking for an excuse to lash out.
  - **Trigger warning:** If the Ember gives the order, or if she is martyred, this faction will almost certainly riot — likely burning the checkpoints at the Basilisk Gate or Wyrm's Rock. The party's plan to use them as a blockade-reinforcement risks turning them into an uncontrollable mob.
